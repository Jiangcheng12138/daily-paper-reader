Abstract 32 

The naked mole rat is an evolutionary outlier among mammals, exhibiting extreme longevity, cancer 33 

resistance, hypoxia tolerance, pain insensitivity, eusociality, poikilothermy and other distinctive 34 

physiological traits , most of which likely resulted from its adaptation to highly adverse subterranean 35 

habitat . Despite accumulating data , the genetic and molecular basis underlying these traits remain 36 

poorly understood. Through analyses of 18 distinct protein attributes and allied datasets across 37 

hystricomorphs, myomorphs, carnivores, and primates, we observed lineage -specific evolutionary 38 

divergence in intrinsic protein disorder in the naked mole rat. The disorder turnover exhibited 39 

functional dichotomy . T he gain of disorder preferentially associated with prote ostasis , immune 40 

regulation , neurodevelopment , skeletal growth and tumour suppressive properties , while loss of 41 

disorder modulated mostly the cardiac development . The proteins that gained disorder in NMR 42 

exhibited lower degradation rates , consistent with stabilization through phase -separation, while the 43 

proteins losing disorder show pronounced divergence in gene expression. The disorder turnover 44 

was primarily driven by indels affecting functional regions including Pfam domains, ANCHOR -45 

predicted binding sites , short linear motifs, stress induced modifications of Tyr, Met, and Cys 46 

residues . Notably , the gained disordered regions were inferred to be redox -sensitiv e, aligning to 47 

exceptional stress tolerance in naked mole rats . Collectively, our results highlight an unusual and 48 

previously overlooked large -scale proteome remodelling that drives the molecular evolution of 49 

ex traordinary traits of naked mole rat. 50

Introduction 68 

Naked mole rats (NMR) are extremophilic rodents that have evolved under hostile environmental 69 

pressures, most notably chronic hypoxia and hypercapnia in subterranean habitat 1–3. To survive the 70 

strenuous conditions , NMR has developed a suit of extra -ordinary traits , including persistent 71 

poikilothermy 4, slow metabolic rate 5–7, enhanced resistance to oxidative stress 8–10 , elastic and 72 

wrinkled skin 11 , and dampened inflammatory response 12 . These adaptations may serve as foundation 73 

for species’ extreme longevity 13 . Though of similar size as mouse, which lives for 2 -3 years, NMR 74 

can live up to 30 years 14,15 . Coherently, the species is resistant to age related disorders like cancer, 75 

cardiovascular abnormalities, immune senescence, neurodegeneration, and musculoskeletal loss 11,16 .76 

Mechanisms such as early contact inhibition through high molecular weight hyaluronan, efficient 77 

DNA repair and dampened inflammatory response not only resist cancer, but also preserve cellular 78 

integrity over time 11 17 . This is further complemented by their stable proteome and epigenome 18 19 .79 

Alongside Damara Mole Rat (DMR) , they are the only known eusocial mammals, living in highly 80 

organized colony of around 70 individuals with a single breeding queen 20 . Despite gaining widespread 81 

interest, the molecular basis of NMR’s unique traits is not thoroughly understood. 82 

83 

Proteome evolution through sequence divergence of proteins has been widely studied 21 . However, the 84 

role of other attribute s of proteins in the evolution of lineage -specific traits remain s underexplored 85 

and underappreciated . Proteome remodel ling through g ain, loss, duplication and fusion of functional 86 

protein domains have been widely reported 22 –33 . Variation in DNA binding potential has been 87 

observed between orth ologous transcription factor s 34 . Harris et al and Ramakrishnan et al reported 88 

widespread conservation as well as certain lineage specific variation s in RNA binding potential of 89 

proteins 35,36 . A well -studied structural attribute, the intrinsic disorder, is present in abundance in the 90 

hubs of protein -protein interaction networks, epigenetic and transcri ptional regulators, signalling 91 

proteins , post -translational modification sites , and alternatively spliced exons 37 –45 . The intrinsically 92 

disordered regions (IDRs) likely function via couple folding and binding mediated through certain 93 

ANCHORs that have increased tendency to fold upon interaction 46 –50 Certain IDRs with suitable 94 

pattern of stickers and spacers implicate in phase separation 51 . A prominent example is that of 95 

Heter ochromatin Protein -1 (HP1), which forms heter ochr omatin compartments upon phase separation 96 

mediated by an IDR 52 . IDR s are evolutionarily labile 53 –55 and , therefore, can serve as hotbeds for the 97 

evolutionary emergence of novel traits. 98 

99 

Despite the wealth of literature on protein intrinsic disorder and the related protein attributes, their 100 

implication in the evolution of lineage -specific traits, particularly the extreme ones, has been 101 

overlooked. We performed an unbiased study of 18 well curated protein attributes across n 102 

orthologous proteins of n mammals. The analyses revealed a high turnover of structural disorder, and 103 4

concomitantly the order, in naked mole rat specifically. Gain and loss of disorder showed stark 104 

functional di stinctions . W here as the former wa s associated with immune regulation, 105 

neurodevelopment, bone growth, apoptosis and tumour suppression , and may function through 106 

stabilization of proteins via phase separation , the lat ter implicated in organogenesis, in particular the 107 

heart development and likely function through expression divergence. The disorder turnover was 108 

clearly linked to indels, affecting functional sites , domains and redox -sensitivity of proteins , aligning 109 

with the stress tolerant physiology of naked mole rat. 110

set (Figure 1D) . Gain of disorder in negative loading set and loss of disorder in positive loading set 136 

was further illustrated by plotting distribution of VSL2 disorder scores of proteins across species for 137 

each set (Figure 1E) . The multivariate analysis, thus, highlighted a significant evolutionary turnover 138 

of intrinsic disorder specifically in naked mole rat. 139 5

Functional dichotomy of disorder turnover 141 

Functional enrichment analyses highlighted overrepresentation of proteasomal regulation, immune 142 

regulation, neurodevelopment, bone growth, apoptosis, and phosphoinositol related terms in protein 143 

set that exhibited gain of disorder (GoD) (Figure 2A) . These terms are broadly associated with the 144 

distinctive phenotype s of NM R, like longevity , cancer resistance, sustained immune competence, 145 

enhanced stress tolerance, skeletal robustness, and neural maintenance throughout lifespan. In 146 

contrast, proteins displaying loss of disorder (LoD) had significant enrichment of h eart , mesoderm 147 

and embryo nic development related terms (Figure 2B), tightly aligning with the unique 148 

cardiometabolic and cardio -myofilament profile of naked mole rat 56 –58 .149 

150 

Phenotype enrichment analysis complemented these observations and also reve aled additional terms 151 

like abnormal erythrocyte magnesium and potassium levels in GoD set, which may relate to unique 152 

ionic homeostasis, hypoxia tolerance, and metabolic resilience observed in NMRs (Figure 2C) .153 

Conversely, LoD associated phenotypes included trophoblast/placenta development, organogenesis 154 

(liver, spleen, thymus) , locomot ion related terms (Figure 2D) , reflecting the NMR’s highly 155 

constrained developmental programs, organ maturation, and coordinated motor function necessary for 156 

subterranean burrow navigation and eusocial colony living 18,59 –65 .157 

158 

These observations impl ied functional dichotomy of disorder turnover in NMR. Broadly, the g ain of 159 

disorder preferentially associate with adaptive , plastic and environmentally responsive trait s, while 160 

loss of disorder likely safeguards the ordered and constrained developmental process. 161 

162 

Biophysical consequences of disorder turnover 163 

To assess how the disorder turnover may have globally impacted the biophy sical properties of the 164 

proteome and consequently the evolutionary adaptations in NMR, we studied the phase separation 165 

potential and degradation rates of proteins in each set. Gain of disorder was associated with greater 166 

phase separation potential and lower degradation rates (Figure 3A -B) . By contrast, proteins losing 167 

disorder (LoD) showed lesser phase separation but did not exhibit alteration in degradation rates 168 

(Figure 3A -B) . These observations imply that the gain of disorder in NMR may have impacted the 169 

functional landscape in NMR by influencing the protein stability and functional compartmentalization 170 

through phase separation , likely supporting the species’ unique cellular and physiological adaptations .171 

172 

Gain of disorder associates with anti -tumorigenic properties 173 6

Naked mole rat exhibit s remarkably lower incidence of cancer 11 . To test the cancer properties of 174 

protein s in the GoD and the LoD sets, we performed the gene set variation (GSVA) and copy number 175 

variation (CNV) analysis of proteins across cancer and normal s am ples from TCGA . The GoD set 176 

exhibited significantly lower GSVA scores and were often deleted in cancer patients (Figure 4A) .177 

LoD set shows no such pattern (Figure 4A) . Similarly, there was a greater risk to death, as marked by 178 

negative hazard ratios for the lower expression levels of proteins in GoD set (Figure 4B) . These 179 

observations together impl ied anti -tumorigenic roles of proteins that GoD set. Coherently, GoD set 180 

also exhibited enrichment of functional terms like regulation of apoptosis and phosphoinositol 181 

regulation , which are often associated with tumour suppression 66,67 . Amon g others, we observed the 182 

well -known anti -tumorigenic protein s like RAD51C ( a DNA repair protein), NQO2 ( a stress inducible 183 

protector of P53, P63, P33 against degradation), Lumican (a proteoglycan having fas -specific pro -184 

apoptotic role) , etc . gained intrinsic disorder in NMR 68 –74 . Conversely, the proteins that had lost 185 

disorder in NMR did not exhibit such properties (Figure 4) . Collectively, these results indicate that 186 

gain of disorder in NMR selectively targets proteins involved in cancer suppression , complementing 187 

the species’ extraordinary longevity, cancer re si stance, and stress tolerance, while loss of disorder 188 

preserves core developmental and physiological programs .189

Loss of disorder coincides with the expression divergence 191 

In tri nsically disordered proteins are tightly regulated at transcriptional and translational level. For 192 

example, IDPs have lower transcriptional and translational rates and are degraded rapidly when 193 

compared with the ordered protein s, implying that the ectopic levels of IDPs may promiscuously 194 

interact with other proteins and cause toxicity 75,76 . We, therefore, tested the evolutionary divergence in 195 

gene expression of proteins showing disorder turnover in NMR. We calculated expression rates in 196 

NMR from guinea pig while taking mouse as an outgroup. Protein s in th e LoD set show ed significant 197 

alteration in expression rates , while the ones in GoD set did not, implying that loss of disorder may 198 

allow expression to diverge owing to lower possibilities of promiscuous interactions (Figure 5) .199 

Expression divergence of proteins gaining disorder, on the contrary, may lead to abrupt and undesired 200 

interactions and, th us , are selected against . The proteins involved in heart development, there fore, lo st 201 

disorder likely to allow for adaptive expression divergence and tighter regulation in NMR. 202 

203 

Disorder turnover is driven by indels affecting functional sites of proteins 204 

To understand the underlying mechanisms driving the disorder turnover in NMR, we performed the 205 

multiple sequences alignment of each protein’s ortholog in all the species . By parsing the alignments, 206 

we identified the indels (Methods). We first show that indels are the major source of disorder 207 

turnover in NMR (F igure 6A -B). Disorder is gained either through insertions of di so rdered regions or 208 

deletion of ordered regions or both (Figure 6A -B) . C on versely, diso rder is lost through deletions of 209 7

disordered regions and insertion or duplications of ordered regions (Figure 6A -B) . We then tested the 210 

representation of functional features like ANCHORs, Pfam domains, short linear motifs ( SLiMs ), and 211 

post -translational modification sites (PTMs) in indels (Methods) . The disordered indels (i.e., 212 

insertions in GoD set and deletions in LoD set) impacted the gain and loss of ANCHOR predicted 213 

bind ing sites and SLiM motifs, while ordered indels drove the dynamics of Pfam domains (Figure 214 

6C). PTM sites show lesser such distinction (Figure 6C) .215

We illustrate d several examples of gain and loss of disorder in NMR (Figure 7) . The protein FKBP7, 217 

FKB10, CAHD1, JAZF1, FBLN5, and TMM3 have gained disorder in NMR, while KLF4, HAND1, 218 

3BP2, HABP4, and TCF20 have lost disorder. FKBP7 and FKBP10 are peptidyl -prolyl cis/trans 219 

isomerases (PPIases), known chaperones with multiple functions including protein folding, 220 

immune regulation, endoplasmic reticulum stress response, and modulation of signaling 221 

pathways 77 –79 . FKBP7 had an insertion of a long intrinsically disorder region, while FKB10 222 

had a small deletion that induced structural disorder in the flanking regions in NMR. Gain of 223 

disorder in PPIases may modulate conformational flexibility and interaction networks of 224 

these chaperones, potentially contributing to enhanced proteostasis and cellular stress 225 

resistance in NMR . CAHD1 is an auxiliary regulator of voltage -gated calcium channels that 226 

modulates calcium influx and neuronal excitability 80 –82 . The gain of intrinsic disorder in 227 

CAHD1 may enhance interaction flexibility, potentially contributing to reduced neuronal 228 

excitability and resistance to excitotoxic stress in subterranean habitat of naked mole rat. 229 

JAZF1 is a key transcriptional repressor in response to metabolic stress 83,84 . It is genetically associated 230 

with the diabetes and its deletion causes weight gain and insulin resistance in mice 85 –87 . We show that 231 

one of the three zinc fingers is deleted in naked mole rat, which may impact its target selection and 232 

binding affinity, likely contributing to the higher insulin sensitivity, low metabolic rates, and 233 

consequently the aging in NMR. FBLN5(Fibulin -5) is a glycoprotein that engages in the formation of 234 

elastic matrix across several tissues, by interacting with other matrix components like fibrillin -1, 235 

tropoelastin , proteoglycan and lysyl oxidases 88 –91 . It mitigates the IL -1β induced inflammation of 236 

chondrocytes 88 . Fibulin -5 is genetically associated with a wrinkled skin condition ‘Cutis Laxa’ and 237 

aging related elastosis in humans and mice 92 –96 . The C -terminal Fibulin domain is important in 238 

cellular secretion as well matrix interactions 90 . Interestingly, fibulin domain is partially deleted in 239 

FBLN5 of NMR. This may have implication in remodeling of matrix properties in NMR. In 240 

particular, it may make the matrix more compliant with the structural properties ascribed by high 241 

molecular mass hyaluronic acid , contributing to the elastic wrinkled skin of NMR , and resistance to 242 

osteoarthritis 97 . TMM33 is an endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane protein that regulates ER 243 

morphology and ER –mitochondria contact sites involved in calcium signaling and cellular 244 8

stress response 98 . Deletion of a centrally located ordered region in NMR’s TMEM33 may 245 

weaken or dynami ze interactions with ER tethering complexes, potentially limiting excessive 246 

ER –mitochondrial calcium transfer and contributing to enhanced cellular stress tolerance 247 

248 

KLF4 is a transcription factor and epigenetic regulator that acts as a pioneer factor, recruiting 249

Ga in of redox -sensitiv ity in naked mole rat 285 

To gain deeper insight, w e calculated the relative amino acid propensities in in dels . Deletion s were 286 

relatively enriched with tyrosine residues, whil e insertions were marked by tryptophan and cysteine 287 

residues (Figure 8A ). Redox induced tyrosine modifications, like nitration , contributes to aging and 288 

cardiovascular pathologies like atherosclerosis , which naked mole rats are resistant to 58,114,115 .289 

Through inference of tyrosine modif ications using a deep learning framework (Methods), we 290 

observed their relative enrichment in the deleted regions in NMR (Figure 8B). Their loss may imply 291 

greater disease surveillance in in NMR . Tryptophan engages in phase -separation through pi -pi 292 

interactions 116 . Their gain may impl y increase phase separation and proteostasis in naked mole rat. 293 

Cysteine residues can act as redox sensors 117 –121 . Overrepresentation of cysteine in inserted regions 294 

may hint at gain of redox sensing function in NMR . The observation also aligns with an earlier study, 295 

where authors observed abundance of cysteine residues in NMR’s proteome 9. Through inference of 296 

cysteine modif ications, we observed relative ly greater tendency of cysteine modif ications in the 297 

inserted regions in NMR (Figure 8B). The role of cysteine is best illustrated by the example of TCF20 298 

protein, which gained an entire P450 domain in naked mole rat (Figure 7) . A highly 299 

conserved cysteine residue, part of the Phe -X-X-Gly -X-Arg -X-Cys -X-Gly consensus signature in 300 

P450 domain, acts as an essential axial ligand to the heme iron 122 . Typically existing as a deprotonated 301 

thiolate, the cysteine side chain lowers the iron's resting redox potential to prevent premature electron 302 

transfer 122 . The substrate binding triggers a conformational shift that finetunes the cysteine -iron 303 

interaction, sharply increasing the redox potential to "sense" and initiate efficient electron flow for 304 

catalysis 122 . These results further warranted the ana lysis of redox -sensitiv ity of the observed intrinsic 305 

disorder in NMR . Through inference of redox -sensitive disorder 123 , we observed that the redox -306 

sensitive disorder co incided with insertions in naked mole rat when compared to deletions (tested in 307 

mouse) (Figure 8C). To control for the overall disorder, the redox -sensitive disorder scores were 308 

divided by the overall disorder of the proteins (Methods) . We illustrate the example of Calpain -2, a 309 

cysteine protease and ANXA6, a calcium -activated membrane -scaffold protein (Figure 8D-E). The 310 

injured plasm a membrane recruits calcium activated Annexins to form scab -like structures, followed 311 

by gradual cleavage of annexins by calpain -2 and finally the release of annexin -containing 312 

microvesicles 124 . Calpain -2 showed gain of a cysteine containing disordered region towards N -313 

terminal of cysteine protease domain in NMR , which is critical in sensing the oxidative stress 125 .314 10 

Concomitantly, ANXA6 has also gained a redox -sensitive an PEST -containing spacer between 315 

annexin domains, which may enhance its responsiveness and endow conformationally adaptive 316 

binding properties under stress conditions. These observations coherently implied that the gain and 317 

loss of intrinsic disorder likely endowed the robustness against redox stress by purging the damaging 318 

residues and incorporating the redox sensors. 319

Discussion 321 

Pronounced divergence of intrinsic disorder highlighted an overlooked aspect of naked mole rat 322 

biology. The evolutionary gain of disorder correlated with increased phase separation potential and 323 

protein degradation rates, aligning with the enhanced proteostasi s in NMR . Several studies have 324 

demonstrated that intrinsically disordered regions can directly im plicate in or facilitate the stress -325 

induced phase separat ion, a property which is suggested to be adaptive and evolutionarily fine -326 

tuned 126 . It is , therefore, plausible that the gain of disorder may modulate the stress tolerance in NMR 327 

by enhancing phase separation potential of protein engaged in NMR . This is supported by the 328 

enrichment of stress related functional terms like protein catabolic process, positive regulation of 329 

apoptosis, and phophatidylniositol -3-kinase signalling terms among the proteins that gained disorder 330 

in NMR. Gained enrichment of cysteine residues, g reater overall potential of redox -sensitive disorder 331 

in the proteins gaining disorder (tested in NMR) when compared to the ones losing disorder in NMR 332 

(tested in mouse), further strengthens the proposal. The example s of Calpain -2 and Annexin -A6 333 

exemplifies the gain of redox -induced disorder in NMR . ANXA6 is a calc ium -dependent , pH -334 

sensitive, membrane scaffold that is activated upon hypoxia stre ss to remodel and repair membrane, 335 

coordinate stress signalling and preserve cellular integrity 127,128 . ANXA6 also exhibits tumor -336 

suppressor properties in melanoma, breast cancer, epithelial carcinoma, gastric cancer, and prostate 337 

cancer by restraining proliferative and survival signaling (e.g., Ras/MAPK and EGFR pathways) and 338 

by promoting membrane and signaling homeostasis 129 . Thus, its upregulation during hypoxia may not 339 

only enhance cellular stress tolerance but also limit maladaptive hyperproliferation in chronically low -340 

oxygen environments. Gain of a stress -sensitive intrinsically disordered region between Annexin 341 

domains may allow conformationally adaptive membrane properties to sense and interact with 342 

varying membrane curvatures under stress conditions. It was noticeable that Calpain -2, which also 343 

gained redox -sensitive disorder, also participates in membrane repair by cleaving the annexins after 344 

scab formation. Interestingly, gained disordered in Annexin -A6 also had a PEST motif, a well -known 345 

substrate of calpain -mediated and other pathways of protein degradation. This implies a346 

complementary and pathway level evolutionary convergence of redox -sensitive intrinsic disorder in 347 

NMR (Figure 9).348 

349 11 

The association between gain of intrinsic disorder and anti -tumorigenic signatures , such as 350 

downregulation, recurrent deletions, and negative hazard log -ratios in cancer patients , suggests that 351 

enhanced phase -separation propensity and reduced degradation arising from disorder may enable 352 

sustained tumour -suppressive signalling in NMR . Through quality -controlled proteostasis, these 353 

proteins can buffer cellular stress and support long -term survival under chronically adverse 354 

conditions. Loss of disorder in heart development related factors, like HAND1, on the other hand ,355 

may imply an evolutionary strategy to endow noise mitigation, avoid promiscuous interactions, and 356 

allow for expression divergence. Expression of proteins with intrinsic disorder are often tightly 357 

regulated at transcription and as well as translation level in order to avoid inappropriate expression 358 

levels, which may cause toxicity. The expression levels of dis ordered proteins may thus be 359 

constrained. Loss of disorder may, however, relieve the constraint and allow the expression to 360 

diverge adaptively, likely underpinning its exceptional cardiovascular health , in NMR. 361

Conclusion 363 

Altogether, the study highlighted dramatic evolutionary remodelling of naked mole rat’s proteome. 364 

The functional polarization of disorder turnover, align ed with the hallmark traits of naked mole rat 365 

like stress tolerance, longevity, cancer resistance , immunity, and cardiovascular health underscores its 366 

likely adaptive significance. The findings position turnover of intrinsic disorder as dynamic 367 

evolutionary tool shaping complex traits. The study also highlights the need to integrate intrinsic 368 

disorder in to evolutio nary framework to understand the molecular basis of lineage -specific 369 

innovations. 370
