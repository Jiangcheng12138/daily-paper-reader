• Heart, adrenal gland, corpus callosum, caudate putamen are hotspots of miRNA 37 

age deregulation , with dramatic influence from sex. 38 

• Non -brain organs show tissue specific miRNA change , with inconsistent overlap 39 

between tissues. 40

# Introduction 51 

Between the years 2015 and 2050, the global population over the age of 60 is expected 52 

to double to 2.1 billion while the population over 80 is expected to triple 1. Improvement in 53 

health -span has not kept pace with increase in lifespan, resulting in many individuals 54 

suffering substantial age -related decline in quality of life. This shift places a significant 55 

burden on individuals and healthcare systems with people 85 -and -above requiring up to 56 

7 times more medical expenditure compared to individuals in their late -30 s2. Therefore, 57 

there is growing interest in understanding aging as a biological process that can be 58 

intervened upon to increase human health span. 59 

Mortality increases exponentially with age and can be modelled as an accumulation of 60 

multiple sources of damage over time 3. However, finding the se specific components and 61 

designing appropriate interventions represents a n ongoing challenge. Major progress 62 

made so far has been summarized in the Hallmarks of Aging 4 and includes somatic DNA 63 

damage accumulation with age 5, loss of proteostasis 6, deregulation of lncRNA 7 and loss 64 

of CpG methylation 8. Among the multiple layers of epigenetic regulation, miRNAs are 65 

included in the hallmarks of aging. 66 

Since the identification of the first miRNA lin -49, our knowledge of this class of non -coding 67 

RNA has exploded . MiRNA biogenesis, species conservation 10 and 3’ untranslated region 68 

targeted downregulation of mRNA is well -established . In addition to fine -tun ing target 69 

gene expression levels 11,12 , they cancel noise in randomly transcribed mRNA 13 and 70 

canalize the transcriptome of cell types 14 . Deepening mechanistic understanding of 71 

miRNA targeting 15,16 has opened up their application in iPSC reprogramming 17 , tissue -72 

specific gene vector transcription 18 and a recent breakthrough in the phase 1/2 treatment 73 

of Huntington’s Disease 19 . Atlas -level next -generation sequencing studies 20,21 shows that 74 

the expression of miRNAs is highly organ -specific and serve as signatures of age -related 75 

diseases such as cancer 22 and Parkinson’s disease progression 23 .76 

MiRNA expression change is widespread during aging, showing strong tissue -specific 77 

signals in human brains 24 , in mice full body organs 25 and brain regions 26 . Multiple miRNA 78 

families such as MIR -29 25 , MIR -17 27 and MIR -188 28 have emerged as age -associated 79 

miRNA, alongside being involved in general stress response 29 . However, this information 80 

has not yet been translated to humans. While mice share miRNA targeting and expression 81 

patterns with humans, there are also large families of miRNA that are specific to primates 30 .82 

Even though miRNA sequences themselves are often heavily conserved across 83 

mammalian genomes , miRNA s are often transcribed from poorly conserved long non -84 

coding RNA host genes 31,32 or vary in their genomic context such as transcription factor 85 

binding sites or their chromosom al position 33 . This difference is potentially contributing to 86

While there are studies of miRNA expression in individual tissues for macaque 37,38 , aging 95

# Discussion 467 

Our dataset represents an unprecedented breadth of tissue, age and gender sampling for 468 

Macaque mulatta and Macaque fascicularis in comparison to previous studies focusing 469 

on a small number of tissues or blood 30,37 . Our detection of at least 400 miRNAs stably 470 

expressed across all tissues lines up with observations from humans and mice 44 . We are 471 

also able to successfully split the regions in our UMAP embeddi ngs and pairwise 472 

clustering, suggesting successful tissue extraction and library preparation. However, we 473 

were tentative in interpreting the results from gallbladder due to high ratio of unique 474 

miRNA to aligned read s ratio .475 

While the monkeys were reared in a relatively controlled environment in comparison to 476 

being in the wild, there exist uncontrollable sources of variance . Individual variation is 477 

notable, especially in the non -brain organ samples. Among all the non -coding RNA types 478 

that are quantifiable using small RNA sequencing, the full length of mature miRNA 479 

molecules is captured by SE50 sequencing . MiRNA is also the best studied regulatory 480

Our findings agree with previous reports that miRNA deregulation with age does not 483

## Macaque monkey rearing conditions 600 

Rhesus macaques ( Macaca mulatta ) and cynomolgus macaques ( Macaca fascicularis )601 

included in this study were housed at the Biomedical Primate Research Centre (BPRC, 602 

Rijswijk, The Netherlands), a non -profit research institute maintaining a self -sustaining 603 

breeding colony of nonhuman primates established in the 1970s. Animals originate from 604 

captive -bred lines, with periodic introduction of new breeding lines to maintain genetic 605 

diversity and outbred status. 606 

All housing and animal care procedures were conducted in accordance with European 607 

Directive 2010/63/EU and the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals (NIH), 608 

and the facility is accredited by AAALAC International. Animal welfare and tissue 609 

collection procedures were reviewed internally by the institutional animal welfare body. 610 

No experimental interventions beyond routine veterinary care were performed on the 611 

animals included in this study. 612 

Animals were socially housed in stable family groups of approximately 20 –30 individuals, 613 

consisting of multiple matrilines and one adult male, reflecting natural social structures. 614 

Environmental enrichment was provided continuously. Animals were fed a commercially 615 

available primate diet (SSniff, Germany) supplemented with fruits, vegetables, and grains, 616 

with water available ad libitum. 617 

Animals were monitored daily by trained caretakers, with health assessments including 618 

behavioral observations, injury checks, and evaluation of general condition. In addition, 619 

animals underwent routine veterinary examinations, including yearly blood sampling for 620 

haematological and biochemical analyses, as part of the health monitoring program. 621 

Tissue collection for this study was performed within the framework of the BPRC 622 

Nonhuman Primate Brain Bank (NHPBB) 83 . Postmortem procedures were carried out 623

Germany). RNA concentration was determined using the NanoDrop ™ 2000 631 

spectrophotometer (Thermo Fisher Scientific, Waltham, MA, USA) and RNA integrity of 632 

all samples was assessed using the Agilent RNA 6000 Nano Kit for Agilent 2100 633

prepared on the high -throughput MGISP -960 sample preparation system using 635 

the MGIEasy smallRNA Library Prep Kit (MGI Tech Co. Ltd., Shenzhen, 636 

Guangdong, China). In brief, 100 ng of total RNA was used as input. After adapter ligation, 637 

reverse transcription. PCR amplification and size selection, concentrations of amplified 638 

PCR products were measured using Qubit ™ 1x dsDNA HS Assay Kit (Thermo Fisher 639 

Scientific, Waltham, MA, USA). For each library, 20 to 21 different barcoded samples were 640 

pooled and circularized to generate single -stranded DNA libraries (ssDNA), and the 641 

concentration of the ssDNA libraries was determined using Qubit ™ ssDNA Assay Kit 642 

(Thermo Fisher Scientific, Waltham, MA, USA). Subsequently, ssDNA libraries were used 643 

to generate DNA nanoballs (DNB) by rolling circle amplification. Libraries were 644 

sequenced using the DNBSEQ -G400RS High -Throughput Sequencing Reagents Set 645

## Ag ing trajectory calculation 693 

Due to the dearth of sampling density across the approx imately 30 year lifespan of rhesus 694 

macaque, we opted a multi -pronged approach to finding deregulated non -coding RNAs. 695 

We calculated the spearman rank correlation, a non -parametric test of correlation , and 696 

filtered by a correlation coefficient of 0.3. 697 

Since Spearman rank is the covariance of two variables (here age and rpmm expression) 698 

normalized by their standard deviations, the absolute value is not necessarily the effect 699 

size of age on rpmm. Therefore, we fit an ordinary least square (OLS) equation between 700 

age and miRNA rpmm, using the change over the lifespan as a proxy for effect size. We 701 

plotted the distribution of the OLS -predicted expression changes and based on 702 

expression change value where the frequency went through the greatest change, picked 703 

thresholds of +10 and -10 rpmm to filter for the changes with the largest effect size. 704

## miRNA pathway analysis calculations 726 

For M. mulatta , currently there is no large set of experimentally validated miRNA -target 727 

interactions available . Therefore, we had to rely on predicted seed -target hits that are 728 

highly conserved in the Targe tscan database 15 . Furthermore , we filtered using a 75% 729 

context score threshold to reduce the likelihood of false positives. 730 

Using the list of targets, we used clusterprofiler 92 to perform overrepresentation analysis 731 

using the enrichKEGG function 93 . The background universe was set as the overlap of 732 

mapped targets from Targetscan and Entrez gene names. The parameters used were 733 

minGSSize of 3, maxGSSize of 500, with a Benjamini -Hochberg pAdjustMethod. An 734 

adjusted pvalue cutoff of 0.05 was applied . We report the unaggregated pathways left with 735 

this cutoff along with pathways aggregated by subcategory, to reduce the number of 736 

pathways in our graph and provide a more coarse overview. For the aggregation, the 737 

number of genes on each pathway inside each subcategory were summed together, while 738 

the negative log10 of pvalues are logged. 739 

## miRNA homology and evolutionary age calculation 740 

To assign the years when a miRNA precursor locus originated, information from 741 

mirgenedb 3.0 and TimeTree 5 94 was used. For the list of taxonomic names list in the 742 

“Node of origin (locus)” column of mirgenedb entries, we assigned a taxonomic id (taxid) 743 

by using the Python script accessing the eutils API from NCBI 95 . The taxid list was used 744 

with the TimeTree 5 API (accessed August 25 2025) to access the corresponding branch 745 

length of the node. The branch node corresponds to Millions of Years Ago (MYA). Since 746 

we also map mirgenedb names to miRbase names which contain miRNAs that have not 747 

been assigned to homologues, we make the assumption that these miRNA loci are 748 

species -specific are as old as the species itself with value s of 6.19 MYA for M. mulatta 749 

and 6.77 MYA for M. musculus .750
