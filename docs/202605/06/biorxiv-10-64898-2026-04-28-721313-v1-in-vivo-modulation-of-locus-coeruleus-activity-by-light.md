## 摘要
蓝斑核（LC）是脑干中的一个小核团，是大脑去甲肾上腺素的主要来源，其功能障碍与多种神经和精神疾病有关。光照影响许多非成像（NIF）生物功能，包括受 LC 活动调节的功能。然而，由于该结构体积小且位置深，光驱动 LC 调节的直接证据一直难以获得。利用超高场（7 特斯拉）功能磁共振成像（fMRI），在不同照度下的情绪任务中，我们证明了光以情绪依赖的方式调节 LC 活动。我们表明，增加照度会减弱 LC 对负面情绪刺激的反应，同时增强对中性刺激的反应。此外，我们提供了初步证据，表明在情绪背景下，光可能通过杏仁核基底内侧核以及包括下丘脑外侧区在内的下丘脑区域影响 LC 活动。这些发现为开发针对性的非药物治疗开辟了道路，利用光在几种神经精神疾病中调节去甲肾上腺素水平和/或 LC 活动。

意义声明：蓝斑核（LC）是大脑的一个小核团，也是去甲肾上腺素的主要来源，其功能障碍与多种脑部疾病有关。在这里，我们提供了活体证据，证明光照可以改变 LC 的活动，较高的照度会导致 LC 对负面情绪刺激的反应减弱。这些发现确定了 LC 是光对情绪产生治疗作用的关键靶点，为利用光治疗神经精神疾病提供了生物学框架。

## Abstract
Dysfunction of the Locus coeruleus (LC), a small brainstem nucleus which consists of the brains primary source of norepinephrine, is linked to various neurological and psychiatric disorders. Light exposure influences many non-image-forming (NIF) biological functions including those modulated by LC activity. Yet direct evidence of light-driven modulation of the LC has remained elusive due to the structures small size and deep location. Using ultra-high-field (7 Tesla) functional MRI during an emotional task under varying illuminances, we demonstrate that light modulates LC activity in an emotion-dependent manner. We show that increased illuminance dampens LC responses to negative emotional stimuli while enhancing responses to neutral stimuli. Furthermore, we provide tentative evidence that within an emotional context, light may affect LC activity through the basomedial nucleus of the amygdala as well as through a region of the hypothalamus encompassing the lateral hypothalamus. These findings open avenues for the development of targeted, non-pharmacological treatments leveraging light to modulate norepinephrine tone and/or LC activity in several neuropsychiatric disorders.

Significance StatementDysfunction of the Locus Coeruleus (LC), a small nucleus of brain and the main source of norepinephrine, is linked to various brain disorders. Here we provide in vivo evidence that exposure to light can alter the activity of the LC with higher illuminance leading to dampened LC responses to negative emotional stimuli. These findings identify the LC as a key target for the therapeutic effects of light on emotion, offering a biological framework for using light to treat neuropsychiatric disorders.
