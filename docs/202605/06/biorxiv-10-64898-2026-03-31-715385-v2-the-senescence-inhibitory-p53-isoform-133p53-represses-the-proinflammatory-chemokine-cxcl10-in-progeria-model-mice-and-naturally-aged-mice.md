## 摘要
目的：Δ133p53 是人类 p53 蛋白的一种天然存在的异构体，可抑制 p53 介导的细胞衰老。我们此前曾报道，这种抑制衰老的 p53 异构体的转基因表达可以抵消早衰模型小鼠（杂合子 LmnaG609G/+）中与衰老相关的病理变化。Δ133p53 的抗衰老作用部分归因于促炎细胞因子 IL-6 水平的降低。本研究旨在全面分析 Δ133p53 诱导的细胞因子和趋化因子的变化。方法：使用表达转基因 Δ133p53 的 LmnaG609G/+ 小鼠和不表达该基因的对照组小鼠的血清样本，进行了基于 Luminex 的多重定量分析。使用定量 RT-PCR 和 RNA 原位杂交分析评估了小鼠组织中 Cxcl10 的表达。此外，还分析了来自人类组织的基因表达数据集。结果：我们证实了 Δ133p53 介导的血清 IL-6 水平抑制。我们还发现 Δ133p53 降低了血清中 CXCL1、IL-1 和 CXCL10 的水平。我们进一步表征了 CXCL10，此前该因子尚未被发现与小鼠或人类的早衰症相关。与血清 CXCL10 水平降低一致，与年龄匹配的非表达对照组相比，表达 Δ133p53 的年轻（15 周龄）和年老（10 个月龄）LmnaG609G/+ 小鼠在肝脏、脾脏和大脑（产生 CXCL10 的主要器官）中均表现出 Cxcl10 表达降低。在自然衰老的野生型小鼠（2 岁）中，转基因 Δ133p53 的表达也显著抑制了脾脏和大脑中 Cxcl10 的表达。在人类脾脏组织中观察到 CXCL10 与 Δ133p53 水平之间存在负相关，表明其与人类衰老具有生理相关性。结论：CXCL10 是一种在加速衰老和自然衰老中均升高的促炎趋化因子，是 Δ133p53 抗炎活性的潜在靶点。

## Abstract
Aims {Delta}133p53 is a naturally occurring isoform of the human p53 protein that inhibits p53-mediated cellular senescence. We previously reported that transgenic expression of this senescence-inhibitory p53 isoform counteracts aging-associated pathological changes in progeria model mice (heterozygous LmnaG609G/+). The anti-aging effect of {Delta}133p53 was attributed in part to reduced levels of the proinflammatory cytokine IL-6. This study aims to comprehensively profile {Delta}133p53-induced changes in cytokines and chemokines. Methods A Luminex-based multiplex quantitative assay was performed using mouse serum samples from transgenic {Delta}133p53-expressing LmnaG609G/+ mice and non-expressing controls. Quantitative RT-PCR and RNA in situ hybridization assays were used to assess Cxcl10 expression in mouse tissues. In addition, gene expression datasets from human tissues were analyzed. Results We confirmed {Delta}133p53-mediated repression of serum IL-6 levels. We also found that {Delta}133p53 reduced serum levels of CXCL1, IL-1, and CXCL10. We further characterized CXCL10, which has not previously been associated with progeria in mice or humans. Consistent with reduced serum CXCL10 levels, both young (15-week-old) and old (10-month-old) {Delta}133p53-expressing LmnaG609G/+ mice showed reduced Cxcl10 expression in the liver, spleen, and brain, major organs that produce CXCL10, compared with age-matched non-expressing controls. In naturally aged wild-type mice (2 years old), transgenic {Delta}133p53 expression also significantly repressed Cxcl10 expression in the spleen and brain. An inverse association between CXCL10 and {Delta}133p53 levels was observed in human spleen tissues, suggesting physiological relevance to human aging. Conclusion CXCL10, a proinflammatory chemokine elevated in both accelerated and natural aging, is a potential target of the anti-inflammatory activity of {Delta}133p53.
