## 摘要
目的：有氧代谢为神经活动提供约 90% 的 ATP。在青蛙中，神经活动具有典型脊椎动物的大量有氧需求，但令人惊讶的是，在冬眠苏醒后，其可以转为仅利用糖酵解。我们假设冬眠触发了神经功能有氧成本的全局性降低。方法：我们同时测量了对照组和冬眠组牛蛙（4周冷水浸没；4°C）离体脑干呼吸网络通过运动神经产生的活动以及组织氧分压（pO2）。为了确定哪些功能差异性地消耗氧气，我们依次阻断了神经活动以及各种需要非活动依赖性离子调节的细胞过程，并将由此产生的组织氧分压变化（ΔpO2）作为耗氧量的指标。我们进一步评估了神经活动如何随组织氧分压变化，以及耗氧量如何在不同网络活动水平下变化。结果：尽管网络活动水平相似，我们提供了三方面的证据表明冬眠降低了其有氧需求。首先，冬眠个体在基础活动中消耗的氧气较少。其次，网络输出从基线到缺氧状态保持稳定，而中度低氧则会干扰对照组。最后，加速活动不会像对照组那样增加耗氧量，但在癫痫样活动期间，有氧代谢最终会增加。结论：冬眠的青蛙降低了维持生理水平神经活动所需的有氧需求，揭示了它们如何在冬眠苏醒期间的低氧背景下克服重启运动回路的挑战。更广泛地说，看似受限于有氧代谢的脊椎动物神经回路在功能所需的有氧要求方面表现出显著的可塑性。实践要点：1. 牛蛙的冬眠在维持正常网络输出的同时减少了神经活动消耗的氧气，证明了脊椎动物大脑功能的有氧需求并非固定不变。2. 冬眠后，脑干运动回路在从高氧到缺氧的过程中保持稳定的功能，并且当活动在生理范围内升高时，耗氧量不会增加。3. 这些发现揭示了脊椎动物神经回路可以进入所需有氧呼吸少得多的代谢状态，这可能为在氧气输送受损和其他代谢功能障碍的情况下提高大脑代谢韧性的策略提供参考。

## Abstract
AimAerobic metabolism supplies [~]90% of the ATP for neural activity. In frogs, activity has large aerobic needs typical of an average vertebrate, but surprisingly, can shift to using only glycolysis upon emergence from hibernation. We hypothesized that hibernation triggers a global reduction in the aerobic cost of neural function.

MethodsWe simultaneously measured activity of the brainstem respiratory network via motor nerves and tissue oxygen partial pressure (pO2) in vitro from control and hibernated bullfrogs (4 weeks cold submergence; 4{degrees}C). To identify which functions differentially consume O2, we sequentially blocked activity and various cellular processes requiring activity-independent ion regulation and used the resulting tissue pO2 change ({Delta}pO2) as an index of O2 consumed. We further assessed how activity varies as a function of tissue pO2 and how O2 consumption varies across network activity levels.

ResultsDespite similar network activity levels, we provide three lines of evidence that hibernation reduces its aerobic requirement. First, hibernators consume less oxygen for baseline activity. Second, network output remains stable from baseline to anoxia, while moderate hypoxia disrupts controls. Finally, accelerating activity does not enhance oxygen consumption as in controls, but aerobic metabolism ultimately increases during seizure-like activity.

ConclusionHibernating frogs reduce aerobic needs for sustaining physiological levels of neural activity, revealing how they overcome the challenge of restarting motor circuits on the background of hypoxia during emergence from hibernation. More broadly, vertebrate neural circuits seemingly constrained by aerobic metabolism can exhibit substantial plasticity in the aerobic requirements for function.

Practitioner pointsO_LIHibernation in bullfrogs reduces the oxygen consumed by neural activity while maintaining normal network output, demonstrating that the aerobic requirements of brain function are not fixed in the vertebrate brain.
C_LIO_LIAfter hibernation, brainstem motor circuits maintain stable function from high levels of O2 to anoxia and do not increase O2 consumption when activity is elevated within the physiological range.
C_LIO_LIThese findings reveal that vertebrate neural circuits can enter metabolic states requiring far less aerobic respiration, which may inform strategies for improving metabolic resilience in the brain during conditions of impaired oxygen delivery and other metabolic dysfunction.

### 6. 论文的主要结论与发现
*   **有氧需求降低**：冬眠牛蛙在维持相同水平的神经活动时，消耗的氧气显著少于对照组。
