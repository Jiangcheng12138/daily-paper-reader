## 摘要
背景：昼夜节律去同步化（CD）发生在昼夜节律生物钟与当地时间不匹配时，例如轮班工作。小鼠模型常用于研究 CD，但可能存在显著缺点，如环境掩蔽、仅关注睡眠生理学以及研究设计之间的显著差异。

目的：本研究利用体内遥测技术，对自由活动的 C57BL/6J 小鼠的运动活性（LA）、核心体温（CBT）和脑电活动（EEG）进行同步实时监测，以评估 CD 的影响。

方法：为 4 月龄 C57BL/6J 小鼠（n=11）手术植入遥测仪，以实现 LA、CBT 和 EEG 的同步实时记录。小鼠先后暴露于对照条件（标准 12:12h 光照-黑暗周期，T24），随后是 4 个为期 8 天的 CD 范式：10:10h 短昼夜周期（T20）、社交时差（SJL）、重复 6h 相位提前（6A2）以及 3:3h 超昼夜周期（T6）。分析每个范式最后 48 小时的数据（采样率 250 Hz）。

结果：我们发现各 CD 范式对睡眠和昼夜节律健康影响的严重程度存在明显差异，表现为 T20 ≈ T6 > SJL > 6A2。CBT 显示出更广泛的紊乱，但 EEG 输出被证明是内部去同步化最敏感的指标。

结论：每种 CD 范式在行为、生理和神经领域都产生了独特的特征。我们还确定了 Gamma CV 是一种新型、敏感的 CD 指标。这些结果强调了多模态监测对于准确表征生态相关压力源对昼夜节律和睡眠生理影响的必要性。

意义声明：由轮班工作、时差和现代不规则光照驱动的昼夜节律去同步化（CD）是与代谢、神经退行性及神经精神疾病相关的重大健康负担。然而，实验室模型中测量 CD 的标准方法通常依赖于简单的运动活性，这可能会“掩盖”内部昼夜节律压力的真实程度。在本研究中，我们在四种不同的昼夜节律压力模型中同步监测了脑电图（EEG）活动、核心体温和运动。我们发现运动活性是一个具有欺骗性的健康指标；虽然小鼠在几种压力范式下似乎没有表现出变化，但它们的脑波和体温揭示了 CD 的潜在影响。具体而言，我们确定了“Gamma CV”作为一种高度敏感的新型脑波标志物，即使在行为表现正常且睡眠量得以维持的情况下，也能检测到早期的环路不稳定性。这些发现为识别对不规则光照计划的早期神经脆弱性提供了一个标志物，为理解成瘾、早期阿尔茨海默病和其他疾病中观察到的类似 Gamma 脑波变化提供了潜在桥梁。

## Abstract
2)BackgroundCircadian rhythm desynchrony (CD) occurs when there is a mismatch between the circadian clock and local time, such as shift work. Mouse models are commonly employed to study CD, but may have significant shortcomings such as environmental masking, a focus only on sleep physiology, and significant variability between study designs.

ObjectiveThis study used in vivo telemetry for simultaneous, real-time monitoring of locomotor activity (LA), core body temperature (CBT), and brain activity (EEG) in freely moving C57BL/6J mice to assess CD effects.

MethodsFour-month-old C57BL/6J mice (n=11) were surgically implanted with telemeters enabling simultaneous real-time recording of LA, CBT, EEG.: Mice were sequentially exposed to a control condition standard 12:12h light-dark cycle (T24) then 4, 8-day CD paradigms: 10:10 h short day (T20), social jet lag (SJL), repeated 6h phase advances (6A2), and a 3:3 h ultradian cycle (T6)For each paradigm, the final 48h of data (250 Hz) were analyzed.

ResultsWe found clear differences in the severity of the effects of each CD paradigm on sleep and circadian fitness, where T20[~]T6>SJL>6A2. CBT revealed broader disruption, but EEG outputs proved the most sensitive indicators of internal desynchrony.

ConclusionsEach CD paradigm produced a unique profile across behavioral, physiological, and neural domains. We have also identified Gamma CV as a novel, sensitive metric of CD. These results highlight the necessity of multimodal monitoring to accurately characterize the impact of ecologically relevant stressors on circadian and sleep physiology.

Statement of SignificanceCircadian rhythm desynchrony (CD), driven by shift work, jet lag, and modern irregular light exposure, is a major health burden linked to metabolic, neurodegenerative, and neuropsychiatric diseases. However, standard methods for measuring CD in laboratory models often rely on simple locomotor activity, which can "mask" the true extent of internal circadian stress. In this study, we simultaneously monitored brain EEG activity, core body temperature, and motion across four distinct models of circadian stress. We discovered that locomotor activity is a deceptive indicator of health; while mice appeared to show no alterations under several stress paradigms, their brain waves and body temperatures revealed the underlying impact of CD. Specifically, we identified "Gamma CV" as a highly sensitive new brain-wave marker that detects early circuit instability even when behavior appears normal and sleep quantity is preserved. These findings provide a marker for identifying early neurological vulnerability to irregular light schedules, offering a potential bridge to understanding similar gamma brain-wave alterations seen in addiction, early-stage Alzheimers disease, and other disorders.
