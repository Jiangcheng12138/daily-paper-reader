## 摘要
动物通过器官间通讯将环境信号与内部生理状态整合，以调节生殖。然而，环境微生物如何被纳入这些系统性通路以控制生殖干细胞增殖，目前仍知之甚少。在本研究中，我们证明定植于肠道的环境微生物通过增加生殖系干细胞（GSC）的数量，促进黑腹果蝇中由干细胞介导的卵子发生。这一过程需要微生物激活肠道代谢通路，包括糖酵解和磷酸戊糖途径（PPP），从而将微生物信号与机体生理联系起来。我们进一步表明，微生物信号调节肠道和大脑中的昼夜节律基因表达，且这些组织中的节律基因活性是微生物诱导 GSC 增加所必需的。这些代谢变化促进了卵巢体细胞中的蜕皮激素和保幼激素信号传导，单细胞转录组分析进一步揭示了生殖系和卵泡细胞群体中细胞类型特异性的代谢和激素反应。总之，我们的研究结果表明，微生物依赖的肠道介导器官间通讯整合了代谢、昼夜节律基因表达和内分泌信号，从而调节果蝇中由干细胞介导的生殖。

## Abstract
Animals integrate environmental cues with internal physiological states through inter-organ communication to regulate reproduction. However, how environmental microbes are incorporated into these systemic pathways to control reproductive stem cell proliferation remains poorly understood. Here, we demonstrate that environmental microbes colonizing the gut promote stem cell-mediated oogenesis in Drosophila melanogaster by increasing germline stem cell (GSC) number. This process requires microbial activation of gut metabolic pathways, including glycolysis and the pentose phosphate pathway (PPP), linking microbial cues to organismal physiology. We further show that microbial cues modulate circadian clock gene expression in both the gut and brain, and that circadian gene activity in these tissues is required for microbe-induced increases in GSCs. These metabolic changes promote ecdysone and juvenile hormone signaling in ovarian somatic cells, and single-cell transcriptomic analyses further reveal cell type-specific metabolic and hormonal responses across germline and follicle cell populations. Together, our findings establish that microbe-dependent gut-mediated inter-organ communication integrates metabolism, circadian gene expression, and endocrine signaling to regulate stem cell-mediated reproduction in Drosophila.
