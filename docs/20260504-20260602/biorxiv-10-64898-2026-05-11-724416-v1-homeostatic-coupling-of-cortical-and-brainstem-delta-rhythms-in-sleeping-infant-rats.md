## 摘要
安静睡眠（QS）期间皮层δ节律（1-4 Hz）的出现是大脑发育的一个重要里程碑。在大鼠中，这一里程碑在出生后（P）8至12天之间实现。我们先前报道了PZ区域δ节律活动随年龄增长而增加，并与皮层δ节律同步且受呼吸节律的诱导。在此，我们探究这种长距离同步在面对睡眠稳态或呼吸扰动时是否持续。首先，利用雄性和雌性P12大鼠，我们研究了在短期强烈睡眠剥夺后额叶皮层与PZ之间的耦合强度。在恢复性睡眠期间，即使QS持续时间未增加，我们观察到PZ和皮层中的δ功率均出现反弹，表明PZ和皮层δ功率是稳态睡眠调节的等效标志。锁相分析和滞后互相关分析显示，两种节律之间的时间耦合持续存在，无论睡眠压力如何变化，皮层δ节律始终滞后于PZδ节律。有趣的是，我们还在恢复性睡眠期间观察到呼吸深度增加，并在另一组幼鼠中进行了验证。接着，使用轻度高碳酸血症（5% CO2）改变呼吸频率和深度，导致皮层和PZδ功率下降，同时呼吸深度降低。这些发现进一步支持了以下观点：PZ和皮层δ节律作为远距离相互连接的组件，在一个发育过程中出现的睡眠-稳态系统中发挥作用，且该系统与脑干呼吸网络紧密相关。

重要意义：我们先前报道了定义慢波睡眠的δ节律不仅局限于前脑，还会在延髓中同步出现。本研究使用两种扰动（睡眠剥夺和高碳酸血症）评估幼鼠皮层与脑干δ节律的耦合强度。结果显示，两个区域的δ功率分别同步增加或减少。我们的结果强化了δ节律在这两个区域间紧密耦合的观点，并为我们理解δ节律与呼吸的相互关联增添了新的维度。

## Abstract
The emergence of the cortical delta rhythm (1-4 Hz) during quiet sleep (QS) is a major milestone in brain development. In rats, this milestone is achieved between 8 and 12 days of postnatal (P) age. We previously reported an age-dependent increase in PZ delta-rhythmic activity that is synchronized with cortical delta and entrained by breathing. Here, we ask whether this long-distance synchrony persists in response to perturbations to sleep homeostasis or respiration. First, using male and female P12 rats, we investigated the coupling strength between frontal cortex and PZ in response to a short but intense period of sleep deprivation. During recovery sleep, we observed a rebound in delta power in both PZ and cortex, even in the absence of increased QS duration, indicating that PZ and cortical delta power are equivalent markers of homeostatic sleep regulation. Analyses of phase-locking and lagged cross-correlation revealed persistent temporal coupling between the two rhythms such that cortical delta reliably lagged PZ delta regardless of changes in sleep pressure. Curiously, we also observed an increase in breathing depth during recovery sleep, which we confirmed in a separate cohort of pups. Next, using mild hypercapnia (5% CO2) to alter breathing frequency and depth, we produced decreases in cortical and PZ delta power along with decreases in the depth of breathing. These findings provide additional support for the notion that PZ and cortical delta rhythms function as distantly interconnected components within a developmentally emerging sleep-homeostatic system that is also intimately tied with the brainstem respiratory network.

SIGNIFICANCE STATEMENTWe reported previously that the delta rhythm that defines slow-wave sleep is not confined to the forebrain but also occurs synchronously in the medulla. This study in infant rats uses two perturbations to assess the coupling strength of cortical and brainstem delta. Using sleep deprivation and hypercapnia, we show that delta power increases or decreases in lockstep in the two regions, respectively. Our results reinforce the notion that delta across these two regions is strongly coupled and adds a new dimension to our understanding of the interconnectedness of the delta rhythm and respiration.
