## 摘要
睡眠紊乱是神经退行性疾病的早期且普遍特征，通常归因于神经元回路功能障碍或细胞丢失。然而，睡眠与代谢状态紧密相关，提示系统性代谢异常可能参与疾病相关的睡眠表型。我们利用TDP-43蛋白病的果蝇模型，研究了外周代谢功能障碍是否在睡眠紊乱中发挥因果作用。我们发现，TDP-43表达诱导一种慢性、类似饥饿的代谢状态，其特征是尽管正常摄食，但外周碳水化合物储存耗竭。恢复睡眠无法纠正这些代谢缺陷，而改善外周代谢状态则能显著挽救睡眠。一项约650个RNAi系的修饰因子筛选将盐诱导激酶3（SIK3）鉴定为睡眠丧失和饥饿敏感性的强效抑制因子。转录组学和空间代谢组学分析揭示，SIK3选择性重塑以磷酸戊糖途径和氧化还原相关代谢物为中心的外周代谢程序，而不整体恢复能量储存。总之，这些发现确定系统性代谢功能障碍是TDP-43蛋白病中睡眠紊乱的关键驱动因素，并强调外周代谢作为神经退行性疾病中睡眠功能障碍的潜在治疗切入点。

## Abstract
Sleep disruption is an early and prevalent feature of neurodegenerative disease, commonly attributed to neuronal circuit dysfunction or cell loss. However, sleep is tightly coupled to metabolic state, raising the possibility that systemic metabolic abnormalities contribute to disease-associated sleep phenotypes. Using Drosophila models of TDP-43 proteinopathy, we investigated whether peripheral metabolic dysfunction plays a causal role in sleep disruption. We show that TDP-43 expression induces a chronic, starvation-like metabolic state characterized by depletion of peripheral carbohydrate stores despite normal feeding. Restoration of sleep fails to correct these metabolic defects, whereas improving peripheral metabolic state robustly rescues sleep. A modifier screen of [~]650 RNAi lines identified Salt-inducible kinase 3 (SIK3) as a potent suppressor of both sleep loss and starvation sensitivity. Transcriptomic and spatial metabolomic analyses reveal that SIK3 selectively remodels a peripheral metabolic program centered on the pentose phosphate pathway and redox-associated metabolites without globally restoring energy stores. Together, these findings identify systemic metabolic dysfunction as a key driver of sleep disruption in TDP-43 proteinopathy and highlight peripheral metabolism as a potential therapeutic entry point for sleep dysfunction in neurodegenerative disease.
