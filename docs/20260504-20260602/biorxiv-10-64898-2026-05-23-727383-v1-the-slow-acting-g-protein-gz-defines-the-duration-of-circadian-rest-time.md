## 摘要
夜间休息的时长是个体间存在差异的一个性状，受到多种遗传和环境因素复杂相互作用的影响。调控行为/睡眠日常节律的中枢生物钟位于视交叉上核（SCN）。然而，SCN如何编码昼夜节律休息阶段（{rho}）的“长度”仍是一个未解之谜。本文证明，独特的G蛋白亚型Gz通过塑造SCN内昼夜节律cAMP-PKA活性节律的波形来参与这一过程。Gz的基因敲除及随后的表达恢复可逆地改变了{rho}的时长，使其从Gz+/+小鼠的约10小时转变为Gz-/-小鼠的约7.5小时，并伴随SCN中cAMP-PKA活性峰值时长的比例变化以及转录组重塑。值得注意的是，SCN内cAMP激活可导致行为性休息，而Gz特异地塑造了这种反应，且不影响24小时节律性。这些发现表明，SCN不仅是节律发生器，还通过Gz信号主动分配{rho}休息期。

## Abstract
Duration of nightly rest is a trait that varies between individuals, influenced by a complex interplay between multiple genetic and environmental factors. The central circadian clock that orchestrates daily rhythm in behavior/sleep resides in the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN). Yet, how the SCN encodes the "length" of the circadian rest phase ({rho}) remains an open question. Here we demonstrate that the unique G-protein-subtype Gz contributes to this process by sculpting the waveform of the circadian cAMP-PKA activity rhythm within the SCN. Genetic deletion and subsequent rescue of Gz expression reversibly altered the {rho} duration, shifting it from [~]10 h in Gz+/+ mice to [~]7.5 h in Gz-/-; mice, accompanied by proportional changes in peak cAMP-PKA activity duration and transcriptome remodeling in the SCN. Notably, intra-SCN cAMP activation led to behavioral rest, with Gz specifically shaping this response without affecting 24-h rhythmicity. These findings suggest that the SCN is not merely a rhythm-generator, but actively allocates the {rho}-rest period via Gz signaling.
