## 摘要
脑功能依赖于时间分辨的定向组织，即谁在何时、持续多久驱动谁。然而，动态神经影像学主要关注无向的、随时间变化的功能连接。在此，我们引入了滑动窗口预测相关（SWpC）作为一种可扩展的框架，用于量化时间分辨的定向影响，并将这些动态过程分解为两个生物学上截然不同的特征：定向强度（x[->]y 的预测效能）和定向持续时间（该影响持续的滞后跨度）。通过对大鼠同步局部场电位（LFP）-fMRI 和人类运动任务 fMRI 的研究，我们发现了趋同的特征：强度最直接地反映了神经-BOLD 耦合以及任务诱发的定向影响重构，而持续时间则捕捉了 BOLD 信号中明显的长尺度传播，后者更容易受到血流动力学定时的影响。作为临床演示，在脑震荡后前庭功能障碍的 fMRI 研究中，改变的强度-持续时间特征定义了可重复的前庭-多感觉状态，并提高了患者与对照组的区分度。总之，这些结果支持了一个普遍原则，即脑网络通过强度和时间持续时间这两个生物学上独特的特征来弹性地调节定向影响，涵盖了从神经生理学到神经系统疾病的范畴。

## Abstract
Brain function relies on time-resolved directional organization, i.e., who drives whom, when, and for how long. Yet, dynamic neuroimaging predominantly focuses on undirected, time-varying functional connectivity. Here, we introduce sliding-window prediction correlation (SWpC) as a scalable framework to quantify time-resolved directed influence and disentangle these dynamics into two biologically distinct signatures: directed strength (predictive efficacy of x[-&gt;]y) and directed duration (the lag span over which that influence persists). Across simultaneous rat local field potential (LFP)-fMRI and human motor-task fMRI, we find convergent signatures: strength most directly reflects neural-BOLD coupling and task-evoked reconfiguration of directional influence, whereas duration captures apparent longer-timescale propagation in BOLD, which is more susceptible to hemodynamic timing. As a clinical demonstration, in post-concussion vestibular dysfunction fMRI, altered strength-duration profiles define reproducible vestibular-multisensory states and improve patient-control discrimination. Together, these results support a general principle that brain networks elastically regulate directed influence through two biologically distinct signatures, i.e., strength and temporal duration, spanning neurophysiology to neurological disease.
