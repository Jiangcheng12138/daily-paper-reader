## 摘要
神经元活动的研究对于理解大脑功能及其在神经退行性疾病中的变化至关重要。活体成像技术的进步实现了对神经元动力学的实时观察，但经典的统计方法难以捕捉神经元网络中复杂的、随时间变化的相互作用。机器学习为分析高维神经元数据提供了极具前景的解决方案，但其在神经科学中的应用仍然有限。在此，我们介绍了 NEuRT，这是一种基于 Transformer 双向编码器表示（BERT）并针对神经元活动分析进行了适配的模型。NEuRT 利用自注意力机制来解释复杂的神经元相互作用，从而提供对传统方法可能忽略的模式的见解。NEuRT 在最近推出的大型标注数据集 MICrONS 上进行了信号重建预训练，展示了强大的泛化能力，能够有效地重建来自视觉皮层双光子和海马微型荧光显微镜的活动。基于 BERT 架构，NEuRT 模型可以针对广泛的下游任务进行高效微调。我们展示了其在基于海马活动对野生型和转基因阿尔茨海默病模型小鼠进行分类的应用，并通过注意力图分析揭示了群体特异性特征。通过减少对大量标注数据的依赖（这解决了神经科学中的一个关键挑战），NEuRT 桥接了基础神经科学和疾病研究，为人工智能驱动且可解释的神经元活动分析提供了一个稳健的框架。

## Abstract
The study of neuronal activity is essential for understanding brain function and its alterations in neurodegenerative diseases. Advances in in vivo imaging have enabled real-time observation of neuronal dynamics, but classical statistical methods struggle to capture the complex, time-dependent interactions within neuronal networks. Machine learning offers promising solutions for analyzing high-dimensional neuronal data, yet their application in neuroscience remains limited. Here, we introduce NEuRT, a Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT)-based model adapted for neuronal activity analysis. NEuRT leverages self-attention mechanisms to interpret complex neuronal interactions, providing insights into patterns that traditional methods may overlook. Pre-trained on the recently introduced large annotated dataset MICrONS for signal reconstruction, NeuRT demonstrates strong generalization, effectively reconstructing activity from both visual cortex two-photon and hippocampal miniature fluorescence microscopy. Built on the BERT architecture, the NEuRT model can be efficiently fine-tuned for a wide range of downstream tasks. We showcase its application in classifying wild-type and transgenic Alzheimer's disease model mice, based on hippocampal activity, revealing group-specific features through attention map analysis. By reducing reliance on extensive labeled data, addressing a critical challenge in neuroscience, NEuRT bridges fundamental neuroscience and disease research, offering a robust framework for AI-driven and explainable neuronal activity analysis.
