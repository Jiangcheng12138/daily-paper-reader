## 摘要
睡眠被认为对清除大脑废物至关重要，但其具体机制仍存在争议。在本研究中，我们证明血脑屏障（BBB）的脑内皮细胞（BECs）中的胞吞作用在睡眠期间增强，从而促进包括β-淀粉样蛋白（amyloid-β）在内的脑源性废物进入血液循环。通过蛋白质组学、体内示踪成像以及小鼠内皮特异性基因扰动，我们证明睡眠增强了脑内皮细胞（BECs）中胞吞囊泡的形成和货物的跨细胞转运（transcytosis）。相反，通过内皮 Dnm2 敲除阻断胞吞作用会抑制 BEC 介导的转运并增加睡眠需求，揭示了睡眠与血管胞吞作用之间的因果反馈回路。这些发现确定了 BBB 胞吞作用是一条关键的睡眠依赖性清除途径，对神经退行性疾病具有重要意义。

## Abstract
Sleep is thought to be important for the clearance of brain waste, but exactly how it does so is still debated. Here, we demonstrate that endocytosis in brain endothelial cells (BECs) of the blood-brain barrier (BBB) is enhanced during sleep, facilitating the removal of brain-derived waste, including amyloid-{beta}, into the circulation. Using proteomics, in vivo tracer imaging, and endothelial-specific genetic perturbations in mice, we demonstrate that sleep enhances endocytic vesicle formation and cargo transcytosis in brain endothelial cells (BECs). Conversely, blocking endocytosis through endothelial Dnm2 knockout suppresses BEC-mediated transport and elevates sleep need, revealing a causal feedback loop between sleep and vascular endocytosis. These findings identify BBB endocytosis as a key sleep-dependent clearance pathway with implications for neurodegenerative disease.
