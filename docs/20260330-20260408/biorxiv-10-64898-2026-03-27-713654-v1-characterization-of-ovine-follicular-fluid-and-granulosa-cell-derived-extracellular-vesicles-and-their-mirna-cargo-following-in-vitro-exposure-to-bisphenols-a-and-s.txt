Abstract 16 Bisphenol A (BPA) and Bisphenol S (BPS) exposure disrupt ovarian function and granulosa cell (GC) 17 steroidogenesis. Extracellular vesicles (EVs) and their miRNA cargo, as mediators of cellular response 18 to environmental stimuli, might be involved in fertility and folliculogenesis. This study explored 19 modulation of microRNA expression after 48h BPA or BPS exposure (10 μM) in ovine primary GC 20 and EVs from corresponding conditioned medium (CM EVs). Small RNA sequencing of control (0h) 21 and 48h treated GC, CM EVs as well as follicular fluid EVs allowed identification of 533 ovine 22 miRNAs, including 129 new sequences. BPA did not alter miRNA expression in GC, while BPS 23 decreased cellular oar-24b miR. In contrast, BPA modified expression of 4 miRNAs in CM-EVs, 24 including 3 new sequences, and two miRNAs were modified by BPS. Both compounds reduced 25 expression of sequence homologous to miR-1306. Further studies are required to decipher their roles 26 in bisphenol toxicity in GC. 27 28

Background 35 During folliculogenesis, the oocyte develops within the follicle, which provides the necessary 36 environment for its growth and acquisition of competence for fertilization and early embryo 37 development. This optimal environment is provided particularly by the surrounding follicular 38 granulosa cells (GC), which enable follicular growth and steroidogenesis (synthesis and secretion of 39 estradiol and progesterone), but also by the follicular fluid (FF). FF fills the antrum and is composed 40 of secretion products from follicular cells and molecules supplied by blood vessels such as ions, 41 hormones, growth factors, metabolic precursors (Monniaux et al., 2019). 42 Ovarian follicle growth, along with oocyte development and maturation, also requires constant, tight 43 communications between the oocyte and follicular cells such as GC and cumulus cells (Dalbies-Tran 44 et al., 2020; Monniaux et al., 2019). This communication includes the exchange of biomolecules like 45 nucleic acids or proteins and occurs through direct transfer between adjacent follicular cells (Collado 46 et al., 2018) mediated by extracellular vesicles (EVs) (Marchais et al., 2022; Raposo and Stoorvogel, 47 2013; Smith and Russell, 2022). These EVs carry a variety of biomolecules, including proteins, lipids, 48 messenger RNAs (mRNAs) and non-coding RNAs such as microRNAs (miRNAs) (Valadi et al., 49 2007). 50 EVs are membranous lipid bilayer vesicles secreted by most cells to deliver signals to target cells. 51 According to the latest instructions in the Minimal information for studies of extracellular vesicles 52 (MISEV) 2023 guidelines, EVs are classified as small EVs (diameter < 200 nm) or large EVs 53 (diameter > 200 nm) (Welsh et al., 2024). They originate either from the endosomal system, where 54 they are released as exosomes (30 to 200 nm) after maturation in multivesicular bodies, or from the 55 shedding of the plasma membrane as microvesicles or ectosomes (100 - 1000 nm). Follicular fluid 56 EVs (FF EVs) (< 200 nm) or cumulus cell EVs can be taken up by granulosa cells, by blastocyst or 57 oocytes through the zona pellucida (Da Silveira et al., 2017; Fiorentino et al., 2024; Matsuno et al., 58 2017; Pavani et al., 2018; Uzbekova et al., 2020). This uptake has been associated with improved 59 oocyte maturation and embryo early development (Azari-Dolatabad et al., 2025; Da Silveira et al., 60 2017, 2015; Lipinska et al., 2025; Schneberger et al., 2026). FF EVs can modulate apoptosis, 61 proliferation and steroidogenesis in bovine GC and human KGN cell line (Hung et al., 2017; Varik et 62

Therefore, EVs and their miRNA cargo could have 76 important implication for fertility, follicular development, and oocyte competence (Fiorentino et al., 77 2024; Santonocito et al., 2014), especially as they could mediate cellular response to environmental 78 stimuli. Discussion 453 Small EVs and miRNA are known to play a crucial role in intercellular communication between 454 ovarian follicular cells (Fazeli et al., 2025; Smith and Russell, 2022) and in follicle development 455 (Alexandri et al., 2020) respectively. In that context, our study aimed to highlight the endocrine 456 disruptor BPA and BPS mechanisms of action on ovine GC especially through EVs and their miRNA 457 content. A further objective was to decipher the potential role of miRNAs in ovine follicle 458 development, as we investigated miRNA profiles in FF EVs, granulosa cells and their conditioned 459 EVs (CM EVs). First, we confirmed BPA and BPS both disrupt ovine GC steroidogenesis after 48h in 460 vitro exposure. We also showed that EV size distribution was similar between FF EVs and 48h CM 461 EVs. No effect of BPA or BPS was observed on 48h CM EV size distribution and concentration. In 462 total, our current study identified 533 miRNA sequences including 129 newly identified sequences. In 463 GC, 354 microRNAs were detected while 110 identified from their EVs. No change in GC miRNA 464 expression was observed following 10 μM BPA exposure for 48h, while 10 μM BPS exposure 465 decreased significantly a cellular miRNA named oar-24b. The expression of 2 CM EV associated 466 miRNAs was altered by both BPA and BPS exposure, BPA also altering 2 additional CM EV 467 associated miRNAs. These modifications could potentially be implicated in the regulation of BPA or 468 BPS steroidogenic effect or in folliculogenesis. 469

Just like, the detection in EV samples of calnexin, an endoplasmic reticulum 657 membrane protein (Welsh et al., 2024), could indicate the presence of early endosomes which can 658 interact with these structures (Doyle and Wang, 2019). However, it should be noted that FF EVs are 659 heterogeneous in size, cargo composition, and biogenesis pathway (Fazeli et al., 2025), especially, 660 different subpopulations of FF EVs were identified in human (Neyroud et al., 2022) and bovine (Wang 661 et al., 2021). 662 663
