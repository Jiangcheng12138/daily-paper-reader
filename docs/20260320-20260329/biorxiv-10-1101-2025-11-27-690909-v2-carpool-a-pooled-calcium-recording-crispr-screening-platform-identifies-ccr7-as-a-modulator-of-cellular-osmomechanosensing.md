## 摘要
细胞必须通过将物理和化学信号转化为胞内信号，不断感知并响应环境变化。然而，由于信号事件的瞬时性以及测量分析的低通量，系统性发现控制这些感知过程的基因一直受到限制。在此，我们介绍了 CaRPOOL，这是一种整合了钙活性记录器 CaMPARI2 与 CRISPR 干扰 (CRISPRi) 的混合式高通量遗传筛选平台，能够稳定捕获瞬时钙信号，用于全基因组范围的功能筛选。以渗透压机械刺激为模型，我们证明了 CaMPARI2 的光转化能忠实地报告刺激依赖性钙响应，并支持基于混合荧光激活细胞分选 (FACS) 的筛选。一个针对膜相关基因的 CRISPRi 文库鉴定出了已知及此前未表征的机械转导调节因子，包括趋化因子受体 CCR7。机制分析表明，CCR7 通过 PIEZO1 依赖性的 Gs-cAMP-PKA 通路促进渗透压机械钙信号传导，从而确立了其作为机械响应型 GPCR 的地位。值得注意的是，渗透压应激上调了免疫细胞系中 CCR7 的表达并增强了机械响应性，提示其在免疫机械适应中发挥作用。总之，这些发现介绍了一个广泛适用的高通量发现控制动态信号响应基因的平台，并揭示了机械转导中 GPCR 与离子通道的跨通路对话机制，对免疫细胞的机械适应具有潜在意义。

## Abstract
Cells must continuously sense and respond to environmental changes by translating physical and chemical cues into intracellular signals. However, systematic discovery of genes governing these sensory processes has been limited by the transient nature of signaling events and the low throughput of measurement assays. Here, we present CaRPOOL, a pooled, high-throughput genetic screening platform that integrates the calcium-activity recorder CaMPARI2 with CRISPR interference (CRISPRi), enabling stable capture of transient calcium signals for genome-scale functional screening. Using osmomechanical stimulation as a model, we demonstrate that CaMPARI2 photoconversion faithfully reports stimulus-dependent calcium responses and supports pooled fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS)-based screening. A CRISPRi library targeting membrane-associated genes identified both known and previously uncharacterized regulators of mechanotransduction, including the chemokine receptor CCR7. Mechanistic analyses revealed that CCR7 promotes osmomechanical calcium signaling through a PIEZO1-dependent Gs-cAMP-PKA pathway, establishing it as a mechanically responsive GPCR. Notably, osmotic stress upregulated CCR7 expression in immune cell lines and enhanced mechanical responsiveness, suggesting a role in immunomechanical adaptation. Together, these findings introduce a broadly applicable platform for high-throughput discovery of genes controlling dynamic signaling responses and reveal a GPCR-ion channel crosstalk mechanism in mechanotransduction with potential implications for immune cell mechanoadaptation.
