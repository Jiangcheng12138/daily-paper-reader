Abstract 23 

Beige adipocytes that emerge during the peri-weaning period support sympathetic 24 

nervous system (SNS)-independent thermogenesis, yet the mechanisms governing this 25 

spontaneous beiging remain unclear. Here, by integrating transcriptomic profiling with 26 

adipocyte-targeted Ctnnb1 deletion in mice, we identify the canonical Wnt signaling as 27 

an endogenous brake on developmental beige thermogenesis. Peri-weaning inguinal fat 28 

from adipocyte Ctnnb1 knockout mice exhibits enhanced beige adipocyte biogenesis, 29 

with increased thermogenesis-related gene expression and mitochondrial oxidative 30 

capacity, which programs durable activation of adaptive thermogenesis and augmented 31 

whole-body energy expenditure into adulthood. Mechanistically, suppression of Wnt/β-32 

catenin signaling induces a non-canonical Wnt5a-Ca 2⁺-AMPK axis that promotes 33 

triglyceride lipolysis and subsequent PPAR-driven fatty acid oxidation, thereby fueling 34 

mitochondrial respiration. Genetic or pharmacological disruption of this axis blunts 35 

thermogenic responses induced by β-catenin inhibition in both murine and human 36 

subcutaneous adipocytes, indicating that Wnt5a-Ca 2⁺-AMPK axis is required for the 37 

cell-autonomous activation of beige fat. These results reveal Wnt/β-catenin signaling 38 

as a developmental constraint on beige adipocyte formation and suggest an SNS-39 

independent route to sustainably raise energy expenditure and improve metabolic health. 40

Introduction 41 

Adipose tissue plays a pivotal role in energy balance and systemic metabolic regulation, 42 

serving not only as the principal site for energy storage but also as a key contributor to 43 

thermogenesis 1,2 . Thermogenic adipocytes are specialized fat cells that dissipate 44 

chemical energy as heat and are consistently linked to improved glucose homeostasis, 45 

enhanced lipid clearance, and protection against obesity-related metabolic disorders 3,4 .46 

Two types of thermogenic adipocytes, known as brown and beige adipocytes, have been 47 

characterized by the expression of uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1), a key thermogenic 48 

marker 5,6 . Brown adipocytes, residing in brown adipose tissue (BAT), are largely 49 

constitutive throughout life, while beige adipocytes can be recruited within 50 

subcutaneous white adipose tissue (sWAT) in response to external cues such as 51 

environmental cold acclimation 5,7-9 . In adult mammals, beige adipocyte recruitment is 52 

predominantly driven by sympathetic nervous system (SNS)-mediated β-adrenergic 53 

receptor (β-AR) signaling, with intracellular regulators such as AMP-activated protein 54 

kinase (AMPK) and peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors (PPARs) modulating 55 

the thermogenic program 2,3,10-16 . However, this reliance on sustained SNS input limits 56 

the feasibility and durability of beige adipocyte activation as a therapeutic strategy. 57 

Moreover, pharmacological β3-AR agonists, although capable of stimulating beige fat, 58 

have been associated with increased cardiovascular risk 11,17-19 . Thus, alternative 59 

mechanisms that promote beige adipocyte formation and function independent of SNS 60 

activation are of considerable interest. 61 

Emerging evidence indicates that, in contrast to the adult situation, beige adipocyte 62 

biogenesis during development can occur independently of environmental temperature 63 

and sympathetic stimulation 20-26 . In mice, sWAT undergoes spontaneous beige 64 

adipogenesis during the peri-weaning period without cold exposure, and this process 65 

persists even under thermoneutral conditions or after sympathetic denervation 24 . The 66 

Ucp1 + beige adipocytes generated during this developmental window subsequently 67

In this study, we took advantage of the peri-weaning window of spontaneous beige 77

Results 98 

Peri-weaning iWAT adipocyte precursors display reduced Wnt/β-catenin 99 

signaling and higher thermogenic capacity upon differentiation 100 

Developmental beige adipocytes, which become thermogenic inactive in adulthood, 101 

have been reported to transiently and spontaneously arise in sWAT of young mice 102 

around the peri-weaning period under ambient-temperature conditions 21-26 . To 103 

delineate the temporal pattern of this phenomenon, we profiled thermogenic features of 104 

the inguinal white adipose tissue (iWAT), a major subcutaneous depot in mice, at room 105 

temperature across age. Analysis of wild-type male mice at 3, 4, 5, and 8 weeks of age 106 

revealed a pronounced peri-weaning peak in thermogenesis-related markers around 4 107 

weeks, followed by a progressive decline (Fig. 1a,b, Supplementary Fig. 1a). We next 108 

asked whether this developmental beiging is driven primarily by age-dependent 109 

systemic cues or by cell-autonomous properties of adipocyte precursors. To this end, 110 

we compared the thermogenetic capacity of adipocytes differentiated in vitro from the 111 

stromal vascular fraction (SVF) cells isolated from peri-weaning (4-week) and adult (8-112 

week) iWAT. Under the standard white fat pro-adipogenic induction, adipocytes 113 

derived from 4-week mice displayed smaller, multilocular lipid droplets and 114 

significantly higher levels of thermogenic genes and proteins than those from 8-week 115 

mice (Fig. 1c–e, Supplementary Fig. 1b). These observations suggest that peri-weaning 116 

adipocyte precursors are intrinsically biased towards a more thermogenic phenotype 117 

than their adult counterparts under induction. 118

Wnt/β-catenin signaling modulates developmental beiging 156 

Next, we asked whether reducing Wnt/β-catenin signaling specifically in adipocytes 157 

would be sufficient to modulate beiging activity in vivo. For this purpose, we generated 158 

adipocyte-specific Ctnnb1 -knockout mice upon compounding Adipoq Cre allele with 159 

floxed Ctnnb1 alleles ( Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox ), using Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/+ littermates 160 

as controls (Supplementary Fig. 2a). To evaluate recombination specificity, we 161 

separated adipose tissues from Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox and control mice into SVF and 162 

mature adipocyte fractions (Supplementary Fig. 2b). We found that, across iWAT, BAT, 163 

and eWAT, Ctnnb1 expression was efficiently suppressed in mature adipocytes but 164 

remained unchanged in SVF cells and in other tissues (Supplementary Fig. 2c,d). 165 

Consistently, β-catenin protein levels were markedly decreased in iWAT from 4-week-166 

old Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox mice, confirming effective Ctnnb1 deletion in adipocytes 167 

(Supplementary Fig. 2e). 168

control mice at 4 weeks of age. Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox mice displayed significantly 170 

lower body weight than littermate controls (Fig. 2a), and their iWAT depots appeared 171 

smaller and more reddish (Fig. 2b). Histological analysis of iWAT revealed that 172 

adipocytes from Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox mice, particularly in the posterior region 173 

relative to controls, exhibited a beige-like morphology with reduced lipid droplet size 174

Adipocyte β-catenin ablation selectively enhances thermogenesis in iWAT 213 

To determine whether enhanced thermogenesis in Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox iWAT could 214 

be explained by altered adipocyte differentiation, we examined adipogenesis in vivo 215

Reduced Wnt/β-catenin signaling engages a non-canonical Wnt/Ca 2+ -AMPK axis 269 

in developmental iWAT beiging 270 

To elucidate the molecular mechanisms by which β-catenin deletion enhances beige 271 

adipocyte thermogenesis during peri-weaning period, we ran bulk RNA-seq on iWAT 272 

from 4-week-old Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox and control mice. As expected, volcano plots 273 

revealed upregulation of thermogenesis-related genes in Adipoq Cre ;Ctnnb1 flox/flox iWAT 274 

(Supplementary Fig. 7a). Notably, functional enrichment analysis showed that the 275 

transcriptional changes were significantly enriched in pathways related to PPAR 276 

signaling, Ca 2+ signaling, and AMPK signaling (Fig. 3a), all of which have established 277 

roles in promoting oxidative metabolism in thermogenic adipocytes 12,15,16,37,38 . On the 278 

basis of these observations and given the well-known antagonistic relationship between 279 

the canonical Wnt and non-canonical Wnt/Ca 2+ signaling 39-44 , we reasoned that β-280 

catenin loss might shift signaling towards the non-canonical Wnt/Ca 2+ pathway that fits 281

Discussion 425 

Beige adipocytes have emerged as attractive targets for improving metabolic health, 426 

because their recruitment and activation increase energy expenditure and ameliorate 427 

obesity-associated metabolic dysfunction 4,6,8,15,51 . In adults, however, beige adipocyte 428 

activity is typically transient and heavily dependent on sympathetic stimulation, which 429 

limits the durability and translational potential of beige fat-based strategies 5,7-9 . By 430 

contrast, during the early postnatal period beige adipocytes arise in murine 431 

subcutaneous fat even in the absence of cold exposure, at a time when sympathetic 432 

innervation is not yet fully mature 20-26 . Previous studies has shown that beige adipocytes 433 

generated in this peri-weaning window can subsequently regress into a dormant state 434 

but retain the plasticity to be reactivated, thereby contributing to long-term thermogenic 435 

potential and improved metabolic outcomes in adulthood 20,24,27-29 . Nevertheless, it has 436 

remained unclear how developmental signals gate this spontaneous beiging and shape 437 

the later thermogenic capacity of subcutaneous fat. In this context, our current study 438 

identifies Wnt/β-catenin signaling as a developmental brake on beige adipocyte 439 

formation. Suppression of this pathway during the peri-weaning period selectively 440 

enhances adipocyte thermogenic activation in subcutaneous depots and imprints a 441 

higher thermogenic set point that persists into adult life. These findings position 442

developmental modulation of Wnt/β-catenin signaling as a mechanism that links early-443 

life beige adipogenesis to long-term thermogenic capacity. 444 

The canonical Wnt pathway is classically recognized as a negative regulator of 445 

adipogenesis 52-54 , and our findings extend this view by showing that Wnt/β-catenin also 446 

constrains adipocyte thermogenic potential. In adipocyte-specific β-catenin loss-of-447 

function models, lowering Wnt/β-catenin activity enhances beige-associated oxidative 448 

metabolism in iWAT while maintaining adipocyte differentiation markers and systemic 449 

adipokine profiles. At the level of lipid metabolism, Wnt/β-catenin inhibition 450 

preferentially augments triglyceride hydrolysis and fatty acid oxidative pathways 451 

without overtly reducing de novo lipogenesis, indicating a bias in lipid handling 452 

towards fuel mobilization and oxidation to support heat production, rather than 453 

thermogenesis arising as a secondary consequence of global defects in lipid synthesis 454 

or adipose storage described in other thermogenic models 55-57 . This pattern is 455 

compatible with a prior report showing that the transcriptional regulator BCL6 is 456 

required for the establishment of a beige adipocyte program around the peri-weaning 457 

period, partly by supporting fatty acid oxidation and mitochondrial uncoupling 24 , and 458 

that BCL6 can antagonize Wnt signaling 58-60 , pointing to potential cross-regulatory 459 

interactions between these pathways. In line with this, previous studies have reported 460 

that deletion of Ctnnb1 in adipocytes alleviates long-term high-fat diet-induced 461 

metabolic dysfunction, with reduced adipose tissue inflammation and improved insulin 462 

sensitivity, features that resemble the systemic benefits typically attributed to increased 463 

beige fat activity 61 . Although most mechanistic insights derive from mouse models, 464 

human genetic and expression data also support an association between aberrant Wnt 465 

activation and adverse metabolic remodeling. For example, gain-of-function alterations 466 

in RSPO1-LGR4 pathway in obese individuals have been associated with enhanced 467 

Wnt/β-catenin activity and impaired adipose tissue plasticity 62 . Consistent with a 468 

conserved role in humans, we observed that pharmacological inhibition of Wnt/β-469

Bulk RNA-Seq 694 

For RNA sequencing of tissue samples, freshly isolated iWAT was immediately snap-695 

frozen in liquid nitrogen for 30 minutes. For cellular sequencing, SVF from 4-week-696 

old and 8-week-old wild-type mice were differentiated into mature adipocytes in vitro, 697

for 30 minutes. All samples were frozen at -80 °C before RNA extraction and analysis. 699 

The following steps were conducted by Majorbio Bio-Pharm Technology Co., Ltd. 700 

(Shanghai, China). Total RNA was isolated from the samples, followed by genomic 701 

DNA removal using DNase I (Takara, Japan). RNA-seq libraries were generated from 702 

1 μg of total RNA using the TruSeq™ RNA Sample Preparation Kit (Illumina, San 703 

Diego, CA, USA) according to the manufacturer’s instructions. In brief, polyadenylated 704 

mRNA was enriched, fragmented, and reverse-transcribed into double-stranded cDNA 705 

using the SuperScript Double-Stranded cDNA Synthesis Kit (Invitrogen, CA) with 706 

random hexamer primers (Illumina). The resulting cDNA fragments underwent end-707 

repair, phosphorylation, and 3′-end adenylation. After fragment size selection (~300 708 

bp), sequencing adapters were ligated, and the libraries were subjected to paired-end 709 

sequencing (2 × 150 bp) on an Illumina HiSeq X Ten or NovaSeq 6000 platform. All 710 

bioinformatics analyses were conducted on the Majorbio Cloud Platform 711 

(www.majorbio.com). 712
