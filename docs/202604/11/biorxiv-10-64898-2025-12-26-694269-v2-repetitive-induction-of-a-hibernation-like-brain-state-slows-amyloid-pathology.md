## 摘要
阿尔茨海默病 (AD) 的特征是在认知障碍发生前淀粉样蛋白病理的逐渐积累，然而改变疾病进展的有效策略仍然有限。在本研究中，我们测试了诱导类冬眠生理状态是否能减缓 AD 小鼠模型中的淀粉样蛋白病理。为了获得持续的效果，我们开发了一种重复诱导低代谢/低温状态的方案，并发现其以时长依赖的方式显著减缓了斑块积累。这种干预还减轻了神经炎症以及与斑块相关的营养不良性神经突的形成。值得注意的是，这种方法并不针对特定的分子通路，而是调节全局脑状态。我们的研究结果为基于脑状态的治疗范式提供了概念验证，旨在改变 AD 的疾病进展。

## Abstract
Alzheimers disease (AD) is characterized by the gradual accumulation of amyloid pathology before the onset of cognitive impairment, yet effective strategies to alter disease progression remain limited. Here, we tested whether induction of a hibernation-like physiological state can slow amyloid pathology in an AD mouse model. To achieve sustained effects, we developed a protocol for repeated induction of a hypometabolic/hypothermic state and found that it significantly slowed plaque accumulation in a duration-dependent manner. This intervention also attenuated neuroinflammation and the formation of dystrophic neurites associated with plaques. Notably, this approach does not target specific molecular pathways but instead modulates global brain state. Our findings establish a proof-of-concept for a brain-state- based therapeutic paradigm for modifying disease progression in AD.
