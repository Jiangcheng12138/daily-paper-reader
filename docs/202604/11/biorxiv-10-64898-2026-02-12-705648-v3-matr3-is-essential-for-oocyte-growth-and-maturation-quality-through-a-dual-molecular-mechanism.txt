Abstract: 22 

The molecular mechanisms governing mRNA accumulation during oocyte growth, essential for 23 developmental competence, remain poorly understood. This study investigates the role of Matrin-3 24 (MATR3), a highly expressed RNA-binding protein in growing oocytes (GOs), using oocyte-specific 25 knockout mouse models and human oocyte maturation arrest (OMA) samples. The results showed that 26 MATR3 was more abundant in GOs than fully-grown oocytes (FGOs), highly expressed in the nucleus 27 of non-surrounded nucleolus (NSN) oocytes, and exited the nucleus during the NSN-to-surrounded 28 nucleolus (SN) transition. In OMA patients, MATR3 nuclear localization was missed, with smaller 29 oocytes than FGOs. Further, Matr3 deletion in mouse GOs caused restricted oocyte growth, global 30 transcription disorders, follicle development failure, blocked GO-granulosa cell communication (via 31 reduced Gdf9 and Radixin expression), and infertility. Mechanistically, MATR3 regulated transcription 32 by recruiting H3K9me2-demethylating lysine-specific demethylase 3B or binding target gene promoters, 33 like Radixin . These findings reveal a critical role of MATR3 in orchestrating transcription and paracrine 34 signaling during oogenesis and suggest its potential as a diagnostic and therapeutic target for OMA. 35 

Key words : Matrin-3; growing oocyte; epigenetic modification; follicle development; fertility 36

Introduction: 38 

The growth of oocytes refers to the process in which growing oocytes (GOs) develop into fully 39 grown oocytes (FGOs). Oocyte growth involves an increase in volume, as well as the accumulation of 40 metabolic molecules, transcripts and proteins. In vivo , FGO determines the developmental potential of 41 early embryo. A high qualified FGO derives from the GO in a growing follicle, which may experience 42 dynamic gene transcription as well as finetuned orchestration between the GO and the surrounding 43 granulosa cells. In the clinic, besides genetic caused female infertility, uncovering the etiologies 44 especially epigenetic causative reasons is equally pivotal for improving the oocyte maturation quality in 45 

vitro (Ren et al. 2017; Wang et al. 2019; He et al. 2021; Gao et al. 2024; Zhu et al. 2024). Patients who 46 suffer from oocyte maturation arrest (OMA) are unable to retrieve mature oocytes during their repetitive 47 

in vitro fertilization (IVF) and intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) cycles (Liu et al. 2018). In the 48 past, the importance of oocyte specific RNA binding proteins (RBPs) like MARF1, LSM14B, and MTR4 49 in improving oocyte development as well as their relationship to OMA have been confirmed (Su et al. 50 2012; Su et al. 2021; Wu et al. 2025). However, it remains unclear how other RBPs coordinates epigenetic 51 molecules contributing to transcribe and preserve massive mRNAs to direct the timely growth of GOs 52 and growing follicles. 53 

Multiple RBPs have been proven to closely correlate to OMA. The PATL2 protein is known to 54 regulate the homeostasis of maternal mRNAs, while a whole exome sequencing study has identified four 55 recessive variants of the PATL2 gene in three OMA families (Wang et al. 2024a). Also, the compound 56 heterozygous ZFP36L2 mutations correlates to OMA (Wan et al. 2024). ZFP36L2 is crucial for 57 regulating the degradation of maternal mRNAs and the maturation of mouse oocytes (Chousal et al. 58 2018). Despite of these, it is unclear if other RBPs contribute to female fertility as well. This study 59 focuses on a conserved RBP Matrin-3 (MATR3) who contains two zinc-finger domains to specifically 60 bind to DNA sequences rich in GC (Zeitz et al. 2009). The two RNA recognition motifs of MATR3 61 participates in the regulation of alternative splicing by specifically binding to mRNA sequences rich in 62 AU (Zeitz et al. 2009; Iradi et al. 2018). Moreover, MATR3 is rich in nuclear localization sequences, 63 which enabling it to localize in the nucleus. One of the characteristic functions of MATR3 is that it 64 recruits other RBPs while binding to the target sequences (Pollini et al. 2021). Importantly, as a powerful 65 RBPs, the abnormal aggregation and pathological mutations of MATR3 may interfere with normal 66 cellular functions, including RNA stability and transport (Coelho et al. 2015; Cha et al. 2021). 67 Unfortunately, whether and how MATR3 correlates to GO to FGO development and female fertility 68 remains unknown. 69 

The growth of growing follicles as well as oocyte maturation depends strictly on endocrine and 70 paracrine regulations in vivo . Studies including ours have shown that during cyclic recruitment of 71 growing follicles in each estrus cycle, paracrine factor like CNP (C-Type Natriuretic Peptide) coordinates 72 gonadotropins to timely control FGO meiosis arrest, meiosis resumption and ovulation (Zhang et al. 73 2010). However, throughout the progress from GO to FGO, cumulative studies have highlighted the 74 critic roles of oocyte-secreted factors (OSFs) in supporting follicular somatic cell proliferation, 75 metabolism and GO development (Hanrahan et al. 2004; Su et al. 2008; Nicol et al. 2009; Silva et al. 76 2011; Gao et al. 2024). Of which, TGF β superfamily members growth differentiation factor 9 (GDF9) 77 and bone morphogenic protein 15 (BMP15) are the most studied ones (Dong et al. 1996; Dube et al. 78 1998). Within a growing follicle, GDF9 functions by regulating the cuboidal transformation and 79 promoting proliferation of granulosa cells, which has been repetitively demonstrated by studies including 80 ours (Carabatsos et al. 1998; Spicer et al. 2008; Gao et al. 2024). BMP15, a highly homologous to GDF9, 81

Most recently, we 83 have proved that the major function of oocyte-derived mushroom-like microvilli (Oo-Mvi) relates to the 84 release of OSFs timely and orderly. Results of this study showed that MATR3 is highly expressed in the GOs of mice, pigs, and humans, 89 indicating a conserved and essential function. Results: 95 

1. MATR3 expression in oocyte of growing follicle is required for female fertility. 96 

To investigate the potential function of MATR3 in female reproduction, we examined its location 97 and expression pattern during folliculogenesis in multiple mammals. The results showed that MATR3 98 was generally expressed in the nucleus of oocytes and somatic cells of mice (Fig.1A, Fig.S1A). Specially, 99 the level of MATR3 in GOs remained at relatively higher levels than those found in the FGOs as well as 100 stages after maturation (Fig.1B, C), implying that MATR3 may be more important for supporting mouse 101 GO growth. Interestingly, compared to the normal group in which the expression pattern of MATR3 is 102 similar to that of mouse, we noticed a decrease in MATR3 protein level in FGO (NSN) from a patient 103 with OMA, which has not been reported before (Fig.1D). This finding suggests that MATR3 may be one 104 of a candidate responsible for OMA. Similarly, we also detected the expression pattern of MATR3 in the 105 ovaries of porcine, which is highly consistent with that in mice and humans, indicating that the 106 physiological function of MATR3 may be highly conserved among species (Fig.S1B-D). 107 

To explore the effect of MATR3 on GO growth, the authors firstly established an oocyte knockdown 108 model of early growing follicles in mouse. This model enables the development of secondary follicles 109 (SFs) into antral follicles (AFs) and, successful ovulation after gonadotropins hormone supplementation 110 (Fig.S2A). After injecting Matr3 siRNA into the oocyte of SF with a diameter of 150 μm in the model, 111 these follicles were in vitro cultured for 5 - 7 days (Fig.S2B-D). Photographs were taken daily to record 112 the follicular development status. On the fifth day, oocytes and granulosa cells were collected 113 respectively to detect the extrusion rate of the first polar body of oocytes and multiple functional markers 114 of granulosa cells. The results showed that after knocking down Matr3 in the oocytes of SFs, follicular 115 development was hindered. Specifically, when cultured in vitro for five days, the SFs in the negative 116 control group (NC) could develop into AFs, while the proportion of AFs in the knockdown group (si-117 

Matr3 ) was significantly reduced (Fig.1E, H 64.87±1.531% vs 27.27±5.613%). Consistently, the 118 extrusion rate of the first polar body of oocytes was significantly reduced (Fig.1I 81.83±3.048% vs 119 36.35±3.380%). Meanwhile, the protein level of FOXL2 in granulosa cells decreased, the protein level 120 of the proliferation marker PCNA decreased, and the protein level of the hormone-responsive receptor 121 IGFIR decreased as well (Fig.S2E). 122 

Based on the above in vitro experiments, we utilized a novel mouse model with specific MATR3 123 deficiency in oocytes to explore the MATR3 function. Specifically, Matr3 was conditionally knocked 124 out after 3 dpp using Gdf9 -Cre mice to generate Matr3 flox/flox ; Gdf9 -Cre (cKO) mice and to evaluate its 125 specific effect in oocytes (Fig.S3A). The knockout efficiency of cKO mice was examined by 126 immunofluorescence (Fig.1F, Fig.S3B). Notably, a fertility test showed that cKO mice were infertile 127 during the 6-month mating process (Fig.1G). Thus, MATR3 in oocytes is crucial for the growth of 128 follicles and the maintenance of fertility in mice. 129

2. MATR3 is indispensable for supporting GO growth. 130 

To clarify how MATR3 deficiency hindered mouse fertility, we injected human chorionic 131 gonadotropin (hCG) after intraperitoneal injection of pregnant mare serum gonadotropin (PMSG) for 48 132 h to the mouse at postnatal day 23 (PD23), and isolated the ovaries and oocytes after treated for 13 h 133 (H13). We evaluated the number of follicles at all developmental stages in the ovaries of H13. The results 134 showed that there were no corpora lutea (CL) in the ovaries of cKO mice (Fig.2A, B). Consistently, hCG 135 failed to induce ovulation in cKO mice (Fig.2C, D). Meanwhile, both the number of AFs (Fig.2B) and 136 the ratio of AFs/SFs (Fig.2L) were significantly lower in cKO mice than in the control (Ctrl). GOs from 137 PD14 cKO mice had normal size and morphology (Fig.S3C, D). While, FGOs from PD23 cKO mice had 138 smaller sizes than Ctrl oocytes (Fig.2E, F) and the ratio of SN-type oocytes in cKO was significantly 139 lower than that in the Ctrl group (Fig.2G). During in vitro maturation, the proportion of oocytes from 140 PD23 cKO mice developing to metaphase II stage was significantly reduced (Fig.2E, H 54.9±2.08% vs 141 9.57±1.11%). 142 

To investigate the inner mechanisms of female infertility in cKO mice, we firstly detected the 143 development of cKO ovaries at different ages (Fig.2I, J). At PD7, a similar morphology of ovaries was 144 found in cKO and Ctrl mice, showing that oocyte deletion of Matr3 does not affect the formation or 145 survival of dormant primordial follicles (Fig.S3E). However, the total follicles began to lost at PD14, 146 and a significant difference began to appear at PD23. This was reinforced by the data of available follicles 147 within the ovaries of mice at PD35 (Fig.2I, J). Further statistical results showed that the development of 148 SFs was slowed down from PD7 to PD23, and the conversion rate to AFs was significantly reduced at 149 PD23 (Fig.2M). Consistently, immunofluorescence staining showed that the numbers of Ki67-positive 150 (Fig.2K, Fig.S9) in cKO mice were lower than those found in the Ctrl (73.55±13.29% vs 24.65±7.80%). 151 

In summary, the deletion of Matr3 lead to impaired growth of oocytes. Oocyte with impaired growth 152 induced a follicle development disorder in a time-dependent manner attributed to the arrested transition 153 of secondary-to-antral follicles. 154

3. MATR3 governed global transcription in GO is pivotal for the growth of follicle. 155 

To investigate the differences of transcription levels in oocytes between the GOs and FGOs, oocytes 156 were isolated based on their diameters. During the time, 5-ethynyl uridine (EU) was used as probes to 157 measure the levels of newly synthesized mRNA in cells. The results indicated a potential role for MATR3 158 in the maintenance of high transcriptional level of GOs, which showed that he expression of MATR3 159 was consistent with the transcriptional activity of oocytes (Fig.S4). 160 

To identify the genes regulated by MATR3, the NC and si-Matr3 oocytes were used for single-cell 161 RNA-sequencing (scRNA-seq). The results of PC clustering analysis and heat map showed good data 162 parallelism (Fig.S5). The results showed that 1552 genes were upregulated and 1155 genes 163 downregulated, respecti vely (|Foldchange|≥2, Pvalue<0.05, Fig.3A). Gene ontology (GO) enrichment 164 analysis showed that the DEGs were enriched in the terms related to fibrillar centers, chromatin 165 organization, and nucleotide binding (Fig.3B). 166 

Subsequently, we performed GO analysis on the down-regulated differentially expressed genes in 167 oocytes. We found that approximately two-thirds of the top 10 entries were related to transcription 168 (Fig.3C). Based on this, we performed EU staining on oocytes to detect the level of their newly 169 synthesized mRNA. The results showed that, after Matr3 knockdown, the transcriptional activity of GOs 170 was significantly reduced (Fig.3D, E). The relative mRNA level of the transcription initiation factor 171 

Tbpl2 (Yu et al. 2020), which is specifically expressed in oocytes, was significantly decreased (Fig.3F). 172 Moreover, the relative protein level of RNAPII fell significantly (Fig.3G). 173 

Through GO enrichment analysis of scRNA-seq, it showed that the differentially expressed genes 174 were significantly enriched in the "chromatin organization" term, which was highly related to 175 transcription. To prove that MATR3 affects the transcriptional activity of oocytes by regulating the 176 chromatin state, we used immunofluorescence to detect the methylation levels of multiple histones, 177 aiming to find the key molecules responsible for the downregulation of oocyte transcriptional activity. 178 After knocking down Matr3 in GOs, the protein levels of H3K9me1 and H3K9me3, which were related 179 to gene transcriptional repression, did not change significantly. Contrarily, the protein levels of 180 H3K27me3 and H3K4me3 even decreased significantly (Fig.3H, Fig.S6). The above results indicate that 181 H3K9me1, H3K9me3, H3K27me3, and H3K4me3 do not play a dominant role in the regulation of GO 182 transcriptional levels mediated by MATR3. Excitingly, the protein level of H3K9me2, which is related 183 to gene transcriptional repression, increased extremely high. The level of H3K9me2, which marks 184 transcriptional repression, was significantly increased either (Fig.3I, J). Based on the above results, 185 MATR3 in GO maintains a high transcriptional level by eliminating the inhibitory modification 186 H3K9me2. 187

4. MATR3 governs follicle growth partially through regulating GDF9 production. 188 

During the follicle growth process, a high transcriptional level in GO is a prominent physiological 189 feature. The substantial accumulation of maternal mRNAs, proteins, and metabolites in the cytoplasm of 190 GO is a crucial factor contributing to the gradual increase in oocyte volume. It is also essential for the 191 development of early-growing follicles. Among these, GDF9 plays a vital role in regulating the 192 development of early-growing follicles. Specifically, we examined the key factors related to the GDF9 193 signaling pathway to verify the paracrine signals. 194 

The results showed that the Gdf9 level was decreased significantly in cKO ovaries, which was 195 confirmed by the mRNA and protein expression assays (Fig.4A-C). When Matr3 in oocytes was knocked 196 down in vitro , the level of GDF9 was significantly decreased consistently (Fig.4E, F). Generally, the 197 GDF9 signal is transmitted to SMAD3 in the cytoplasm of granulosa cells and enters the nucleus through 198 phosphorylation, thereby promoting the differentiation of granulosa cells. Compared with the Ctrl group, 199 the level of phosphorylated SMAD3 protein was decreased in cKO ovaries (Fig.4B). Although there was 200 no significant difference in SMAD3 protein levels, most of SMAD3 molecules could not be 201 phosphorylated and was therefore located in the cytoplasm rather than inside the nucleus of the granulosa 202 cells (Fig.4D). 203 

To prove that GDF9 is a key downstream molecule whose production is affected by MATR3, we 204 conducted a rescue experiment on growing follicles with Matr3 -knocked-down oocytes by adding pure 205 GDF9 in vitro . We divided the in vitro cultured follicles into four groups: the negative control group, the 206 

Matr3 -knocked-down group, the group with GDF9 added after Matr3 knockdown, and the group with 207 GDF9 added alone. Consequently, after knocking down Matr3 in the oocytes of SFs and culturing them 208 

in vitro for 5 days, we photographed and recorded the follicle diameters, and counted the extrusion rate 209 of the first polar body of oocytes (Fig.4G). The results showed that the follicle diameter of the group 210 with GDF9 added after Matr3 knockdown significantly rescued that of the group with Matr3 knocked 211 down alone. And, there was no significant difference compared with the control group. The extrusion 212 rate of the first polar body in the group with GDF9 added after Matr3 knockdown also significantly 213 rescued that of the group with Matr3 knocked down alone, but there was still a significant difference 214 compared with the extrusion rate of the first polar body in the NC group (Fig.4H). These results indicate 215 that GDF9 can partially rescue the follicular development arrest caused by Matr3 knockdown and is a 216 key functional molecule in MATR3 - regulated follicular development. 217

5. MATR3 promotes the expression of Gdf9 by recruiting KDM3B. 218 

To explore how MATR3 is involved in the regulatory synthesis of GDF9, we detected the level of 219 lysine (K)-specific demethylase 3B (KDM3B), which directly regulates H3K9me2. Under physiological 220 conditions, KDM3B is localized in the cytoplasm and nucleus of oocytes, and is highly expressed in GO 221 (Fig.S7). This study showed that KDM3B was downregulated as the transcriptional activity of oocytes 222 decreases (Fig.5A, B). To further clarify how MATR3 regulates KDM3B, previous literature indicates 223 that RBPs can recruit KDM3B to regulate the level of H3K9me2 (Kim et al. 2012; Li et al. 2020). Then, 224 to prove this hypothesis, we detected the interaction between MATR3 and KDM3B in the HEK293T-cell 225 line using co-immunoprecipitation (CO-IP). The results showed that there was an interaction between 226 MATR3 and KDM3B (Fig.5C, D). To identify the specific domains of MATR3 for recruiting KDM3B, 227 we firstly constructed the MATR 3-EGFP fusion protein. The Western blotting results demonstrated the 228 successful construction of the vector after we transfected the HEK293T -cell line with the constructed 229 plasmids (Fig.S8A, B). 230 

We injected low-concentration of Matr3-Egfp mRNA into either GOs or FGOs and performed live-231 cell fluorescence imaging. The results showed that in GOs and NSN-type FGOs, MATR3 was localized 232 in the nucleus, while in SN-type oocytes, MATR3 presented as aggregated punctate structures and 233 diffuses into the cytoplasm as meiosis resumes (Fig.5F). In addition, we injected the truncated form into 234 germinal vesicle (GV) oocytes to further detect the localization of MATR3 (Fig.S8C). The CO - IP results 235 indicated that MATR3 interacts with KDM3B through the 2nd RNA recognition motif (RRM2) and the 236 4th nuclear localization signal (pNLS4) (Fig.5E). Furthermore, we injected in vitro -transcribed mRNAs 237 (egfp , Matr3-egfp , Δrrm2-egfp , Δpnls4-egfp ) into GOs and carried out living-cell fluorescence imaging. 238 The results showed that the functional truncation mutants of the RRM2 motif and the pNLS4 motif both 239 change the spatiotemporal specific localization of MATR3 (Fig.5G). 240 

To determine the domain that affects the physiological function of MATR3 in oocytes, we firstly 241 knocked down the endogenous level of MATR3 in GOs and then injected Matr3-egfp , Δrrm2-egfp , and 242 

Δpnls4-egfp mRNAs respectively so as to detect the protein level of GDF9. The results showed that after 243 deleting RRM2, there was no significant change in the GDF9 protein level, while after deleting the 244 pNLS4 domain, the GDF9 level decreased significantly (Fig.5H, I). Together, these results suggested 245 that MATR3 is localized in the nucleus of GOs through the pNLS4 domain, thereby recruiting KDM3B 246 and promoting the expression of GDF9. 247

6. MATR3 promotes the expression of Rdx to facilitate GDF9 secretion. 248 

As a key OSF of oocyte paracrine, both the transcription and secretion of GDF9 is regulated by 249 MATR3. Notably, our immunofluorescence results showed that the structure of Oo-Mvi in cKO mice 250 was damaged (Fig.6A B), which may impair the communication of oocyte and the surrounding cumulus 251 cells. 252 

To test if MATR3 in GO affects oocyte-somatic cell communication by binding to the DNA 253 sequences pivotal for oocyte growth, the low-input affinity cleavage enrichment sequencing (LACE-seq) 254 assay was performed by collecting growing oocytes from 10 to 12-day-old mice. The sequencing results 255 showed that MATR3 directly binds to the coding sequence (CDS) region and 3' untranslated region 256 (3'UTR) of mRNAs (Fig.6C). Motif analysis indicated that MATR3 tends to bind to regions rich in GA 257 (Fig.6D). The results of GO enrichment analysis showed that the genes bound by MATR3 were 258 significantly enriched in biological processes such as nucleocytoplasmic protein shuttling, chromatin 259 assembly, and RNA splicing (Fig.6F). 260 

Later, a joint analysis of LACE-seq and scRNA-seq was performed to further confirm our 261 assumption that MATR3 is very important for oocyte-cumulus cells communication. The results showed 262 that among the genes directly bound by MATR3, 84 genes such as Son , Ehmt1 , Ran , Igf2bp2 , Ccnb1 ,263 and Rdx simultaneously showed a significant decrease while 109 genes simultaneously showed a 264 significant increase in the oocytes of the knockdown group (Fig.6E, G). Of which, RDX is a specific Oo-265 Mvi-related protein in oocytes that contributes to the formation of Oo-Mvi in oocyte development. The 266 sequencing data were visualized by IGV. The results showed that MATR3 directly binds to Rdx mRNA 267 (Fig.6H). Fifty of the ovaries of 10 to 12-day-old-mice were collected for RNA immunoprecipitation 268 (RIP). Afterward, RIP was used to verify the direct binding of MATR3 to Rdx mRNA in the ovaries. 269 Consistent with the sequencing results, MATR3 directly binds to Rdx mRNA (Fig.6I, J). Furthermore, 270 the Rdx mRNA level in the GOs of the cKO group was significantly decreased (Fig.6K), respectively, as 271 compared with the mice in the Ctrl group. In sum, the above results indicates that in GOs, MATR3 272 directly binds to Rdx mRNA and regulates its level, thereby participating in the GDF9 secretion through 273 Oo-Mvi. 274

Discussion 275 

This study shows that the spatiotemporal-specific localization of MATR3 in GO is required for 276 female fertility. MATR3 in GO not only affects the transcription of specific genes but influences the 277 overall chromatin organizational conformation. Further, in the condition that Matr3 is deleted in vivo or 278 is silenced in vitro , seldom of growing follicles could develop into antral follicles. Consequently, the 279 efficiency of superovulation is markedly downregulated, indicating a poor response to gonadotropins in 280 cKO mice. In vitro , GDF9 partially rescued the phenotype of antral follicle failure as well as oocyte 281 maturation caused by si-Matr3 implying that GDF9 is one of the downstream molecules of MATR3. 282 Furthermore, reduced GDF9 and RDX productions induced by MATR3 missing impairs the mutual 283 communication between GO and surrounding granulosa cells. In agree with the existed reports, this is 284 one of typical examples to emphasize the importance of OSFs on granulosa cell proliferation as well as 285 GO growth (Gilchrist et al. 2008). We further demonstrated that MATR3 may exert its action by either 286 coordinating KDM3B directed histone methylation, chromosome structure loosing and improving gene 287 transcription through directly binding to the promoters of targeted genes. Since the protein expression 288 pattern of MATR3 in oocytes of human, porcine and mice are similar to each other, MATR3 is a 289 conservative key regulator of GO growth and follicle development among mammals (Fig.7). Therefore, 290 MATR3 is a key regulator of oocyte growth and follicular development. 291 

During follicle growth, the high transcriptional activity in GO is a prominent physiological feature 292 of oocytes. The uniqueness of GOs lies in their high transcriptional activity, if compared with oocytes at 293 other developmental stages. When primordial follicles are activated, oocytes in the follicles are 294 transformed from dormant state to the growing state. At this time, a large number of mRNAs start to be 295 transcribed within the oocytes. As GO progress to FGO, transcriptional silencing occurs, which persists 296 until the 2-cells stage. This indicates that the synthesis and accumulation of mRNAs for subsequent 297 oocyte maturation, fertilization, and early embryo development mainly occur in the GO. Consequently, 298 the large amounts of maternal mRNAs, proteins, and metabolites accumulated in the cytoplasm of FGO 299 are important reasons for the gradual increase in oocyte volume, and are also essential for the oocytes to 300 acquire the abilities of meiosis, fertilization, and embryo development (Su et al. 2021). Therefore, the 301 high transcriptional level of mRNAs during the growth process of GOs is of particular importance. 302 

MATR3 could also be one of candidate causative RBPs contributing to OMA in females. Recently, 303 mutations of some RBPs pivotal for maternal mRNA homeostasis have been linked to the consequence 304 of OMA, including PATL2 (Hu et al. 2024), PABPC1L (Wang et al. 2023) and ZFP36L2 (Wan et al. 305 2024). Here, the localization of MATR3 changes in oocytes of OMA patient as well. Specifically, 306 MATR3 is highly expressed in the nucleus of NSN oocytes and exits the nucleus when the nuclear type 307 transforms to SN in healthy people. However, in an OMA oocyte that remained at GV stage after ICSI, 308 the nuclear localization of MATR3 disappeared. Moreover, the diameter of this oocyte was smaller than 309 that of normal FGO. Furthermore, we noticed that deletion of MATR3 in mouse GOs resulted in 310 significant chromosome configuration disorders by that the percentage of SN versus NSN FGOs was 311 reversed in cKO mice. These findings imply that MATR3 is pivotal for chromosome configuration. In 312 agree with existed studies explaining that the SN chromatin configuration correlates with transcriptional 313 silence and full oocyte developmental competence (Bouniol-Baly et al. 1999; Zuccotti et al. 2005), this 314 study also found that multiple oocyte-specific genes and genes involved in oocyte/granulosa cell 315 communication are direct targets of MATR3, including Ehmt1 (Demond et al. 2023), Ran (Dehapiot and 316 Halet 2013), Igf2bp2 (Li et al. 2021), Ccnb1 (Wang et al. 2024b) (Fig.S9), Rdx (Zhang et al. 2021), Zar1 317 (Rong et al. 2019) and Dnmt1 (Shelby et al. 2023). This study provided additional evidences to highlight the crucial roles of OSFs in supporting 325 follicular development at different stages (Dong et al. 1996). Studies including ours have shown that 326 GDF9 is indispensable for granulosa cells proliferation during the transition from primary follicles to 327 secondary follicles (Dong et al. 1996; Ackert et al. 2001; Gao et al. 2024). In the differentiated cumulus 328 cells of antral follicles, GDF9 inhibits the synthesis of estrogen and the expression of LH receptors, 329 thereby maintaining the differentiated state of cumulus cells. Meanwhile, it promotes the expression of 330 NPR2 in cumulus cells, upregulates the levels of cGMP and cAMP in oocyte, and contributes to the 331 oocyte meiotic arrest (Hinckley et al. 2005; Norris et al. 2009; Zhang et al. 2010). In vitro , GDF9 332 promotes the synthesis of new transzonal projections in the cumulus-oocyte complex (El-Hayek et al. 333 2018). Besides the powerful roles in regulating multiple gene expression responsible for oocyte 334 development and oocyte/granulosa cell mutual communication, this study specifically emphasized the 335 importance of MATR3 controlled production and secretion of GDF9 on antral follicle formation as well 336 as oocyte maturation with the assistance of RDX for establishing profound Oo-Mvi. This is one of typical 337 examples to emphasize the importance of OSFs on granulosa cell proliferation, GO growth as well as 338 antral follicle formation, which are all pivotal for supporting high qualified oocyte development. This 339 study highlights the importance of a sustaining feedback regulation between the oocyte and the 340 surrounding somatic cells on GO growth and follicle development. 341 

Previous studies reported that GOs could be divided into three stages based on the diameter, namely 342 GO1 (30 - 45 μm), GO2 (50 - 55 μm), and GO3 (60 - 65 μm) (Gu et al. 2019). According to our results, 343 there is no significant difference in the abundance of transcripts and chromatin accessibility among the 344 three states of GO, but the DNA methylation levels vary significantly. After knocking down Matr3 in 345 GO2, the levels of H3K4me3 and H3K27me3 decreased significantly, which is contrary to the increasing 346 trend of H3K4me3 and H3K27me3 levels with oocyte growth depicted in the DNA methylation map. 347 The results proves that MATR3 is a positive regulatory on GO growth. During the GO growth, the levels 348 of classical histone methylation modifications H3K4me3 and H3K9me3 increase and reach their peaks 349 in FGO (Kageyama et al. 2007). Here, the levels of H3K9me1 and H3K9me3 linked to gene 350 transcriptional repression were unchanged while H3K27me3 and H3K4me3 decreased significantly, 351 indicating that classical histone methylations do not play dominant roles in MATR3-mediated 352 transcription in GO. Instead, the level of H3K9me2, which is related to gene transcriptional repression, 353 increased significantly. Consistently, the level of KDM3B who directly regulates H3K9me2 decreased 354 significantly (Kim et al. 2012). So, H3K9me2 is crucial for MATR3-mediated high transcriptional levels 355 in GO. Specifically, the pNLS4 domain of MATR3 recruits KDM3B, and regulate the transcriptional 356 synthesis of GDF9 by removing the inhibitory H3K9me2 mark. Besides its coorperation with KDM3B 357 to regulate gene transcription ability, MATR3 may directly bind to the promoters of target genes like Rdx 358 to regulates transcription. 359
