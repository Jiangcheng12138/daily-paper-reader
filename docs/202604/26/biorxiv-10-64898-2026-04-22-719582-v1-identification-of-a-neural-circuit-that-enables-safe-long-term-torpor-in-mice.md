## 摘要
休眠（Torpor）是一种受调节的低温和低代谢状态，是某些哺乳动物的关键生存策略。先前的研究确立了神经元控制在启动急性休眠中的主导地位。然而，神经调节是否能安全地长期维持休眠，以及具体的调控通路，仍是限制其转化潜力的未解挑战。在此，我们在小鼠中鉴定出一群表达 General control nonderepressible 2 的独特视前区神经元群体（简称“G 神经元”），它们对于诱导休眠是必要且充分的。令人惊叹的是，持续且选择性地激活 G 神经元可诱导一种稳定的、长达数周的休眠状态（G 神经元驱动的长期休眠，简称“GLT”），动物从中苏醒后未检测到行为缺陷或组织病理损伤。相比之下，通过激活同一脑区的泛神经元驱动的长期休眠（PLT）会导致恢复后的多种损伤，这凸显了 G 神经元选择性通路的独特安全性。此外，在小鼠癌症模型中，GLT 直接抑制了肿瘤增殖，并显著提高了肿瘤对化疗的敏感性，取得了显著的治疗效果。总之，我们描绘了一个能够实现安全、持久休眠的专用神经环路。这建立了一种诱导稳定且持续的低代谢状态的特定范式，为基础生理学研究以及针对癌症等慢性疾病的开创性临床策略提供了一个变革性的平台。

## Abstract
Torpor, a state of regulated hypothermia and hypometabolism, is a critical survival strategy in certain mammals. Previous works established the primacy of neuron control in initiating acute torpor. However, whether neuromodulation can safely sustain torpor over the long term, and the specific governing pathways, remain unanswered challenges limiting its translational potential. Here we identify a distinct preoptic neuronal population expressing General control nonderepressible 2 ('G neurons') in mice that is essential and sufficient for torpor induction. Strikingly, sustained and selective activation of G neurons induced a stable, weeks-long torpid state (G neuron-driven long-term torpor, 'GLT'), from which animals arouse without detectable behavioral deficits or tissue pathology. In contrast, long-term torpor driven by pan-neuronal activation of the same brain region (PLT) causes multiple post-recovery damages, underscoring the unique safety profile of selective pathway of G neurons. Furthermore, in a mouse cancer model, GLT directly suppressed tumor proliferation and markedly sensitized tumors to chemotherapy, achieving profound therapeutic outcomes. Together, we delineate a dedicated neural circuit enabling safe, prolonged torpor. This establishes a specific paradigm for inducing a stable and sustained hypometabolic state, providing a transformative platform for fundamental physiological research and pioneering clinical strategies against chronic pathologies such as cancer.
