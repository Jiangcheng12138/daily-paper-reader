## 摘要
研究问题：组织透明化结合体积成像能否对完整人卵巢组织中的卵泡和血管系统进行可靠的定量三维分析？

设计：针对人卵巢髓质和冷冻保存的皮质调整了基于 CUBIC 的透明化方案。对育龄捐赠者的组织进行透明化处理、荧光标记，并使用共聚焦和光片显微镜进行成像。对组织膨胀率、成像深度和血管形态计量学进行了定量，并将卵泡密度与传统组织学进行了比较。

结果：透明化处理产生了光学透明的组织，皮质和髓质的线性膨胀系数均为 1.2。皮质的成像深度增加了 6.5-11 倍，髓质增加了 6-8 倍。免疫标记的透明化皮质中的卵泡密度测量结果与组织学相当，支持了体积卵泡定量的有效性。对凝集素标记的皮质进行光片显微成像显示，血管形态计量学在不同捐赠者之间无显著差异，包括平均血管直径为 12-14 μm，分支点密度为 632-965 个/mm³，血管长度密度为 117-175 mm/mm³，体积分数为 1.9-2.3%。体积成像进一步展示了卵泡与周围血管之间异质的空间关系。

结论：组织透明化和体积成像补充了常规组织学，并能够对完整人卵巢组织中的卵泡-血管相互作用进行定量三维研究，为推进生育力保存和卵巢组织移植研究提供了框架。

## Abstract
Research questionCan tissue clearing, combined with volumetric imaging, enable reliable, quantitative three-dimensional analysis of follicles and vasculature in intact human ovarian tissue?

DesignA CUBIC-based clearing protocol was adapted for human ovarian medulla and cryopreserved cortex. Tissue from reproductive-aged donors was cleared, fluorescently labeled, and imaged using confocal and light sheet microscopy. Tissue expansion, imaging depth, and vascular morphometrics were quantified and follicle density was compared to conventional histology.

ResultsClearing produced optically transparent tissue with a linear expansion factor of 1.2 across cortex and medulla. Imaging depth increased 6.5-11-fold in cortex and 6-8-fold in medulla. Follicle density measurements in immunolabeled cleared cortex were comparable to histology, supporting the validity of volumetric follicle quantification. Light sheet microscopy of lectin-labeled cortex revealed no significant donor-to-donor differences in vascular morphometrics, including mean vessel diameters of 12-14 {micro}m, branch point densities of 632-965 points/mm3, vessel length densities of 117-175 mm/mm3, and volume fractions of 1.9-2.3%. Volumetric imaging further illustrated heterogeneous spatial relationships between follicles and surrounding vessels.

ConclusionTissue clearing and volumetric imaging complement routine histology and enable quantitative three-dimensional investigation of follicle-vascular interactions in intact human ovarian tissue, providing a framework for advancing fertility preservation and ovarian tissue transplantation research.
