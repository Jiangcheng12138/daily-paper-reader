Abstract 21 

The ovary contains two major somatic lineages, granulosa cells and interstitial cells, that 22 

arise from progenitors within the coelomic epithelium. However, how these two lineages 23 

diverge during ovarian development remains unclear. By analyzing joint single-nucleus 24 

transcriptomic and chromatin accessibility profiles of murine ovarian cells at the onset of 25 

ovary formation, we identified two somatic progenitor populations from the coelomic 26 

epithelium distinguished by expression of the nuclear receptors Nr5a1 and Nr2f2 . Based 27 

on their transcriptomic trajectories, the Nr5a1 + epithelial cells preferentially transitioned 28 

toward the granulosa lineage whereas the Nr2f2 + epithelial cells differentiated into 29 

mesenchymal populations. This lineage relationship was supported by Nr2f2 lineage 30 

tracing experiments that fetal Nr2f2 + progenitors contribute to ovarian interstitial cells 31 

postnatally. To define the molecular features underlying this divergence, we performed 32 

differential gene expression and chromatin accessibility analyses and found that Nr2f2 +

33 

epithelial cells, but not Nr5a1 + cells, were enriched for Notch pathway components and 34 

Notch effector motifs. Consistently, lineage tracing of Notch-responsive cells marked 35 

Nr2f2 + interstitial cells in postnatal ovaries, whereas ectopic Notch activation in Nr5a1 +

36 

cells promoted expansion of the interstitial population accompanied by reduced granulosa 37 

cells. By integrating motif analysis with accessible chromatin-gene linkage, we also 38 

identified downstream targets regulated by Notch effectors in Nr2f2 + cells, which showed 39 

concordant changes upon ectopic Notch activation. These findings demonstrate that 40 

somatic cell fate is established early during ovarian development, with active Notch 41 

signaling specifying the interstitial lineage and a balanced Notch activity required for 42 

proper somatic lineage establishment. 43

Significance Statement 45 

Proper differentiation of somatic cell types in the fetal ovary lays the foundation for future 46 

ovarian function in adulthood. Understanding how each cell type is formed is essential for 47 

developing methods to intervene in ovarian diseases caused by cellular dysfunction. 48 

Given that common somatic progenitors give rise to both supporting and interstitial 49 

lineages, a main unanswered question is how these two lineages diverge apart from each 50 

other during ovarian development. By integrating joint single-nucleus transcriptomic and 51 

chromatin accessibility assays with lineage tracing, single cell RNA-sequencing, and 52 

mouse genetic models, we demonstrate the role of Notch signaling in specifying the 53 

interstitial lineage and separating it from the supporting cell fate. 54

Introduction 56 

The mammalian ovary develops from the gonadal primordium, which can arise from the 57 

coelomic epithelium along the surface of the mesonephros around embryonic day (E) 10 58 

in mice (1, 2). The coelomic epithelium proliferates, thickens, and contributes to two major 59 

ovarian somatic lineages: supporting/granulosa cells and interstitial cells (2, 3). The 60 

supporting lineage begins with pre-supporting cells positive of Runx1 expression (4), 61 

which subsequently differentiate into pre-granulosa cells positive of Foxl2 (4). These pre-62 

granulosa cells surround the primordial germ cells that migrate from the hindgut into the 63 

gonad (5, 6) forming germ cell cysts (7). These cysts eventually break down into primordial 64 

follicles with individual germ cells encapsulated by pre-granulosa cells perinatally (8). 65 

Primordial follicles remain non-growing until activated, after which pre-granulosa cells 66 

differentiate into granulosa cells to support oocyte growth and maturation (9, 10). In 67 

addition, pre-granulosa cells continue to arise from the ovarian epithelium throughout 68 

embryonic development and contribute to the second wave of follicle formation (11, 12). 69 

The interstitial lineage comprises both non-steroidogenic stromal cells and steroidogenic 70 

theca cells, which reside between follicles, outside the basement membrane that 71 

separates granulosa cells from the interstitium/mesenchyme (13). Unlike the epithelial-like 72 

pre-granulosa and granulosa cells, interstitial cells are mesenchymal in nature. They 73 

provide structural support to the ovary and contribute to extracellular matrix production 74 

(13). In the case of theca cells, in response to hormone stimulation they produce 75 

androgens, which is subsequently converted to estrogen by granulosa cells (13). Our 76 

group demonstrated, using mouse genetic models, that interstitial cells arise from Wt1 +

77 

somatic progenitors and Gli1 + mesonephric progenitors recruited during fetal development 78 

(14). As Wt1 + progenitors give rise not only to interstitial but also to supporting lineages 79 

(14, 15), an unresolved question is how interstitial progenitors diverge from the supporting 80 

cell lineage during ovarian development. 81 

Notch signaling is a highly conserved pathway essential for numerous developmental 82 

processes, including cell fate specification (reviewed in (16)). In mammals, Notch signaling 83 

is activated when ligands of the Jagged or Delta-like families bind to one of four Notch 84 

receptors (Notch1-4). Ligand-receptor interaction triggers proteolytic cleavage of the 85 

receptor, releasing the Notch intracellular domain to be translocated to the nucleus and 86 

forms a transcriptional complex with the DNA-binding protein RBPJ and co-activators of 87 

the MAML family. This complex regulates the expression of downstream target genes, 88 

including members of the Hes and Hey families of transcription factors. Conversely, Notch 89 

signaling is negatively regulated by NUMB, which promotes receptor degradation and 90 

suppresses pathway activation (16). Notch signaling plays critical roles during ovarian 91 

development, including follicle formation, follicle growth, and meiotic maturation (17). Loss 92 

of Notch ligand Jag1 in germ cells and receptor Notch2 in granulosa cells result in follicles 93 

containing multiple germ cells, indicating disrupted follicle assembly (18). In addition, 94 

global deletion of Hes1 decreases germ cell numbers while increasing pre-granulosa cells, 95 

whereas Notch activation in pre-granulosa cells increases oocyte numbers and reduces 96 

pre-granulosa cell abundance (19). Furthermore, Numb has been associated with 97 

asymmetric division of the coelomic epithelium in the fetal mouse ovary, and its global 98 

deletion prior to ovarian formation leads to increased expression of undifferentiated cell 99 

markers and a reduction in supporting cells by E13.5 (20). These findings underscore the 100 

requirement of regulated Notch activity in somatic cell differentiation and ovarian function. 101

Given the shared progenitor origin of ovarian somatic lineages, we sought to determine 102 

whether differential Notch signaling activity contributes to the divergence of supporting 103 

and interstitial cell fates during early ovarian development. We hypothesized that active 104 

Notch signaling in epithelial progenitors promotes interstitial lineage specification, 105 

whereas reduced Notch activity favors supporting cell differentiation during early gonad 106 

development. To address this, we combined single-nucleus transcriptomic and chromatin 107 

accessibility profiling at the onset of ovary formation with lineage tracing, single-cell RNA 108 

sequencing, and mouse genetic models to investigate how Notch signaling influences 109 

early ovarian somatic lineage specification .110

To understand how distinct somatic lineages are established during ovary formation, we 115

To identify molecular features distinguishing the Nr5a1 ⁺ and Nr2f2 ⁺ epithelial populations, 161

Ectopic Notch activation in Nr5a1 + cells increases interstitial-to-supporting cell 264 

ratio, upregulates interstitial gene expression, and disrupts ovarian morphology 265 

Based on our finding that Notch-responsive and/or Nr2f2 + cells mark interstitial progenitors 266 

(Fig. 3 ), we hypothesized that active Notch signaling in Nr2f2 + cells at the onset of ovary 267 

formation specifies the interstitial lineage fate, whereas repressed Notch signaling in the 268 

Nr5a1 + cells permits supporting cell differentiation. To test this, we ectopically activated 269 

the Notch pathway in Nr5a1 + cells by crossing Tg(Nr5a1-cre) mice with ROSA Notch mice, 270 

generating embryos expressing a constitutively active Notch intracellular domain in the 271 

Nr5a1 + cells. Fetal ovaries from Notch-induced (Nr5a1-cre+; ROSA +/Notch ) and control 272 

(Nr5a1-cre+; ROSA +/+ ) littermates were collected at E13.5, E15.5, and E18.5 for analysis 273 

(Fig. 4A ). 274 

At E13.5, control ovaries contained on average half as many NR2F2 + interstitial cells as 275 

FOXL2 + supporting pre-granulosa cells. In contrast, Notch-induced ovaries exhibited a 276 

significant increase in interstitial relative to supporting cells, with interstitial cell numbers 277 

equal to or exceeding those of supporting cells ( Fig. 4B , top, Fig. 4C ). Moreover, NR2F2 +

278 

cells were frequently observed in clusters near the ovarian epithelium ( Fig. 4B , top). The 279 

presence of germ cells, supporting cells, and interstitial cells in Notch-induced ovaries 280 

indicates that ectopic Notch activation in Nr5a1 + cells does not prevent initial ovary 281 

formation. By E15.5, morphological differences between the two genotypes became more 282 

apparent. In control ovaries, laminin staining delineated well-organized germ cell cysts in 283 

which oocyte clusters were surrounded by pre-granulosa cells. In contrast, Notch-induced 284 

ovaries displayed disrupted cyst organization, characterized by smaller, irregular cysts 285 

with increased accumulation of interstitial cells between them ( Fig. 4B , middle). By E18.5, 286 

ovarian organization was further compromised in Notch-induced ovaries with reduced 287 

oocyte numbers and disorganized pre-granulosa cells that failed to properly surround 288 

oocytes ( Fig. 4B, bottom, Fig. S5 ). 289

Interstitial gene expression is linked to Notch-related transcription factor motifs 312 

To delineate how Notch signaling regulates interstitial-related gene expression in the fetal 313 

ovary, we performed motif analysis using the joint single-nucleus RNA and ATAC dataset. 314 

We first identified differential accessible chromatin peaks (DAPs) between Nr2f2 + and 315 

Nr2f2 - cells and used these peaks for transcription factor (TF) motif enrichment analysis 316 

(Fig. 5A, i ). We next performed motif scan within accessible peaks of Nr2f2 + epithelial and 317 

mesenchymal cells to determine motif positions ( Fig. 5A, ii ) and linked putative TFs, 318 

including HES1, RBPJ, and NR2F2 to their downstream target genes ( Fig. 5A, iii ). 319 

Using this workflow, we first identified DAPs between all Nr2f2 + (including Nr2f2 + epithelial 320 

and mesenchymal cells) and Nr2f2 - cells (including Nr5a1 + epithelial, pre-supporting, and 321 

supporting-like cells). The analysis revealed over 3,000 DAPs in Nr2f2 + cells and over 322 

2,000 in Nr2f2 - cells ( Fig. S6A ). Motif enrichment within Nr2f2 + DAPs revealed significant 323 

enrichment for motifs of NR2F2 and Notch-related TFs including RBPJ and HES1 ( SI 324 

Data ). Next, we performed motif scan to identify the precise genomic locations of these 325

intragenic peak containing both HES1 and RBPJ motifs ( Fig. 5D ). 345 

We also detected negatively linked genes, defined as genes for which increased 346 

chromatin accessibility was associated with decreased gene expression, suggesting 347 

potential repressive effects of Notch-related TF binding. Negatively associated genes 348 

included Wnt4 , Kitl , Ndfip1 , and Arhgap26 , which were more highly expressed in Nr5a1 +

349 

epithelial, pre-supporting, and supporting-like cells ( Fig. 5E ). Consistently, these genes 350 

were downregulated in Notch-induced ovaries ( Fig. 5F ). One example of such a negatively 351 

linked region was the Hspg2 promoter peak containing both HES1 and RBPJ motifs, which 352 

showed a negative association with Wnt4 expression ( Fig. 5G , Fig. S6C ). 353 

Together, these results reveal distinct transcriptional regulatory networks underlying 354 

somatic cell lineage separation in the fetal ovary. Notch-related TFs may directly promote 355 

interstitial fate specification by activating interstitial-associated gene programs (e.g. 356 

Pdgfrb) while repressing supporting-associated genes (e.g. Wnt4 ). 357

Discussion 359 

In this study, we aimed to address an important unresolved question in ovarian biology: 360 

how the two major somatic lineages, supporting granulosa and interstitial cells, establish 361 

distinct identities and diverge during development. Previous studies using ex vivo 362 

fluorescent dye labeling of the coelomic epithelium and BrdU pulse-chase experiments 363 

have shown that coelomic epithelial cells overlying the gonad proliferate and migrate 364 

inward without apparent lineage restriction toward either supporting or interstitial fates (3, 365 

30). Evidence for the earliest divergence of the two lineages in the fetal ovary comes from 366 

mouse genetic lineage-tracing studies using tamoxifen-inducible Axin2 CreERT2 model, which 367 

marks WNT-responsive cells. When Axin2 -positive progenitors are labeled at E10.5, they 368 

give rise exclusively to supporting cells of first-wave medullary follicles (12). Similarly, 369 

lineage tracing using Foxl2 CreERT2 (11, 31) and Lgr5 CreERT2 (12) at E12.5 and later 370 

timepoints label only granulosa cell lineages. In addition, granulosa cells can arise from 371 

Nestin ⁺ perivascular cells labeled at E12.5, although labeling at later embryonic stages 372 

also gives rise to steroidogenic interstitial cells (32). On the other hand, interstitial cell 373 

progenitors have been exclusively traced from Gli1 ⁺ mesonephric progenitors at E12.5 374 

(14) and from Wnt5a + cells at E15.5 (33). In the present study, we demonstrate that 375 

progenitors of ovarian interstitial cells can be lineage labeled as early as E10.5, 376 

demonstrating that separation of the two somatic lineages occurs at the beginning of ovary 377 

formation. 378 

To gain mechanistic insight into how somatic lineage separation occurs, we analyzed our 379 

10x Multiome dataset (21) and identified two coelomic epithelial populations in the E11.5 380 

mouse ovary: one characterized by Nr2f2 expression and enrichment of Notch effectors, 381 

and the other defined by Nr5a1 expression and enrichment of Notch antagonist Numb .382 

Trajectory inference using RNA velocity analysis suggested that Nr5a1 + epithelial cells 383 

preferentially give rise to the supporting lineage, whereas Nr2f2 + epithelial cells contribute 384 

to the interstitial lineage. These observations are supported by lineage-tracing 385 

experiments using Nr2f2 and the Notch-responsive Hes1 models, which indicate that 386 

ovarian interstitial progenitors can be identified as early as E10.5 through labeling of 387 

Notch-active and or Nr2f2 + progenitors. Nonetheless, our lineage-tracing models do not 388 

exclusively label these progenitors within the epithelium. In addition, we cannot fully 389

The transcriptional heterogeneity of the coelomic epithelium in E11.5 ovary was confirmed 395

single-cell analysis and 24h short-term tamoxifen labeling. Although the fraction of labeled 409

detected sporadically in the coelomic epithelium, a small number of cells within the gonad, 413 

and endothelial cells. This restricted activation may reflect local ligand availability and or 414 

concurrent expression of the Notch antagonist Numb . Spatially resolved approaches will 415 

be required to identify the cellular sources and contexts of Notch activation. 416 

Importantly, our data indicate that Notch signaling is by no means merely a lineage marker, 417 

but actively drives interstitial gene program. Ectopic activation of Notch in Nr5a1 + cells 418 

promoted interstitial lineage expansion, disrupting the balance between interstitial and 419 

supporting cell populations. This result is further supported by prior studies showing that 420 

global deletion of Hes1 reduces interstitial marker expression while increasing pre-421 

granulosa marker expression in the ovary (19). It has also been reported that ubiquitous 422 

CreER-mediated deletion of Notch antagonist Numb leads to an expansion of somatic 423 

progenitor cells marked by LHX9 (20). However, while one would expect that loss of this 424 

Notch antagonist to phenocopy ectopic Notch activation, we did not observe increased 425 

expression of progenitor-associated markers including Lhx9 and Emx2 in our bulk RNA-426 

seq analysis. In addition, we did not detect epithelial invagination phenotypes described 427 

in Numb -deleted ovaries. This suggests that Notch activation is separable from NUMB-428 

mediated asymmetric distribution and division of the coelomic epithelium, and that loss of 429 

Numb alone does not necessarily result in activate Notch signaling sufficient to drive an 430 

interstitial cell transcriptional program. 431 

Notch signaling has been shown to promote epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition in 432 

various developmental and disease contexts (37, 38). Through motif and peak-gene 433

associated with supporting markers such as Wnt4 and Kitl . The observation that Notch-437

with adjusted p-value < 0.05 and log2FC >1 was performed using Enrichr (49-51). Single-675

from the 10X Multiome dataset served as our single-cell reference. 677
