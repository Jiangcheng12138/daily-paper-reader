Abstract ：12 

Aging arises from interconnected molecular defects, yet upstream regulatory 13 

mechanisms that coordinate these hallmarks remain incompletely defined. While 14 

epitranscriptomic regulation has emerged as a critical layer of gene control, the 15 

contribution of tRNA-specific modifications to aging remains largely unexplored. 16 

Here, we systematically profile tRNA modifications across multiple organs, species, 17 

and senescence models and identify mannosyl-queuosine (manQ), a wobble-position 18 

modification of tRNA Asp , as the first tRNA-specific modification that consistently 19 

declines with age. ManQ depletion is evolutionarily conserved and tightly correlates 20 

with functional deterioration. Mechanistically, loss of manQ impairs translational 21 

fidelity, leading to proteome imbalance, collapse of proteostasis, and aberrant 22 

expression of senescence-associated proteins, including GPNMB. These translational 23 

defects intersect with established aging hallmarks and accelerate cellular and 24 

organismal aging. We further demonstrate that circulating queuine, a25 

microbiota-derived precursor required for manQ biosynthesis, declines with age in 26 

rodents and humans. Queuine deficiency promotes senescence, whereas 27 

supplementation restores manQ levels, improves translational accuracy, suppresses 28 

p16/p21-driven senescence programs, and re-establishes proteostatic balance. Across 29

INTRODUCTION 41 

Aging constitutes a complex physiological process characterized by progressive 42 

functional decline, diminished adaptive capacity across molecular, cellular, tissue, and 43 

organ systems, and an elevated mortality risk (Gladyshev et al., 2021; López-Otín et 44 

al., 2023). Demographic projections estimate that by 2050, the global population aged 45 

over 60 years will reach 2.1 billion, constituting 22% of the total population. This 46 

unprecedented demographic shift poses escalating health and socioeconomic 47 

challenges (Navaneetham and Arunachalam, 2023). Consequently, aging research 48 

represents not only a frontier in fundamental biology but also a strategic priority for 49 

societal resilience. Healthspan extension has thus emerged as both a perennial human 50 

aspiration and a central focus of contemporary geroscience. Moreover, systematic 51 

elucidation of the molecular networks governing aging is crucial to deciphering 52 

senescence regulation and developing targeted geroprotective strategies (López-Otín 53 

et al., 2013). This paradigm transition, from reactive disease treatment to proactive 54 

aging modulation, holds transformative potential for achieving a pivotal biomedical 55 

milestone: realizing humanity's shared vision of "aging without frailty". 56 

The landmark 2023 review in Cell systematically defined twelve conserved 57 

hallmarks of aging, spanning genomic instability to mitochondrial dysfunction, 58 

recently expanded to include two emerging features: extracellular matrix alterations 59 

and psychosocial isolation (Kroemer et al., 2025; López-Otín et al., 2023). 60 

Collectively, these hallmarks constitute an intricately interconnected network that 61 

profoundly informs our understanding of aging etiology and intervention strategies 62 

(Bao et al., 2023). However, critical gaps persist in their mechanistic interpretation 63 

and translational application. While these classical hallmarks have catalyzed the 64 

development of aging assessment frameworks, their utility in deciphering real-time 65 

regulatory dynamics remains limited, and identifying actionable targets for 66 

therapeutic intervention remains challenging (Bao et al., 2023). Novel geroprotective 67 

strategies, including caloric restriction, fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT), and 68 

heterochronic parabiosis, have garnered significant attention for their 69

2020). However, prohibitive medical costs and inconsistent therapeutic efficacy 71

continue to advance. Rapamycin extends healthspan in model organisms primarily 74 

through mTORC1 inhibition (Mannick et al., 2023). Spermidine delays cellular 75 

senescence by inducing autophagy and potentiating antioxidant responses (Zhang et 76 

al., 2023). Quercetin, a recognized senolytic agent, selectively eliminates senescent 77 

cells (Novais et al., 2021). Despite promising preclinical results, the clinical 78 

application of these compounds faces unresolved controversies regarding human 79 

efficacy and safety. Rapamycin’s therapeutic utility is constrained by dose-dependent 80 

immunosuppressive effects and metabolic complications (Mannick et al., 2023). 81 

Although efficacious in cellular and animal models, spermidine and quercetin lack 82 

robust validation in large-scale human clinical trials (López-Otín et al., 2023). 83 

Critically, single-target approaches inadequately address the multifactorial 84 

pathogenesis of aging, underscoring the need for combinatorial strategies that 85 

concurrently target multiple hallmarks of senescence. 86 

Epitranscriptomics investigates post-transcriptional gene regulation through 87 

dynamic RNA modifications, offering unique insights into aging biology (McMahon 88 

et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2023). The field gained significant momentum when He et al. 89 

identified FTO as the first m 6A RNA demethylase, establishing RNA modifications as 90 

a reversible regulatory layer (Jia et al., 2011). Subsequent research has elucidated the 91 

dynamic regulatory roles of diverse RNA modifications in disease pathogenesis and 92 

aging progression (Roundtree et al., 2017). For instance, METTL3 deficiency in 93 

human pluripotent stem cells triggers premature senescence and apoptosis (Wu et al., 94 

2023). Conversely, TRDMT1, the m5C methyltransferase, alleviates cellular 95 

senescence by orchestrating DNA damage repair through recruitment of 96 

RAD51/RAD52 complexes (Chen et al., 2020). Despite these advances, current 97 

research disproportionately focuses on mRNA modifications, while the 98 

epitranscriptomic regulation of aging by tRNA modifications remains largely 99

over 70% of known epitranscriptomic modifications localized to these molecules 101

pathologies, particularly neurodegenerative disorders, highlighting their systemic 105 

importance in aging (Jonkhout et al., 2017; Zhou et al., 2021). Recent studies have 106 

identified that METTL1, the methyltransferase responsible for tRNA m7G107 

modification, exhibits age-dependent depletion (Fu et al., 2024). This deficiency leads 108 

to tRNA destabilization, ribosomal stalling at specific codons, and activation of 109 

ribotoxicity and integrated stress responses, ultimately driving exacerbated 110 

senescence-associated secretory phenotypes (SASP). While these findings establish 111 

tRNA epitranscriptomics as a critical regulator of aging, the field remains nascent. 112 

Notably, the mechanistic roles of tRNA-specific modifications, particularly those 113 

distinct from modifications shared with mRNA, within aging pathways have not yet 114 

been systematically characterized. 115 

Mannosyl-queuosine (manQ), first characterized in the 1970s, is a hypermodified 116 

nucleoside uniquely found at position 34 of the anticodon loop in tRNA Asp(manQUC) of 117 

mammals (Hillmeier et al., 2021; Okada and Nishimura, 1977). Despite its early 118 

discovery, both its structural conformation and biosynthetic pathway remained 119 

unresolved until recent advances (Hillmeier et al., 2021). The identification of 120 

GTDC1 as the enzyme responsible for manQ biosynthesis marked a pivotal 121 

breakthrough, thereby addressing a critical knowledge gap that had impeded 122 

mechanistic studies (Zhao et al., 2023). Strikingly conserved evolutionary patterns 123 

emerge in manQ's developmental regulation: Thomas et al. reported significantly 124 

higher manQ levels in adult mouse kidneys and other organs compared to neonates, 125 

with similar patterns observed in rat liver models (Costa et al., 2004; Thumbs et al., 126 

2020). Zebrafish GTDC1 knockout studies further demonstrated severe growth 127 

defects, establishing manQ as indispensable for vertebrate post-embryonic 128 

development (Zhao et al., 2023). Queuine, the 7-deazaguanine-derived essential 129

ulcerative colitis and cognitive impairment, and Ames designate it as a "longevity 139 

vitamin" (Ames, 2018; Cirzi et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023). Nevertheless, its 140

In this study, leveraging our group’s established tRNA modification profiling 143 

platform (Cao et al., 2020; Yan et al., 2021), we mapped the landscape of tRNA 144 

modifications across multiple organs in aging rats. Strikingly, manQ modification 145 

exhibited consistent age-dependent depletion in diverse senescence models, emerging 146 

as the first tRNA-specific modification demonstrating hallmark-like correlations with 147 

aging. This positions manQ decline as a novel biomarker candidate for aging 148 

assessment, fulfilling key criteria of biological relevance and dynamic responsiveness. 149 

Mechanistic studies revealed that manQ depletion compromises translational fidelity, 150 

as demonstrated by aberrant expression of senescence effector proteins such as 151 

glycoprotein non-metastatic melanoma protein B (GPNMB). These molecular 152 

alterations drive accelerated aging phenotypes through dual mechanisms: error-prone 153 

translation and proteostasis network collapse. Crucially, we provide the first 154 

experimental evidence that queuine supplementation delays aging and extends 155 

healthspan by restoring manQ modification levels, rescuing translational accuracy, 156 

and rebalancing proteostatic capacity. Queuine further modulated canonical aging 157 

hallmarks, including nutrient-sensing pathways through proteostasis remodeling, 158 

enabling systemic regulation of aging. Our findings collectively propose two 159

molecular failures, yet the upstream regulatory events that coordinate these hallmarks 900 

remain incompletely defined. Although the hallmarks framework provides a powerful 901 

conceptual scaffold, many hallmarks primarily describe downstream cellular and 902 

tissue consequences rather than the primary molecular defects that initiate or 903 

synchronize functional decline. Here, we identify an evolutionarily conserved, 904 

age-dependent depletion of tRNA mannosyl-queuosine (manQ) as a previously 905 

underappreciated epitranscriptomic alteration that links translational control to 906

Among the more than one hundred known RNA modifications, tRNA 911 

modifications constitute the most abundant and structurally diverse class, yet their 912 

contributions to aging biology remain comparatively understudied. Our profiling 913

manQ hypomodification is selective rather than reflecting global tRNA depletion: 916

corresponding unmodified tRNA Asp fragment, and other queuosine-modified tRNAs 918 

relatively unchanged. This pattern supports a regulated defect in modification 919 

homeostasis rather than a generalized change in transcript abundance. Such specificity 920 

argues that manQ loss is not merely a passive consequence of tissue degeneration, but 921

mechanistic impact. 923 

A central conceptual advance of our work is the positioning of manQ as a 924 

functionally informative biomarker candidate that aligns with widely accepted criteria 925 

for robust aging indicators: (i) a quantifiable trajectory across age, (ii) causal linkage 926 

to aging phenotypes, and (iii) therapeutic reversibility upon targeted intervention. 927 

First, we observe an age-associated decline in circulating queuine — the 928 

microbiota-derived precursor required for manQ biosynthesis — in both rodents and 929 

humans, consistent with a systemic determinant that can be measured reproducibly 930 

across aging. Second, experimental depletion of manQ through pathway perturbation 931 

accelerates senescence programs, whereas restoration of queuine availability 932

cellular and organismal contexts. Third, queuine supplementation confers therapeutic 934 

reversibility across biological scales, improving healthspan phenotypes and extending 935
