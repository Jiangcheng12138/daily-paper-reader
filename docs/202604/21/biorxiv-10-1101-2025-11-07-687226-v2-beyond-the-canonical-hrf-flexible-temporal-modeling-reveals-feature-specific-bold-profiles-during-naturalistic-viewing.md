## 摘要
自然刺激（如电影和叙事）在认知神经科学中被越来越多地用于将认知和情感过程映射到通过功能磁共振成像 (fMRI) 测量的脑活动上。从电影中提取的特征跨越多个层面，从视觉和听觉输入的计算描述到生理信号和主观评分。然而，这些特征与血氧水平依赖 (BOLD) 响应之间的时间对齐可能存在显著差异，而常用的带有时间导数的标准血流动力学响应函数 (HRF) 可能无法充分捕捉这些延迟。在本研究中，我们分析了三个观看电影的数据集，并考察了一系列特征，包括视觉亮度和对比度、听觉音高、瞳孔大小的生理测量以及心理理论的主观评分。我们在全脑范围内对特征时间序列与 fMRI 响应进行了互相关分析和有限脉冲响应 (FIR) 反卷积。我们的结果表明，标准 HRF 能够很好地捕捉感官特征，但与瞳孔大小和主观评分等较慢的信号存在对齐偏差。值得注意的是，原始瞳孔大小和主观评分与延迟的血流动力学响应表现出更强的对应关系。最后，利用 FIR 反卷积，我们系统地刻画了这些多样化特征的真实 BOLD 响应剖面，揭示了不同区域间明显的时间层级和峰值潜伏期，这些特征系统性地偏离了标准假设。这些发现强调了时间对齐在将自然特征映射到脑活动中的关键作用。使用灵活的时间建模来考虑特征特异性延迟，可能会提高使用复杂现实世界刺激的 fMRI 研究的可解释性和准确性。

## Abstract
Naturalistic stimuli, such as movies and narratives, are increasingly used in cognitive neuroscience to map cognitive and affective processes onto brain activity measured with functional MRI (fMRI). Features extracted from movies span multiple levels, from computational descriptions of visual and auditory input to physiological signals and subjective ratings. However, the temporal alignment between these features and the blood-oxygen-level-dependent (BOLD) response can vary considerably, and the commonly used canonical hemodynamic response function (HRF) with temporal derivatives may not adequately capture these delays. In this study, we analyzed three movie-watching datasets and examined a range of features, including visual luminance and contrast, auditory pitch, a physiological measure of pupil size, and subjective ratings of theory of mind. We performed cross-correlation analyses and finite impulse response (FIR) deconvolution between feature time series and fMRI responses across the whole brain. Our results show that the canonical HRF captures sensory features well, but misaligns with slower signals, such as pupil size and subjective ratings. Notably, raw pupil size and subjective ratings exhibited stronger correspondence with delayed hemodynamic responses. Lastly, using FIR deconvolution, we systematically characterized the true BOLD response profiles of these diverse features, revealing distinct inter-regional temporal hierarchies and peak latencies that systematically deviate from canonical assumptions. These findings highlight the critical role of temporal alignment in mapping naturalistic features to brain activity. Accounting for feature-specific delays using flexible temporal modeling may improve the interpretability and accuracy of fMRI studies using complex, real-world stimuli.
