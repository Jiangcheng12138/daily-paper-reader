## 摘要
我们介绍并分析了一类受果蝇中央复合体启发的神经网络模型，旨在捕捉定向选择性活动隆起（activity bumps）的出现及其动力学。从生物启发的环形连接模型出发，我们推导出一个简化的循环神经活动约化模型，该模型支持编码角位置的稳定局部模式。我们首先研究了确定性动力学，并确定了确保隆起解存在性和全局稳定性的参数范围。随后，我们将该框架扩展到随机环境，纳入了加性布朗噪声和代表随时间变化的外部线索的马尔可夫切换机制。所得系统是一个具有分段线性漂移的切换扩散过程，为此我们确立了其适定性，表征了无穷小生成元，并证明了不变测度的存在性。低维和高维的数值模拟说明了隆起吸引子在噪声和切换刺激下的鲁棒性，以及向预测平稳态的收敛性。这些结果提供了一个数学上易于处理的框架，用于理解在存在变异性的情况下，昆虫中央复合体中的群体活动如何编码航向。

## Abstract
We introduce and analyze a class of neural network models motivated by the Drosophila central complex, designed to capture the emergence and dynamics of orientation-selective activity bumps. Starting from a biologically inspired ring connectivity model, we derive a simplofied reduced model of recurrent neural activity that supports stable localized patterns encoding angular position. We first study the deterministic dynamics and identify parameter regimes ensuring existence and global stability of bump solutions. We then extend the framework to a stochastic setting, incorporating both additive Brownian noise and a Markovian switching mechanism representing time-varying external cues. The resulting system is a switching diffusion with piecewise linear drift, for which we establish well-posedness, characterize the infinitesimal generator, and prove the existence of an invariant measure. Numerical simulations in low and high dimensions illustrate the robustness of the bump attractor under noise and switching stimuli, as well as the convergence toward the predicted stationary states. These results provide a mathematically tractable framework for understanding how population activity in the insect central complex encodes heading direction in the presence of variability.
