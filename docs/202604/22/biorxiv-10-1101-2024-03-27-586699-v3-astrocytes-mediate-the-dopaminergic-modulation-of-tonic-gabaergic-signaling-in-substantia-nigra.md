## 摘要
最近的研究表明，黑质网状部（SNr）内的多巴胺能信号传导与帕金森病运动障碍的出现有关。为了更好地理解这种依赖性的潜在机制，研究者在离体小鼠脑切片中研究了多巴胺调节 SNr 神经元活动的能力。除了在突触前抑制相位性 GABA 释放外，D2 多巴胺受体（D2R）信号传导出乎意料地抑制了由 GABAA 受体介导的 SNr 神经元放电的持续性抑制。我们的研究表明，这种持续性调节主要由表达 ALDH1A1 的多巴胺能神经元通过 GAT-1 依赖性的 GABA 释放所介导。相比之下，D2R 激动剂解除 SNr 神经元抑制的能力取决于表达 D2R 的星形胶质细胞以及对细胞外空间 GABA 的 GAT-3 摄取的刺激。除了强调树突释放的多巴胺在调节突触传递中的重要性外，我们的研究还证明了星形胶质细胞的多巴胺能调节在调节 SNr 环路和运动行为中起着关键作用。

## Abstract
Recent studies have implicated dopaminergic signaling within the substantia nigra pars reticulata (SNr) in the emergence of Parkinsonian motor deficits. To better understand the mechanisms underlying this dependence, the ability of dopamine to modulate SNr neuron activity was studied in ex vivo mouse brain slices. In addition to presynaptically inhibiting phasic GABA release, D2 dopamine receptor (D2R) signaling unexpectedly suppressed a tonic, GABAA receptor-mediated inhibition of SNr neuron spiking. Our studies demonstrated that this tonic modulation was largely mediated by GAT-1-dependent GABA release from ALDH1A1-expressing dopaminergic neurons. In contrast, the ability of D2R agonists to disinhibit SNr neurons depended upon D2R-expressing astrocytes and stimulation of GAT-3 uptake of GABA from the extracellular space. In addition to underscoring the importance of dendritically released dopamine in modulating synaptic transmission, our studies demonstrate that dopaminergic modulation of astrocytes plays a key role in modulating SNr circuits and motor behavior.
