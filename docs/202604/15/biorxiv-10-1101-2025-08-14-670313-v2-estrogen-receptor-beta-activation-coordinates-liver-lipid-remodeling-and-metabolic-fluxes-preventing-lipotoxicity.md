## 摘要
已知使用雌二醇进行非选择性激素替代疗法可改善绝经期间的代谢稳态。然而，对于具有激素反应性癌症遗传倾向的个体，不建议采用这种治疗方法。相比之下，选择性激活雌激素受体β (ERβ) 已显示出良好的前景，能够促进抗肿瘤作用并调节代谢结果，尽管这些变化发生的机制仍不清楚。我们利用选择性 ERβ 激动剂二芳基丙腈 (DPN)，在绝经后体内模型和代谢超载体外模型中研究了 ERβ 激活的影响。雌性 Wistar 大鼠接受卵巢切除术 (OVX)，随后接受 DPN 治疗。ERβ 激动剂治疗恢复了空腹血糖和血脂水平，改善了胰岛形态，并减少了腹膜后白色脂肪组织。血清酮体和游离脂肪酸水平也恢复至对照水平，表明肝脏脂质氧化受到了调节。DPN 治疗还特别促进了 OVX 动物体内显著的脂质组重塑，产生了一种脂质缓冲谱。为了从机制上分离这些效应，使肝细胞处于营养超载状态并接受 DPN 治疗。在体外，通过调节性细胞外通量分析评估，DPN 同样恢复了酮体分泌，并促进了对完全脂肪酸氧化的依赖性增加，同时降低了代谢灵活性。总之，这些发现证明了 ERβ 在调节肝脏脂质和酮体代谢中的新作用，并在雌二醇缺乏的动物中产生了积极的代谢结果。

## Abstract
Non-selective hormone replacement with estradiol is known to improve metabolic homeostasis during menopause. However, this treatment is not recommended for individuals with genetic predisposition to hormone-responsive cancers. In contrast, selective activation of estrogen receptor beta (ER{beta}) has shown promising results, promoting antitumor effects and modulating metabolic outcomes, although the mechanisms in which these changes occur remain poorly understood. We investigated the effects of ER{beta} activation using diarylpropionitrile (DPN), a selective ER{beta} agonist, in both an in vivo model of post menopause and in vitro model of metabolic overload. Female Wistar rats were submitted to ovariectomy (OVX) and later treated with DPN. ER{beta} agonist treatment recovered fasting glucose and lipid profiles, improved pancreatic islet morphology, and reduced retroperitoneal white adipose tissue. Serum ketone bodies and free fatty acid levels were also recovered to control levels, suggesting modulation in liver lipid oxidation. DPN treatment also promoted substantial lipidome remodeling specifically in OVX animals, generating a lipid-buffering profile. To isolate the effects mechanistically, hepatocytes were submitted to nutrient overload and treated with DPN. In vitro, DPN also recovered ketone body secretion and promoted an increased dependence on complete fatty acid oxidation, as well as decreased metabolic flexibility, as assessed by modulated extracellular flux analysis. Overall, these findings demonstrate a new role of ER{beta} in the modulation of hepatic lipid and ketone metabolism, with positive metabolic outcomes in estradiol-deficient animals.
