## 摘要
生物周期是跨越广泛时间尺度的普遍细胞过程。诸如细胞周期、昼夜节律或分节时钟等基础周期以细胞自主方式发生，且通常与其他细胞过程（包括细胞类型身份、代谢状态和疾病相关程序）相耦合。在单细胞转录组学（scRNA-seq）中，将这些连续的周期性轨迹从其他细胞变异源中解耦出来仍然是一项重大挑战。在此，我们介绍了 CoPhaser，这是一种学习上下文相关周期性流形的算法，旨在将 scRNA-seq 计数数据分解为独立的周期性和非周期性变异源，同时保持流形坐标在不同生物上下文中的可解释性。CoPhaser 基于一种具有结构化潜空间的生物启发式变分自编码器，该空间在控制互信息的同时，明确地将周期相位与细胞上下文分离。通过将基因表达建模为受上下文调制的谐波函数，该模型捕捉了周期性流形灵活且具有生物学依据的形变。我们展示了 CoPhaser 在四种生物周期中产生新生物学见解的能力。它在无需基因程序或细胞周期状态先验知识的情况下，能够跨多种测序技术（包括发育和癌症等高度异质性的场景）恢复准确的连续细胞周期相位。在癌症应用中，CoPhaser 揭示了亚型特异性的增殖动力学，识别了复发性儿童急性髓系白血病中的静息原始状态，并区分了三阴性乳腺癌中增殖驱动型与组成型基因过表达，突显了潜在的稳健治疗靶点。它进一步扩展到空间癌症转录组学，揭示了卵巢肿瘤中细胞周期相位的空间同步性。CoPhaser 可推广到其他周期系统，能够重建小鼠主动脉中的昼夜节律钟，并识别细胞类型和亚型特异性的昼夜节律差异。此外，它描绘了人类月经周期中连续的子宫内膜重塑，并揭示了子宫内膜异位症中改变的转录动力学。最后，它揭示了小鼠胚胎中细胞周期与体节时钟之间的耦合。总之，CoPhaser 为剖析单细胞数据中细胞身份与生物周期之间的相互作用提供了一个通用且可解释的框架。

## Abstract
Biological cycles are ubiquitous cellular processes operating across a wide range of time scales. Fundamental cycles such as the cell cycle, circadian rhythms, or the segmentation clock occur cell-autonomously and are typically coupled to other cellular processes, including cell-type identity, metabolic states, and disease-associated programs. In single-cell transcriptomics (scRNA-seq), disentangling these continuous periodic trajectories from other sources of cellular variability remains a major challenge. Here, we introduce CoPhaser, an algorithm that learns context-dependent periodic manifolds to decompose scRNA-seq count data into independent periodic and non-periodic sources of variation, while preserving interpretability of manifold coordinates across biological contexts. CoPhaser is based on a biologically informed variational autoencoder with a structured latent space that explicitly separates cycle phase from cellular context while controlling their mutual information. By modeling gene expression as context-modulated harmonic functions, the model captures flexible yet biologically grounded deformations of periodic manifolds. We demonstrate CoPhasers ability to yield novel biological insights across four biological cycles. It recovers accurate continuous cell-cycle phases across diverse sequencing technologies, including highly heterogeneous settings such as development and cancer, without prior knowledge of gene programs or cell-cycle states. In cancer applications, CoPhaser reveals subtype-specific proliferation dynamics, identifying quiescent primitive states in relapsed pediatric acute myeloid leukemia and distinguishing proliferation-driven from constitutive gene overexpression in triple-negative breast cancer, highlighting potential robust therapeutic targets. It further extends to spatial cancer transcriptomics, revealing spatial synchronisation of cell-cycle phases in ovarian tumors. CoPhaser generalizes to other periodic systems, enabling reconstruction of circadian clocks in the mouse aorta, and identifies cell-type and subtype-specific circadian differences. In addition, it maps continuous endometrial remodeling across the human menstrual cycle and reveals altered transcriptional dynamics in endometriosis. Finally, it reveals coupling between the cell cycle and the somite clock in the mouse embryo. Together, CoPhaser provides a versatile and interpretable framework for dissecting the interplay between cellular identity and biological cycles in single-cell data.
