## 摘要
肥胖中的代谢失调会重塑免疫功能，但脂质信号如何驱动免疫抑制尚不清楚。本研究鉴定出一个 FABP4/PD-L1 轴，该轴将单核细胞和巨噬细胞中的脂质代谢与免疫检查点调节联系起来。单细胞转录组学揭示了一个在高脂饮食（HFD）条件下显著富集的独特 FABP4高表达（FABP4high）免疫抑制性巨噬细胞亚群，其特征为抗原呈递受损和 PD-L1 表达升高。机制上，棕榈酸（PA）诱导 FABP4 并促进 PD-L1 棕榈酰化，从而导致其在细胞表面的稳定，且该过程独立于转录调节。FABP4 对这一过程至关重要，它促进了 PD-L1 的表面稳定、免疫抑制和乳腺肿瘤进展。在人类中，一个保守的 CD14intCD16+ 单核细胞群体表现出升高的 FABP4/PD-L1 信号，并与肥胖及侵袭性乳腺癌相关。这些发现确立了 PD-L1 是一种受代谢调节的蛋白质，并揭示了脂质过量驱动免疫逃逸的机制，提示靶向 FABP4 可能会增强对免疫检查点阻断疗法的反应。

## Abstract
Metabolic dysregulation in obesity reshapes immune function, but how lipid signals drive immune suppression remains unclear. Here, we identify a FABP4/PD-L1 axis that links lipid metabolism to immune checkpoint regulation in monocytes and macrophages. Single-cell transcriptomics revealed a distinct FABP4high immunosuppressive macrophage subset enriched under high-fat diet (HFD) conditions, characterized by impaired antigen presentation and elevated PD-L1 expression. Mechanistically, palmitic acid (PA) induces FABP4 and promotes PD-L1 palmitoylation, leading to its stabilization on the cell surface independent of transcriptional regulation. FABP4 is essential for this process, which enables PD-L1 surface stabilization, immunosuppression and mammary tumor progression. In humans, a conserved CD14intCD16+; monocyte population exhibits elevated FABP4/PD-L1 signaling and correlates with obesity and invasive breast cancer. These findings establish PD-L1 as a metabolically regulated protein and reveal a mechanism by which lipid excess drives immune evasion, suggesting that targeting FABP4 may enhance responses to immune checkpoint blockade.
