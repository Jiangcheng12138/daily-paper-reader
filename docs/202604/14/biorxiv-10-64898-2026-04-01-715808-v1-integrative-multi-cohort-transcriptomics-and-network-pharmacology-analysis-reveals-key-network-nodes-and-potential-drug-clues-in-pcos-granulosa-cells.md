## 摘要
背景：多囊卵巢综合征（PCOS）是一种常见的内分泌疾病，具有复杂的病理生理机制且治疗选择有限。识别关键分子驱动因素和潜在候选药物对于改善临床预后至关重要。方法：我们整合了多队列转录组学数据（GSE155489、GSE138518、GSE226146），并结合加权基因共表达网络分析（WGCNA）、蛋白质-蛋白质相互作用（PPI）网络分析以及药物重定向研究。差异表达分析识别出1,039个差异表达基因（DEGs），WGCNA识别出10个PCOS相关模块。DEGs与模块基因的交集产生了498个核心候选基因，随后对其进行了功能富集、PPI网络分析以及基于连接图（Connectivity Map）的药物重定向（CLUE/LINCS）。通过三重交集策略（核心基因、高差异表达、药物-靶点证据），利用分子对接和ADMET预测对候选药物进行了进一步评估。结果：功能富集显示在细胞黏附和TGF-beta信号通路中显著富集。PPI网络分析识别出CD44为排名首位的核心基因（度值=42）。药物重定向识别出106种候选药物，包括曲格列酮和恩扎卢胺。利用三重交集策略，筛选出5个基因（ID2、NR4A1、GJA5、ID1、MYH11）优先进行分子对接。GJA5与氟芬那酸表现出较强的预测结合亲和力（-7.88 kcal/mol），而胞孢菌素B表现出良好的类药性（0次违反Lipinski规则）。结论：本研究系统地表征了PCOS相关的基因网络，并通过纯计算框架提供了一组优先的候选靶点和药物。CD44作为关键网络节点脱颖而出，在PCOS病理生理中具有潜在相关性。这些发现为未来PCOS的机制研究和药物开发提供了可验证的假设。

## Abstract
BackgroundPolycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is a prevalent endocrine disorder with complex pathophysiology and limited therapeutic options. Identifying key molecular drivers and potential drug candidates is critical for improving clinical outcomes.

MethodsWe integrated multi-cohort transcriptomics (GSE155489, GSE138518, GSE226146) with weighted gene co-expression network analysis (WGCNA), protein-protein interaction (PPI) network analysis, and drug repurposing. Differential expression analysis identified 1,039 DEGs, and WGCNA identified 10 PCOS-associated modules. Intersection of DEGs with module genes yielded 498 core candidate genes, which were subjected to functional enrichment, PPI network analysis, and connectivity map-based drug repurposing (CLUE/LINCS). Candidate drugs were further evaluated by molecular docking and ADMET prediction using a triple intersection strategy (hub genes, high differential expression, drug-target evidence).

ResultsFunctional enrichment revealed significant enrichment in cell adhesion and TGF-beta signaling. PPI network analysis identified CD44 as the top hub gene (degree=42). Drug repurposing identified 106 candidate drugs, including troglitazone and enzalutamide. Using the triple intersection strategy, five genes (ID2, NR4A1, GJA5, ID1, MYH11) were prioritized for molecular docking. GJA5 showed strong predicted binding affinity with flufenamic acid (-7.88 kcal/mol), and cytosporone B exhibited favorable druglikeness (0 Lipinski violations).

ConclusionThis study systematically characterizes PCOS-associated gene networks and provides a prioritized set of candidate targets and drugs through a purely computational framework. CD44 emerges as a key network node with potential relevance in PCOS pathophysiology. These findings offer testable hypotheses for future mechanistic studies and drug discovery efforts in PCOS.
