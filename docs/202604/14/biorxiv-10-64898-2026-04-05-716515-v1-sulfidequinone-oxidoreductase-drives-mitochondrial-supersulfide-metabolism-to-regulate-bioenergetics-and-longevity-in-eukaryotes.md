## 摘要
硫化氢:醌氧化还原酶 (SQR) 是维持硫代谢的关键酶，通过将硫化物氧化为超硫化物（目前定义为具有六个价电子且不带电荷、与其他硫原子共价连接且不包括二硫化物的硫代谢物）发挥作用。虽然已知 SQR 参与线粒体电子传递，但其对全身能量代谢和寿命的生理影响在很大程度上尚不明确。在本研究中，我们利用 SQR 缺陷型裂殖酵母 (Δhmt2) 和线粒体选择性 SQR 缺陷型 (SqrdlΔN/ΔN) 小鼠模型，研究了 SQR 在线粒体生物能量代谢和衰老中的作用。功能分析表明，Δhmt2 在葡萄糖中生长正常，但在甘油中生长受阻，表明其线粒体呼吸受损；此外，该菌株表现出膜电位降低、ATP 水平下降和寿命缩短。与酵母的研究结果一致，SqrdlΔN/ΔN 小鼠表现出硫化氢和过硫化物的积累，并显示出线粒体能量代谢受损。此外，补充超硫化物供体能选择性地延长野生型酵母的寿命，但在 SQR 缺陷株中无效；同样，它仅能改善野生型小鼠胚胎成纤维细胞的线粒体功能，而在 SQR 突变细胞中未观察到益处。总之，我们的研究结果表明，线粒体 SQR 在硫呼吸中发挥着至关重要的作用，对支持真核生物的线粒体功能和生物体寿命具有关键意义。研究亮点包括：开发了表现出硫代谢异常、线粒体功能障碍和历时寿命缩短的 SQR 缺陷型裂殖酵母模型；证实硫化物和超硫化物供体以 SQR 依赖的方式延长酵母寿命；明确了线粒体 SQR 对酵母和哺乳动物的膜电位形成及 ATP 产生至关重要。

## Abstract
Sulfide:quinone oxidoreductase (SQR) is a critical enzyme that maintains sulfur metabolism by oxidizing sulfide to supersulfides, currently defined as sulfur metabolites with six valence electrons and no charge that are covalently catenated with other sulfur atoms and excludes disulfides. While SQR is known to contribute to mitochondrial electron transport, its physiological impact on systemic energy metabolism and longevity remains largely undefined. In this study, we investigated the role of SQR in mitochondrial bioenergetics and aging using SQR-deficient Schizosaccharomyces pombe ({Delta}hmt2) and a mitochondria-selective SQR-deficient (Sqrdl{Delta}N/{Delta}N) mice model. Functional analysis demonstrated that{Delta} hmt2 grew normally in glucose but not in glycerol, indicating impaired mitochondrial respiration. It showed reduced membrane potential, ATP, and lifespan. Consistent with the yeast findings, Sqrdl{Delta}N/{Delta}N mice exhibited accumulated levels of hydrogen sulfide and persulfides, and demonstrated impaired mitochondrial energy metabolism. Furthermore, supersulfide donor supplementation selectively conferred lifespan extension in wild-type yeast, but not in SQR-deficient strain, and similarly improved mitochondrial function exclusively in wild-type mouse embryonic fibroblasts, with no benefit observed in SQR-mutant counterparts. Together, our findings demonstrate that mitochondrial SQR plays an essential role in sulfur respiration, critically supporting mitochondrial function and organismal longevity across eukaryotes.
