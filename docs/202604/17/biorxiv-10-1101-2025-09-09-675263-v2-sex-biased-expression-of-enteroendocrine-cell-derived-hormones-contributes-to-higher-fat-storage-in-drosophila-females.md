## 摘要
果蝇肠道中的肠内分泌（EE）细胞产生并释放多种因子，包括抑咽侧体素 A (AstA)、抑咽侧体素 C (AstC)、神经肽 F (NPF)、速激肽 (Tk)、利尿激素 31 (Dh31)、囊壳素 (Bursicon)、CCH 酰胺 1 (CCHa1) 和 CCH 酰胺 2 (CCHa2) 以及短神经肽 F (sNPF)。总的来说，这些肽类确保了生理机能（如脂肪储存、体液平衡）和行为（如进食、睡眠）与营养数量和质量等环境因素相协调。尽管在生理和行为上存在显著的性别差异，但这些 EE 细胞衍生因子的调节和功能在雌雄之间是否共有仍不清楚。鉴于近期数据发现两种 EE 细胞衍生激素对果蝇摄食和能量动员具有性别偏向性的生理影响，我们对雌雄果蝇中的这些激素进行了详细表征。尽管在全身和头部样本中，AstA、AstC、Tk、NPF 和 Dh31 的 mRNA 水平总体呈现雄性偏向，但我们观察到肠道中 AstC、Tk 和 NPF 的 mRNA 水平具有强烈的雌性偏向。为了确定这种性别偏向性调节是否具有生理意义，我们监测了肠道特异性缺失 EE 细胞衍生激素的果蝇的甘油三酯水平。在 5 日龄果蝇中，EE 细胞衍生的 AstC 或 Tk 的缺失降低了雌性的脂肪储存，而对雄性没有影响。这些对脂肪储存的雌性特异性影响在神经元特异性缺失 AstC 受体 (AstC-R2) 和 Tk 受体 (TkR99D) 的果蝇中得到了重现。总之，这些数据揭示了 EE 细胞衍生激素受强烈的性别偏向性调节，并表明其中两种激素的肠道特异性缺失对体脂具有雌性特异性影响。

研究亮点：
1. 肠内分泌细胞表达的激素表现出强烈的性别偏向性表达；
2. 肠内分泌细胞衍生的 AstC 和 Tk 的缺失仅降低了雌性的体脂；
3. 神经元缺失 AstC 或 Tk 受体仅降低了雌性的储存脂肪。

## Abstract
Enteroendocrine (EE) cells in the Drosophila gut produce and release multiple factors, including Allatostatin A (AstA), Allatostatin C (AstC), neuropeptide F (NPF), tachykinin (Tk), Diuretic hormone 31 (Dh31), Bursicon, CCHamide 1 (CCHa1) and CCHamide 2 (CCHa2), and short neuropeptide F (sNPF). Collectively, these peptides ensure that physiology (e.g., fat storage, fluid balance) and behavior (e.g., feeding, sleep) are coordinated with environmental factors such as nutrient quantity and quality. Despite notable sex differences in physiology and behavior, it remains unclear whether the regulation and function of these EE cell-derived factors is shared between males and females. Given that recent data identified sex-biased physiological effects of two EE cell-derived hormones on Drosophila food intake and energy mobilization, we performed a detailed characterization of these hormones in male and female flies. Despite an overall male bias in mRNA levels of AstA, AstC, Tk, NPF, Dh31 in whole-body and head samples, we observed a strong female bias in mRNA levels of AstC, Tk, and NPF in the gut. To determine whether this sex-biased regulation was physiologically significant, we monitored triglyceride levels in flies with gut-specific loss of EE cell-derived hormones. In 5-day-old flies, loss of either EE cell-derived AstC or Tk reduced fat storage in females with no effect in males. These female-specific effects on fat storage were reproduced in flies with neuron-specific loss of the AstC (AstC-R2) and Tk receptors (TkR99D). Together, these data uncover strongly sex-biased regulation of EE cell-derived hormones, and show that gut-specific loss of two of these hormones had a female-specific effect on body fat.
