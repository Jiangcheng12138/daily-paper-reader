## 摘要
背景：尽管采用了标准治疗方案——治疗性低温（TH），神经发育障碍在新生儿缺氧缺血性脑病（HIE）中仍然很常见。补体反应在再灌注时激活，已知会加剧神经炎症和损伤，但其完整作用以及与低温的相互作用尚未完全明确。我们假设调节补体反应可以改善 HIE 的结构和功能结果，并测试了一种新型补体疗法（CT），该疗法由 C3a 肽和 C5a 受体拮抗剂 PMX205 组成，既作为独立治疗手段，也作为 TH 的辅助疗法。方法：Wistar 幼鼠被随机分配到以下治疗组：假手术组（Sham，未受损对照）、常温组（NT，未受损、常温/未治疗对照），或受损后接受 TH、CT 或 CT+TH 治疗的组别。在足月等效期，采用 Vannucci 方法诱导轻中度缺氧缺血性损伤。为了捕捉治疗的短期和长期效果，分别在损伤后 3 天或 66-72 天采集样本。通过量化炎症标志物水平和脑组织丢失量来衡量脑损伤，并通过一系列行为测试评估功能结果。对数据进行分层以检测性别二态性。结果：与未治疗的受损大鼠相比，CT 和 TH 治疗在改善方面表现出测试依赖性和性别依赖性的差异。在雄性大鼠中，TH 治疗加重了长期的海马和丘脑脑损伤，以及共济失调和注意力的功能指标。CT 治疗加重了雌性大鼠长期的丘脑丢失。两种疗法结合（CT+TH）在两性中均表现出累加性的改善，包括短期和长期的皮质丢失以及共济失调。结论：补体调节增强了 TH 在新生儿缺氧缺血性损伤后的神经保护作用，且对炎症和行为的影响具有性别特异性。将补体调节与标准治疗相结合，通常在两性中均表现出协同改善，支持补体靶向治疗作为新生儿 HIE 中低温疗法的一种有前景的辅助手段。

## Abstract
BackgroundNeurodevelopmental impairment remains common in neonatal hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE) despite treatment with the standard of care, therapeutic hypothermia (TH). The complement response activates at reperfusion and is known to exacerbate neuroinflammation and injury, though its full role and interaction with hypothermia are incompletely defined. We hypothesized that modulating the complement response could improve structural and functional outcomes in HIE, and tested a novel complement therapy (CT), consisting of C3a peptides and the C5a-receptor antagonist PMX205, as both a stand-alone treatment and as an adjuvant to TH.

MethodsWistar rat pups were randomized to the following treatment groups: Sham (uninjured control), NT (uninjured, normothermia/not treated control), or injured and treated with either TH, CT, or CT+TH. At term-equivalence, mild-moderate hypoxic-ischemic injury was induced by Vannuccis method. To capture the short and long-term effects of the treatments, cohorts were harvested 3 or 66-72 days post-injury, respectively. Cerebral injury was measured by quantifying levels of inflammatory markers and cerebral tissue loss, and functional outcomes were assessed in a series of behavioral tests. The data were stratified to detect sexual dimorphisms.

ResultsCT and TH treatments demonstrated test and sex-dependent differences in improvement compared to untreated, injured rats. In male rats, TH treatment worsened long-term hippocampal and thalamic brain injury and functional measures of ataxia and attention. CT-treatment worsened long-term thalamic loss in females. Combining the two treatments (CT+TH) demonstrated additive improvement in both sexes, including short and long-term cortical loss and ataxia.

ConclusionsComplement modulation enhances the neuroprotective effects of TH after neonatal hypoxic-ischemic injury, with sex-specific effects on inflammation and behavior. Combining complement modulation with the standard of care often demonstrated synergistic improvement in both sexes, supporting complement-targeted therapy as a promising adjunct to hypothermia in neonatal HIE.
