## 摘要
阐明意识产生的底层机制仍然是现代神经科学面临的一项根本挑战。整合信息理论（IIT）提供了一个数学框架，该框架以意识的现象学特性作为公理推导而来。IIT 提出，意识等同于系统的内在因果信息结构，并由整合信息 {Phi} 进行量化。尽管 IIT 预测神经系统的 {Phi} 在意识丧失期间应该会降低，但这一假设在神经回路层面尚未得到验证。本研究为 IIT 的这一预测提供了实证支持。研究发现，与清醒状态和快速眼动（REM）睡眠相比，非快速眼动（NREM）睡眠期间局部回路内的 {Phi} 有所下降，且这种下降与皮层层级结构、放电率或脑区无关。在神经活动被集体抑制的“关闭期”（off-periods），{Phi} 的减少尤为显著。这些结果表明，意识是一种无法还原为单个系统元素属性（如放电率）的信息结构，其崩塌与意识的丧失有着根本的联系。这些发现为 IIT 作为一种旨在解释意识体验的数学理论提供了关键的实证支持。

意义声明：本研究弥合了抽象的意识数学理论与高分辨率神经生理学之间的鸿沟。根据整合信息理论（IIT），意识的存在取决于系统的内在因果结构。通过分析神经元群体的活动，本研究证明了从清醒到 NREM 睡眠的转变是以局部回路内整合信息（{Phi}）的减少为特征的。这种减少在 NREM 睡眠的“关闭期”最为明显，此时因果整合被有效切断，导致系统内在信息结构的崩溃。这些发现为 IIT 提供了神经科学基础，并表明意识是由大脑内特定的、不可还原的因果结构所支撑的。

## Abstract
Clarifying the mechanisms underlying the emergence of consciousness remains a fundamental challenge in modern neuroscience. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) provides a mathematical framework derived from the phenomenological properties of consciousness as its axioms. IIT proposes that consciousness is identical to a systems intrinsic cause-effect information structure, quantified by integrated information {Phi}. While IIT predicts that the {Phi} of a neuronal system should decrease during the loss of consciousness, this hypothesis has remained untested at the neural circuit level. The present study provides empirical support for this IIT prediction. It was found that {Phi} within local circuits decreases during non-rapid-eye-movement (NREM) sleep compared to wakefulness and REM sleep, independent of cortical laminar structure or firing rates or regions. The reduction in {Phi} was particularly pronounced during off-periods, when neural activity is collectively suppressed. These results imply that consciousness is an information structure that cannot be reduced to the properties of individual system elements (such as firing rates), and that its collapse is fundamentally linked to the loss of consciousness. The findings provide critical empirical support for IIT as a mathematical theory aiming to explain conscious experiences.

Significance StatementThis study bridges the gap between abstract mathematical theories of consciousness and high-resolution neurophysiology. According to Integrated Information Theory (IIT), conscious existence depends on a systems intrinsic cause-effect structure. By analyzing neural population activity, this study demonstrates that the transition from wakefulness to NREM sleep is characterized by a reduction in integrated information ({Phi}) within local circuits. This reduction is most pronounced during NREM off-periods, where causal integration is effectively severed, leading to a breakdown of the systems intrinsic information structure. These findings provide a neural foundation for IIT and suggest that consciousness is underpinned by specific, irreducible cause-effect structures within the brain.
