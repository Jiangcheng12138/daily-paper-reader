## 摘要
先验预期引导注意力并支持感知过滤，以实现决策过程中的高效处理。在此，我们展示了在视觉辨别任务中，小鼠通过估计连续试验之间证据的差异（|{triangleup}Dir|），利用先前的刺激历史来引导当前的决策。丘脑后外侧核（LP）/枕核向参与选择性注意和预测处理的前扣带回皮层（ACC）提供强有力的输入，但 LP-ACC 投射的功能尚不清楚。我们发现，对 LP-ACC 轴突的光遗传学操纵破坏了动物有效估计和利用跨刺激历史信息的能力，导致了依赖于 |{triangleup}Dir| 的偏差。对 LP-ACC 轴突的双光子钙成像揭示了刺激在弯曲流形上呈现出一种依赖于参与度的低维组织。这种表征受 |{triangleup}Dir| 的缩放，其方式强调了与先前证据之间更大的偏差。因此，我们的工作确定了 LP-ACC 通路对于根据先前证据选择和评估刺激以引导决策至关重要。

## Abstract
Prior expectations guide attention and support perceptual filtering for efficient processing during decision-making. Here we show that during a visual discrimination task, mice use prior stimulus history to guide ongoing choices by estimating differences in evidence between consecutive trials (|{triangleup}Dir|). The thalamic lateral posterior (LP)/pulvinar nucleus provides robust inputs to the Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC), which has been implicated in selective attention and predictive processing, but the function of the LP-ACC projection is unknown. We found that optogenetic manipulations of LP-ACC axons disrupted animals ability to effectively estimate and use information across stimulus history, leading to |{triangleup}Dir|-dependent biases. Two-photon calcium imaging of LP-ACC axons revealed an engagement-dependent low-dimensional organization of stimuli along a curved manifold. This representation was scaled by |{triangleup}Dir| in a manner that emphasized greater deviations from prior evidence. Thus, our work identifies the LP-ACC pathway as essential for selecting and evaluating stimuli relative to prior evidence to guide decisions.
