## 摘要
衰老是一个复杂的生物学过程，其特征是生理功能的逐渐衰退，这导致了对疾病和死亡的易感性增加。许多研究已在单细胞分辨率下探讨了衰老的细胞和分子层面，但个体内部细胞衰老的异质性仍不为人所知。为了增强我们在单细胞水平研究衰老的能力，我们开发了一个统计框架，根据转录组图谱预测单个细胞的年龄。我们的贝叶斯方法根据细胞的读取计数（read counts）估计其最可能的年龄。我们将该模型应用于 Tabula Muris Senis 的数据，并检查了器官和细胞类型特异性的衰老转录组特征。与标准的基于回归的方法相比，我们的框架实现了更高的预测准确性。我们证明了 scMLEAge 是剖析衰老和年龄相关功能衰退的细胞异质性的强大工具。

## Abstract
1Aging is a complex biological process marked by a gradual decline in physiological function that contributes to increased vulnerability to disease and mortality. Numerous studies have investigated the cellular and molecular aspects of aging at single-cell resolution, yet the heterogeneity of cellular aging in an individual remains poorly understood. To enhance our ability to study aging at the single cell level, we developed a statistical framework to predict the age of individual cells based on their transcriptomic profiles. Our Bayesian approach estimates the most likely age of a cell given its read counts. We applied the model to data from Tabula Muris Senis and examined organ- and cell-type-specific transcriptomic signatures of aging. Compared with standard regression-based methods, our framework achieved higher predictive accuracy. We show that scMLEAge is a powerful tool for dissecting the cellular heterogeneity of aging and age-related functional decline.
