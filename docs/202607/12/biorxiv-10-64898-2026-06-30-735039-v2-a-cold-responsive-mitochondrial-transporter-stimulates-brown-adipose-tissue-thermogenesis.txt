Abstract 51 

Metabolites are emerging as signaling molecules that mediate cellular 52 

function, extending beyond their well -established roles in metabolic pathways. 53 

Members of the solute carrier (SLC) family mediate metabolite transport 54 

across cellular compartments, raising the possibility that these proteins may 55 

sense environmental stimuli and regulate cellular biological processes by 56 

triggering signaling cascades linked to metabolite transport. This study 57 

investigated the response of the SLC25A family, a unique set of inner 58 

mitochondrial membrane -localized transporters , to cold as an environmental 59 

stimulus in mediating me tabolic reprogramming ; and whether this 60 

reprogramming, driven by the metabolites transported by SLC25A proteins, 61 

subsequently promotes the activation of thermogenesis in brown adipocytes. 62 

After screening members of the S LC 25A family for their responsivenes s to 63 

cold stimuli and brown adipose tissue (BAT) activation, we found that 64 

Slc25a34 was robustly induced under these conditions. We further 65 

demonstrated that Slc25a34 mediates the transport of adenosine 66 

monophosphate (AMP), derived from de novo glucose synthesis, from 67 

mitochondria to the cytosol. This transport potentiates AMP -activated protein 68 

kinase (AMPK) signaling and glycolytic flux in brown adipocyt es , both of which 69 

facilitate BAT thermogenesis during cold exposure. Intriguingly, cold exposure 70 

directly promoted the activation of peroxisome proliferator -activated receptor 71 

gamma (PPARγ), which transcriptionally upregulated Slc25a34 expression. 72 

More imp ortantly, genetic ablation of Slc25a34 impaired BAT thermogenesis. 73 

Thus, our study reveals a novel cold -induced metabolite -sensing pathway, 74 

where Slc25a34 -mediated AMP transport between mitochondria and the 75

Introduction 99 

In recent years, the concept of metabolites as signaling molecules in 100 

regulating cellular homeostasis has garnered significant attention 1, 2 .101 

Traditionally dismissed as mere intermediates in metabolic pathways, 102 

metabolites are now widely recognized for their critical roles in orchestrating 103 

cellular signaling and re gulatory processes 3. This paradigm shift has spurred 104 

growing interest in unraveling the interplay between metabolic states and 105 

cellular regulatory mechanisms 4, 5 . Emerging research indicates that a cell’s 106 

physiologic al state reflects a dynamic interplay between its regulatory systems 107 

and intermediary metabolism , a reciprocity where regulatory networks not only 108 

shape metabolic dynamics but are themselves modulated by feedback from 109 

metabolic states 6. This bidirectional crosstalk highlights metabolites as 110 

central hubs linking metabolism to function, yet many of its underlying 111 

mechanisms remain to be fully elucida ted. 112 

113 

Metabolic reprogramming mediates cellular signaling through diverse 114 

metabolite -sensing mechanisms, including direct binding to sensors, 115 

interaction with G protein -coupled receptors (GPCRs) 7, and regulation of 116 

protein modifications 8. However, while these mechanisms have been 117 

extensively characterized, the extent to which metabolite transport across 118 

subcellular compartments , particularly between mitochondria and the cytosol ,119 

drives signaling cascades remain poorly understood. 120 

121 

Mitochondria, often termed the “powerhouses of the cell,” play a pivotal role in 122 

cellular metabolism by regulating metabolite flux across their inner membrane ,123

tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle, oxidative phosphorylation, and fatty acid 125

mitochondrial metabolite transport in health and disease. 136

Against this backdrop, cold -induced thermogenesis in brown adipose tissue 138

metabolic processes and the regulation of gene expression. BAT is uniquely 140 

speciali zed to dissipate energy as heat either via uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1) 141 

or through several UCP1 -independent mechanisms , a process critical for 142 

maintaining core body temperature during cold exposure and regulating 143 

systemic energy expenditure 15 . Cold stimuli activate BAT through well -144 

cha racterized pathways, including the β -adrenergic receptor (β -AR) signaling 145 

cascade, which triggers downstream events such as cyclic AMP (cAMP) 146 

production and protein kinase A (PKA) activation 16 . Additionally, activation of 147 

the AMP -activated protein kinase (AMPK) pathway is pivotal for enhancing 148

To address this gap, we set out to identify mitochondrial transporters that 160 

bridge metabolic reprogramming and thermogenic signaling in BAT. We 161 

screened the RNA expre ssion profiles of SLC 25 A family genes in several 162 

cold -induced thermogenesis mouse models . This approach led to the 163 

identification of Slc25a34 as a gene whose expression is robustly induced by 164 

cold exposure and β -adrenergic receptor (β -AR) stimulation in BA T. 165 

Using Slc25a34 knockout mice, we demonstrated that S lc 25 a34 is 166 

indispensable for adaptive thermogenesis in BAT . Mechanistically, Slc25a34 -167 

deficient brown adipocytes exhibited disrupted purine metabolism due to 168

mitochondria to the cytosol. This defect hindered cytosolic AMPK 170 

phosphorylation and glycolytic activity, ultimately impai ring BAT thermogenic 171 

function .172

mediating metabolic crosstalk between mitochondria and the cytosol. Individual 642 

members of this family govern the transport of nucleotides, amino acids, and 643 

organic acids, thereby regulating cellular metabolism and influencing disease 644
