## 摘要
昼夜节律系统在自然光/暗循环中进化，而现代人大部分时间待在室内，在强度、光谱组成和时间结构上均与日光显著不同的人造电照明下。此类照明环境如何影响昼夜节律系统尚未在生态相关条件下的昼夜活动模型中系统研究。本研究使用昼夜活动的尼罗河草鼠(Arvicanthis niloticus)，评估四种近似常见人类暴露场景的照明条件下的日常运动节律：矩形日光(D65-R；约5,600 lux)、模拟自然强度动态的半S形日光(D65-S；匹配峰值强度但累积能量低约50%)、荧光室内光(F12；约150 lux)，以及补充一小时正午日光脉冲的荧光灯(F12+D65-P)。采用受试者内设计(n=8)，雄性草鼠在每个条件下饲养两周。D65-R产生最高的白天活动水平和最强的昼夜活动比，与稳健的昼夜节律夹带一致。尽管匹配峰值强度，D65-S并未产生相当的昼夜节律结果，表明累积光子暴露而非仅峰值强度有助于夹带强度。值得注意的是，在其他方面为荧光条件下，添加一小时正午日光脉冲(D65-P)部分增加了昼夜节律幅度，与单独F12相比周期图幅度更高。另一组雄性暴露于D65-R或F12六周(每组n=10)以评估生理结果，包括代谢和生殖指标。与D65-R组相比，F12组表现出更高的糖尿病率(40% vs. 10%)和降低的精子活力(19% vs. 45%)，这与昼夜节律紊乱的潜在下游后果一致。总之，这些发现表明，室内环境特征的照明条件较等效日光照明的昼夜活动啮齿动物产生更弱的昼夜节律组织，这强调了光照质量在塑造昼夜节律及下游生理过程中的重要性。

## Abstract
The circadian system evolved under natural light/dark cycles, whereas modern humans spend much of their time indoors under electric lighting that differs substantially from daylight in intensity, spectral composition, and temporal structure. How such lighting environments influence the circadian system has not been systematically examined in a diurnal model under ecologically relevant conditions. In this study, we used the diurnal Nile grass rat (Arvicanthis niloticus) to assess daily locomotor rhythms across four lighting conditions designed to approximate common human exposure scenarios: rectangular daylight (D65-R; ~5,600 lux), semi-sigmoidal daylight mimicking natural intensity dynamics (D65-S; matched peak intensity with ~50% lower cumulative energy), fluorescent indoor light (F12; ~150 lux), and fluorescent light supplemented with a one hour midday daylight pulse (F12+D65-P). Using a within subject design (n = 8), male grass rats were housed under each condition for two weeks. D65 R produced the highest daytime activity levels and the strongest day/night activity ratio, consistent with robust circadian entrainment. Despite matching peak intensity, D65 S did not yield comparable circadian outcomes, indicating that cumulative photon exposure, rather than peak intensity alone, contributes to entrainment strength. Notably, the addition of a one hour midday daylight pulse (D65-P) partially increased circadian amplitude under otherwise fluorescent conditions, with higher periodogram amplitude relative to F12 alone. Another cohort of males was exposed to D65-R or F12 for six weeks (n = 10/condition) to assess physiological outcomes, including metabolic and reproductive measures. Compared with the D65-R group, the F12 group exhibited a higher diabetic rate (40% vs. 10%) and reduced sperm motility (19 vs. 45 %), consistent with potential downstream consequence of circadian rhythm disruption. Together, these findings demonstrate that lighting conditions characteristic of indoor environments produce weaker circadian organization than daylight equivalent lighting in a diurnal rodent, which underscores the importance of light quality in shaping circadian rhythms and downstream physiological processes.
