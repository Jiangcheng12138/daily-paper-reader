## 摘要
大脑在巩固广泛记忆的同时，如何在经历间保持其独特性的能力仍不清楚。尖波涟漪——在不活动和睡眠期间主要发生在海马CA1区的神经振荡——已被证明在巩固过程中起关键作用。近期证据揭示了CA1不同亚层内锥体神经元的功能异质性，这些神经元在涟漪期间表现出独特特性，可能有助于记忆特异性。尽管如此，涟漪如何改变CA1神经元群体的活动以适应特定记忆的巩固，以及亚层差异如何体现，仍不清楚。本研究探讨了前扣带回皮层（ACC）与CA1神经元在涟漪期间的相互作用，并发现了学习后它们通讯的重组。具体而言，利用广义线性模型解码器，我们证明了ACC到CA1的通讯预先存在，而在学习后的训练后睡眠期间这种通讯减弱，表明ACC活动在记忆形成过程中重新分配了CA1神经元的贡献。有趣的是，这种重组似乎仅对一组任务不活跃的CA1浅层（CA1sup）神经元是独特的，而ACC与CA1深层神经元之间的通讯在训练前后睡眠中基本保持稳定。与这种亚层选择性重组一致，我们发现对ACC的光遗传刺激优先抑制CA1sup神经元，同时激活一组独特的CA1中间神经元。总体而言，这些发现强调了ACC在平衡CA1神经元群体对学习和记忆巩固的贡献中的重要作用。

## Abstract
The brains ability to consolidate a wide range of memories while maintaining their distinctiveness across experiences remains poorly understood. Sharp-wave ripples, neural oscillations that occur predominantly within CA1 of the hippocampus during immobility and sleep, have been shown to play a critical role in the consolidation process. More recently, evidence has uncovered functional heterogeneity of pyramidal neurons within distinct sublayers of CA1 that display unique properties during ripples, potentially contributing to memory specificity. Despite this, it remains unclear exactly how ripples shift the activity of CA1 neuronal populations to accommodate the consolidation of specific memories and how sublayer differences manifest. Here, we studied interactions between the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) and CA1 neurons during ripples and discovered a reorganization of their communication following learning. Specifically, using a generalized linear model decoder, we demonstrated the pre-existence of ACC-to-CA1 communication, which is weakened during post-training sleep following learning, suggesting that ACC activity reallocates the contribution of CA1 neurons during memory formation. Interestingly, the reorganization appeared unique for a subset of CA1 superficial (CA1sup) neurons that were task inactive, whereas communication between the ACC and CA1 deep neurons remained largely stable across pre- and post-training sleep. Consistent with this sublayer-selective reorganization, we found that optogenetic stimulations of the ACC preferentially suppressed CA1sup neurons while activating a unique subset of CA1 interneurons. Overall, these findings highlight an important role of the ACC in rebalancing CA1 neuronal populations contribution in learning and memory consolidation.
