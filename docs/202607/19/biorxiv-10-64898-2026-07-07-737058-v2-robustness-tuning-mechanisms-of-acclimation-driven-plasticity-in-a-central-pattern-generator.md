## 摘要
温度影响神经和环路输出，极端温度可破坏神经性能。适应作用引发一种我们称之为鲁棒性调谐的神经可塑性形式，在环境条件的季节性变化中保持神经系统性能。美国龙虾（Homarus americanus）的 stomatogastric 神经系统（STNS）产生刻板的节律性运动模式，这些模式在一系列急性温度变化中得以维持，但在更极端条件下丧失。在野外，H. americanus 一年中经历水温从约2°C到25°C的变化。我们将龙虾分别在18°C和4°C下驯化约3周，发现与冷驯化动物相比，温驯化动物的幽门节律在更宽的温度范围内保持其特征性特性。幽门神经元对神经肽甲壳类心活性肽（CCAP）的响应存在驯化和温度依赖性差异。计算模型表明，幽门神经元形态和神经调节素电导分布在鲁棒性调谐中发挥作用，这种可逆变化使动物能够反复适应季节性变化。

## Abstract
Temperature influences neuronal and circuit output and extreme temperatures can disrupt neuronal performance. Acclimation invokes a form of neuronal plasticity that we call  robustness tuning, that preserves nervous system performance during seasonal alterations in environmental conditions. The stomatogastric nervous system (STNS) of the American lobster, Homarus americanus, produces stereotyped rhythmic motor patterns that are maintained over a range of acute temperature changes, but lost under more extreme conditions. In the wild, H. americanus experience water temperatures from [~]2{degrees}C to 25{degrees}C during the course of a year. We acclimated lobsters to 18{degrees}C versus 4{degrees}C for [~]3 weeks, and found that the pyloric rhythm from warm-acclimated animals maintained its characteristic properties over an extended temperature range, when compared to those recorded from cold-acclimated animals. There were acclimation and temperature dependent differences in the responses of pyloric neurons to the neuropeptide, Crustacean Cardioactive Peptide (CCAP). Computational models suggest that pyloric neuron morphology and neuromodulator conductance distribution play a role in robustness tuning, the reversible changes that allow animals to repeatedly adapt to seasonal change.
