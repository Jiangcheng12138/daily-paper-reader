## 摘要
栖息在极寒或极热环境中的动物的适应性为体温调节提供了独特的见解。羚松鼠是一种栖息在沙漠中的啮齿动物，处于冬眠动物的一个演化支系中，是这些体温调节极端情况的一个显著范例。作为与冬眠动物亲缘关系密切的非冬眠动物，羚松鼠可能代表了一个罕见的性状逆转案例，它们具有维持超过 43°C 核心体温的惊人能力，但对长期寒冷的耐受性较差。在本研究中，我们通过生成该物种的首个基因组组装，并利用其在松鼠科中进行比较基因组学研究，探索了这些独特特征的遗传和表型基础。我们还辅以急性及慢性冷暴露实验，并结合产热器官（棕色脂肪组织、白色脂肪组织和骨骼肌）的转录组分析。总之，这些发现揭示了产热方式从非战栗产热向代谢需求更高的战栗产热的转变，这是一种用于持续产热的危险策略。

## Abstract
Adaptations in animals occupying environments of extreme cold or heat offer unique insights into thermoregulation. The antelope ground squirrel, a desert-dwelling rodent situated within a clade of hibernators, provides a notable example of these thermoregulatory extremes. As a non-hibernator closely related to hibernators, antelope ground squirrels may represent a rare case of trait reversal, with a striking ability to maintain core body temperatures exceeding 43{degrees}C while displaying poor tolerance to prolonged cold. Here, we explore the genetic and phenotypic basis of these unique traits by generating the first genome assembly for this species, which we use to conduct comparative genomics across the squirrel family. We complement this with acute and chronic cold-exposure experiments coupled with transcriptomic profiling of thermogenic organs: brown adipose tissue, white adipose tissue, and skeletal muscle. Together, these findings reveal a shift away from non-shivering thermogenesis toward metabolically demanding shivering thermogenesis, a perilous strategy for sustained heat generation.
