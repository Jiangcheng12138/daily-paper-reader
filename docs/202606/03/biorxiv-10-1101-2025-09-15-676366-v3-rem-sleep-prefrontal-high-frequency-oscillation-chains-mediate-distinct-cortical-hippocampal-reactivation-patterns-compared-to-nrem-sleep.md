## 摘要
快速眼动（REM）和非快速眼动（NREM）睡眠阶段在海马-皮层回路中促成系统记忆巩固。然而，与NREM记忆再激活相比，REM记忆过程的生理机制仍相对不清楚。在此我们报告，在啮齿动物中，近期获得的空间记忆巩固期间，REM睡眠中存在前额叶皮层（PFC）高频振荡（HFO）链。海马CA1区和PFC的高密度四极管记录显示，REM皮层HFO以特征性链的形式出现，并受到θ振荡的相位调制，对应于CA1-PFC θ相干性的增加，并描绘出海马-皮层通信增强的时期。REM HFO链在局部抑制期间顺序组织了稀疏的PFC集合体行为活动再激活，这与NREM尖波涟漪振荡期间的广泛再激活爆发不同。REM HFO链还优先招募CA1神经元群体，这些神经元在从行为到REM睡眠的转变中表现出其偏好的θ相位偏移。REM HFO链期间的CA1神经元激活与NREM PFC涟漪期间的CA1活动抑制相关，并与睡眠中CA1放电率的变化差异相关，表明REM驱动的海马兴奋性调节。一个结合乙酰胆碱效应的皮层网络模型可以重现不同的REM和NREM活动模式，这为NREM皮层涟漪期间的广泛共活动提供了机制基础，而REM HFO链期间则是在局部抑制背景下的稀疏、时间延长的再激活。总体而言，这些发现确立了前额叶高频振荡在调节不同的双重睡眠阶段再激活模式中的作用。

## Abstract
REM (rapid eye movement) and non-REM (NREM) sleep stages contribute to systems memory consolidation in hippocampal-cortical circuits. However, the physiological mechanisms underlying REM memory processes remain relatively unclear compared to NREM memory reactivation. Here we report, in rodents, the existence of prefrontal cortical (PFC) high-frequency oscillation (HFO) chains in REM sleep during consolidation of recently acquired spatial memory. High-density tetrode recordings in hippocampal area CA1 and PFC reveal that REM cortical HFOs occur in characteristic chains that are phase modulated by theta oscillations, corresponding to increased CA1-PFC theta coherence and delineating periods of enhanced hippocampal-cortical communication. REM HFO chains sequentially organize sparse PFC ensemble reactivation of behavioral activity during periods of local suppression, distinct from widespread reactivation bursts during NREM ripple oscillations. REM HFO chains also preferentially engage CA1 neuronal populations that demonstrate a shift in their preferred theta-phase from behavior to REM sleep. CA1 neuronal activation during REM HFO chains was correlated with CA1 activity suppression during NREM PFC ripples, and linked to differential changes in CA1 firing rates in sleep, suggesting REM-driven regulation of hippocampal excitability. A cortical network model incorporating the effects of acetylcholine can reproduce the distinct REM and NREM activity patterns, providing a mechanistic basis for widespread coactivity during NREM cortical ripples, compared to sparse, temporally extended reactivation on a background of local suppression during REM HFO chains. Overall, these findings establish a role for PFC high-frequency oscillations in regulating distinct dual sleep stage reactivation patterns.
