## 摘要
新兴传染病对野生动物构成严重威胁，而气候变化可能进一步加剧其发生。本研究旨在探讨是否可以利用冬眠地点的当地气候条件和宿主物种丰度来预测白鼻病（一种冬眠蝙蝠的真菌疾病）的发生。此外，我们利用模型预测了病原体引入后可能面临风险的区域，并研究了未来气候变化如何导致白鼻病潜在分布的变化。我们采用逻辑回归作为生态位建模方法的一部分，将气候和普查数据作为解释变量，以及从448个冬眠洞穴获得的疾病状况（响应变量）整合在一起。这种方法使我们能够将这些气候变量应用于当前全球气候条件和气候变化情景，预测白鼻病高风险区域。结合年均地表温度、降水量和三种宿主物种数据的模型能够可靠地预测欧洲白鼻病的发生。该模型在欧洲以外的区域表现出强大的可迁移性，理论和实证评估均证实了这一点（例如，准确预测了北美观察到的死亡事件）。我们确定了南半球的几个高风险区域，并表明气候变化可能导致白鼻病分布范围的显著变化。我们的结果强调了环境因素在控制病原体和适宜宿主同时存在的地区疾病表现中的重要性。我们指出了几个需要加强监测和预防措施以避免病原体引入的区域，并表明气候变化具有重塑和扩大这些区域的巨大潜力，使新的种群面临风险。

## Abstract
Emerging infectious diseases pose a serious threat to wildlife, and their occurrence will likely be further exacerbated due to climate change. The aim of our study was to investigate whether the occurrence of White-nose disease (WND), a fungal disease of hibernating bats, can be predicted using local climatic conditions and host species abundance at hibernation sites. In addition, we used our model to predict areas potentially at risk if the pathogen is introduced and investigated how the potential distribution of WND may shift in the future due to climate change. We employed logistic regression as part of our ecological niche modelling approach, integrating climate and census data as explanatory variables, along with WND status (the response variable) obtained from 448 hibernacula. This approach allowed us to predict regions at elevated risk of WND by applying these climatic variables to current global climatic conditions and a climate change scenario. A model incorporating data on mean annual surface temperature, precipitation, and three host species reliably predicted WND occurrence in Europe. This model demonstrated robust transferability beyond Europe, as confirmed by both theoretical and empirical assessments (e.g., accurately predicting the observed mortality events in North America). We identified several high-risk areas in the southern hemisphere and demonstrate that climate change may cause a remarkable shift in the distribution range of WND. Our results highlight the importance of environmental factors in controlling the manifestation of disease in localities where both the pathogen and suitable hosts are present. We pinpoint several areas requiring increased surveillance and precautions to avoid the introduction of the pathogen, and show that climate change has massive potential to reshape and expand these areas, putting new populations at risk.
