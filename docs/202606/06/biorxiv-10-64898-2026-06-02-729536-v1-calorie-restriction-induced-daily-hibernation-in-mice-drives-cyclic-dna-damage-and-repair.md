## 摘要
冬眠由一系列蛰伏期（其特征为代谢和体温显著降低）与称为间歇觉醒的常温期交替组成，在间歇觉醒期间，正常代谢和体温得以恢复。季节性冬眠动物在蛰伏期间积累DNA链断裂，这些断裂在觉醒期间被修复。本文评估了由30%热量限制诱导的小鼠每日连续蛰伏过程中DNA损伤与修复的动态变化，并研究了代谢挑战对DNA修复的影响。在室温20°C下饲养的C57/BL6J雌雄小鼠中，由CR诱导的每日连续蛰伏持续6-12小时。与季节性冬眠动物类似，CR诱导的蛰伏期间DNA损伤增加，并在随后的常温期得到修复，这通过彗星实验和γH2AX积累得以证实。为了让动物面临代谢挑战，将环境温度降至4°C，因为蛰伏小鼠维持体温约20°C或更高。尽管引发了显著的代谢挑战，但与20°C环境相比，将蛰伏小鼠置于4°C并不会增加DNA损伤。然而，在常温期将环境温度降至4°C会抑制DNA修复。有趣的是，暴露于4°C的小鼠中p21水平升高，表明在暴露于4°C期间发生了细胞周期抑制。因此，30%的热量限制在小鼠中诱导了蛰伏引起的DNA损伤与常温期相关DNA修复的每日循环，且在觉醒期间暴露于4°C的环境温度会抑制DNA修复并引发细胞周期抑制反应。因此，蛰伏-觉醒循环可能是热量限制延长小鼠寿命的一个促成因素，通过促进基因组完整性进而维护细胞和组织健康。

## Abstract
Hibernation consists of bouts of torpor, characterized by profound decreases in metabolism and body temperature (Tb), alternated with periods of euthermia called interbout arousals, during which normal metabolism and Tb resume. Seasonal hibernators accumulate DNA strand breaks during torpor, which are repaired during arousal. Here, we assess dynamics of DNA damage and repair during serial daily torpor in mice induced by 30% calorie restriction (CR) and investigate the effects of metabolic challenge on DNA repair. Serial daily torpor induced by CR in C57/BL6J mice of both sexes housed at 20{ring}C lasts 6-12 hours. Like seasonal hibernators, DNA damage increases in CR-induced torpor and is repaired in the subsequent euthermic period, as evidenced by comet assay and yH2AX accumulation. To metabolically challenge animals, ambient temperature (Ta) was lowered to 4{ring}C, since torpid mice defend a Tb of around 20{ring}C or higher. Despite inducing a significant metabolic challenge, housing of torpid mice at 4{ring}C does not increase DNA damage compared to 20{ring}C housing. However, reducing Ta to 4{ring}C during euthermia inhibits DNA repair. Interestingly, p21 levels increase in mice exposed to 4{ring}C, indicating cell-cycle inhibition during exposure to 4{ring}C. Thus, 30% CR induces daily cycles of torpor-induced DNA damage and euthermia-associated DNA repair in mice, and exposure to a Ta of 4{ring}C during arousal inhibits DNA repair mounting a cell cycle inhibition response. Thus, the torpor-arousal cycle may be a contributing factor to the lifespan extension benefits of CR in mice, promoting genomic integrity and thereby cellular and tissue health.
