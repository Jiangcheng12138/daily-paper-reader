## 摘要
REM睡眠似乎参与情绪记忆的处理，但其参与的性质和神经基础仍不清楚。最新发现提示，REM相关的θ活动可能发挥核心作用。为验证这一点，我们开发了一种自动化方案，将非唤醒性声音记忆线索精确投放到REM睡眠θ振荡的特定相位。在睡眠期间，经历过恐惧条件化结合记忆回忆和情绪评估任务的参与者，被重新暴露于条件刺激，且刺激时机与θ波的上升或下降相位同步。上升和下降相位定向的记忆线索均显著增强了θ动态。此外，与假再激活（无声音记忆线索）相比，θ相位锁定的记忆再激活显著降低了条件化记忆的情绪基调，且不影响识别表现或梦境质量。这构成第一个证据，表明REM睡眠θ振荡因果性地参与情绪记忆处理，特别是减弱记忆的情绪负荷。这些发现对于理解情绪记忆巩固具有重要意义，并可能为以适应不良性情绪记忆为特征的疾病带来治疗应用。

## Abstract
REM sleep appears to be involved in emotional memory processing, yet the nature and neural underpinnings of this involvement remain largely unclear. Recent findings suggest REM-related theta activity may play a central role. To test this, we developed an automated protocol to target non-arousing acoustic memory cues to specific phases of REM sleep theta oscillations. During sleep, participants who had undergone a fear conditioning procedure combined with a memory recollection and emotional evaluation task, were re-exposed to conditioned stimuli, timed to either the upswing or downswing of theta waves. Both up- and down- targeted memory cues significantly enhanced the theta dynamic. Moreover, theta phase-locked memory re-activation significantly reduced the emotional tone of conditioned memories com-pared to sham reactivation (no acoustic memory cue), without affecting recognition performance or dream qualities. This constitutes the first evidence that REM sleep theta oscillations are causally involved in emotional memory processing, specifically in attenuating memories' emotional charge. These findings have important implica-tions for understanding emotional memory consolidation and may lead to therapeutic applications in disorders featuring maladaptive emotional memories.
