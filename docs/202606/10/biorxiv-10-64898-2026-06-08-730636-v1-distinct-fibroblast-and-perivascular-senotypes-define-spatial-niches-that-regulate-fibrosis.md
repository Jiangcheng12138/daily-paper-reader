## 摘要
纤维化疾病导致显著的全球发病率和死亡率。然而，由于调节纤维化疾病进展的基质、免疫和血管区室的细胞和空间复杂性，协调纤维化的潜在过程仍知之甚少。衰老细胞（SnCs）与纤维化有关，但其作用尚不清楚，因为有证据表明它们既具有致病功能又具有修复功能。在这里，我们展示了与纤维化相关的SnCs包含功能上不同的衰老表型，这些表型被组织成不同的空间微环境。通过在小鼠纤维化模型中使用整合的单细胞和空间转录组学分析以及层次因子分解，我们识别出成纤维细胞和血管周围SnC亚群，这些亚群上调与细胞外基质（ECM）产生、免疫信号传导和血管重塑相关的多种程序。成纤维细胞衰老表型定位于具有不同组织结构的离散微环境中，包括与纤维化信号、免疫活性和软骨发育相关的微环境。血管周围SnCs占据纤维化信号和免疫活性微环境之间的界面，并上调血管和纤维化重塑途径。清除周细胞谱系SnCs增加了血管成熟和纤维化ECM沉积，为这些SnCs在血管重塑和纤维化调节中的有益作用提供了机制验证。此外，利用一个新的基于Web的基础设施来查询公共数据集中的衰老表型基因特征，我们证明了这些衰老表型在不同的小鼠和人类纤维化状况中是保守的。这些发现将衰老确立为纤维化的空间组织调节因子，并将血管周围衰老确定为血管重塑与纤维化结果之间的联系。

## Abstract
Fibrotic conditions contribute to significant global morbidity and mortality. Yet the underlying processes that orchestrate fibrosis remain poorly understood due to the cellular and spatial complexity of the stromal, immune, and vascular compartments that regulate fibrotic disease progression. Senescent cells (SnCs) have been implicated in fibrosis, but their roles are unclear, as evidence indicates that they serve both pathogenic and reparative functions. Here, we show that fibrosis-associated SnCs contain functionally divergent senotypes that are organized into distinct spatial niches. Using integrated single-cell and spatial transcriptomics analyses and hierarchical factorization in a murine fibrosis model, we identify fibroblast and perivascular SnC subpopulations that upregulate diverse programs related to extracellular matrix (ECM) production, immune signaling, and vascular remodeling. Fibroblast senotypes localize to discrete microenvironments with distinct tissue architectures, including niches associated with fibrotic signaling, immune activity, and cartilage development. Perivascular SnCs occupy interfaces between fibrotic signaling and immune-active niches and upregulate vascular and fibrotic remodeling pathways. Depletion of pericyte-lineage SnCs increases vascular maturation and fibrotic ECM deposition, providing mechanistic validation of the beneficial role of these SnCs related to vascular remodeling and fibrosis modulation. In addition, using a new web-based infrastructure to query the senotype gene signatures in public datasets, we demonstrate that these senotypes are conserved across different murine and human fibrotic conditions. These findings establish senescence as a spatially organized regulator of fibrosis and identify perivascular senescence as a link between vascular remodeling and fibrotic outcomes.
