## 摘要
脓毒症的一个持久悖论是，尽管几乎没有不可逆组织损伤的证据，器官却出现衰竭。新证据表明，这种状态反映了宿主组织内受调控的代谢关闭，但这种代谢低下是否导致病理或促进生存仍不清楚。这种现象类似于休眠，一种由环境应激诱导并通过可逆蛋白磷酸化介导的深度代谢低下生理状态。本文确定脓毒症代谢低下是一种保守的组织特异性代谢适应，其特征是糖原合酶激酶3β (GSK3β) 的瞬时激活。这种激活降低了细菌性脓毒症的疾病严重程度，但不影响宿主的病原体负荷，表明GSK3β活性促进了对感染的疾病耐受性。与这些发现一致，与GSK3β抑制相关的血浆特征与脓毒症患者较差的临床结局相关。总之，这些结果将脓毒症代谢低下定义为一种类似休眠的反应，并确定可逆磷酸化是宿主适应严重细菌感染的关键机制。

## Abstract
One of the enduring paradoxes of sepsis is that organs fail despite little evidence of irreversible tissue injury. Emerging evidence suggests that this state reflects a regulated metabolic shutdown within host tissues, yet whether such hypometabolism contributes to pathology or promotes survival remains unclear. This phenomenon resembles torpor, a physiological state of profound hypometabolism induced by environmental stress and mediated through reversible protein phosphorylation. Septic hypometabolism is identified here as a conserved tissue-specific metabolic adaptation which is characterized by transient activation of Glycogen Synthase Kinase (GSK)3{beta}. This activation reduced disease severity of bacterial sepsis without affecting the hosts pathogen burden, indicating that GSK3{beta} activity promotes disease tolerance to infection. Consistent with these findings, plasma signatures associated with GSK3{beta} inhibition correlated with worse clinical outcomes in patients with sepsis. Together, these results define septic hypometabolism as a torpor-like response and identify reversible phosphorylation as a key mechanism governing host adaptation to severe bacterial infection.
