Abstract 35 Ovarian disorders, including anovulation, primary ovarian insufficiency (POI), and 36 polyendocrine metabolic ovarian syndrome (PMOS), affect millions of reproductive-age 37 women worldwide; however, mechanistic studies of ovarian biology and 38 pathophysiology remain challenging because current experimental approaches often 39 lack selectivity, tunability, or physiological relevance. Genetically modified animal 40 models are labor-intensive and irreversible; small molecules frequently exhibit off-target 41 effects; and conventional antibodies have limited tissue penetration and restricted 42 temporal control. Designed ankyrin repeat proteins (DARPins) represent a highly 43 modular protein engineering platform with advantages in specificity, size, stability, and 44 extracellular targeting, but their utility in reproductive biology remains largely unexplored. 45 Here, we used epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR)-targeting DARPins as a proof-46 of-concept platform to interrogate ovarian signaling during ovulation. Screening of 47 engineered anti-EGFR DARPins identified SX-006, a bispecific tetravalent construct 48 with robust cross-species EGFR binding and potent biological activity. Using an ex vivo 49 murine ovulation system, SX-006 inhibited follicle rupture in a dose-dependent manner 50 with IC50 of 1.21 μM without overt cytotoxicity. Lower concentrations of SX-006 51 preferentially perturbed follicle rupture while largely preserving oocyte meiotic 52 maturation and luteinization, suggesting differential sensitivity of ovulatory processes to 53 extracellular EGFR blockade. Comparative transcriptomic analyses further revealed that 54 extracellular EGFR blockade and small molecule-based intracellular EGFR kinase 55 inhibition produce overlapping but also distinct transcriptional responses, supporting 56 biologically distinct modes of ovulatory signaling pathway perturbation. Introduction 85 Up to 15-20% of women of reproductive age experience infertility, with ovarian 86 disorders as a leading cause, including primary ovarian insufficiency (POI), polycystic 87 ovary syndrome (PCOS, now termed polyendocrine metabolic ovarian syndrome or 88 PMOS), anovulation, and aneuploidy[1-3]. Despite its importance, mechanistic studies 89 of ovarian biology and pathophysiology remain challenging due to the lack of precise 90 and scalable approaches. Genetically modified animal models have provided critical 91 insights; however, the process is time-consuming, costly, and not readily adaptable for 92 reversible or dose-dependent interrogation of targeted signaling pathways. Small 93 molecules are often limited by pharmacokinetic constraints, including rapid clearance 94 and variable bioavailability [4]. Small molecules also often exhibit off-target effects, 95 complicating the interpretation of complex signaling networks governing biological 96 functions [5, 6]. The relatively large antibodies exhibit high specificity targeting ligand– 97 receptor interactions. However, they restrict tissue penetration within complex ovarian 98 structures. Moreover, the Fc-mediated effector function and long systemic half-life may 99 introduce confounding biological effects and limit temporal control of targeted signaling 100 [7]. The antibody engineering and production can also be costly and less amenable to 101 rapid iteration. Collectively, these limitations underscore a critical need for new tools that 102 enable selective, tunable, and physiologically relevant modulation of ovarian signaling. 103 104 Designed Ankyrin Repeat Proteins (DARPins) emerge as a precision targeting 105 tool to address the limitations of both small molecules and monoclonal antibodies [8, 9]. 106 DARPins are highly stable, engineered scaffold proteins derived from naturally 107

Compared with traditional monoclonal antibodies, DARPins lack 111 disulfide bonds and Fc regions, allowing for cost-effective, high-yield expression in 112

This architectural advantage enables 118 specific DARPin constructs to potently inhibit receptor recycling and down-regulate 119 surface receptors [12]. The binding of LH to its membrane receptor LHCGR 131 induces EGF-like ligands in granulosa cells, including amphiregulin (AREG), epiregulin 132 (EREG), and betacellulin (BTC)[18-20]. These EGF ligands bind to EGFR and activate 133 extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK) signaling cascades, which are essential for 134 cumulus expansion, oocyte meiotic resumption, and follicle rupture. So far, most studies 135 investigating EGFR signaling rely on genetically modified mouse models or small-136 molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitors [21-23]. While these models disrupt EGFR 137 expression or intracellular kinase activity, they do not target ligand-dependent EGFR 138 activation and often broadly affect downstream signaling pathways. Moreover, EGFR 139 ligands bind to the extracellular domain of EGFR; thus, experimental tools that 140 selectively block extracellular ligand-EGFR interactions may provide a precise strategy 141 to elucidate EGF ligand-EGFR-mediated ovulatory signaling. 142 143 In the present study, we used anti-EGFR DARPins in combination with a 3D in vitro 144 ovarian follicle growth (IVFG) and ex vivo ovulation system as a novel platform to study 145 ovarian signaling during ovulation. We demonstrate that by selectively targeting the 146 extracellular ligand-receptor interactions, anti-EGFR DARPin inhibits ovulation in a 147 dose-dependent manner without overt cytotoxicity and enables precise modulation of 148 EGFR signaling distinct from a small-molecule EGFR inhibitor that suppresses 149 intracellular EGFR kinase activity. Our findings establish DARPin as a versatile and 150 tunable platform for studying ovarian signaling and highlight its therapeutic potential for 151 ovarian disorders, infertility, and the development of non-hormonal contraceptives. 152 153

MATERIALS AND METHODS 154 

Cloning, Expression, and Purification of anti-EGFR DARPins 155 The design and recombinant expression strategy for anti-EGFR DARPins were 156 adapted from previously established protocols [13]. The genes encoding the anti-EGFR 157 DARPins were optimized for E. coli expression and cloned into the pET28a(+) 158 expression vector under the control of a T7 promoter. Each construct was designed with 159 an N-terminal 6xHis-tag followed by a tobacco etch virus (TEV) protease cleavage site 160 to facilitate affinity purification. Plasmids were transformed into E. coli BL21(DE3) 161 competent cells. Bacterial cultures were grown in Luria-Bertani (LB) medium 162 supplemented with 50 μg/mL kanamycin at 37°C until the optical density at 600 nm 163 reached 0.6-0.8. Recombinant protein expression was induced via the addition of 0.5 164 mM isopropyl β-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG), followed by an incubation at 37°C for 165 3-4 h. Cells were harvested by centrifugation and resuspended in a lysis buffer (20 mM 166 Sodium Phosphate, pH 7.4, 500 mM NaCl, 10 mM imidazole) containing 0.5 mg/mL 167 lysozyme and an EDTA-free Protease Inhibitor Cocktail. Lysis was achieved by 168 sonication on ice, and soluble proteins were recovered from the supernatant after 169 centrifugation. The DARPins were isolated using nickel-nitrilotriacetic acid (Ni-NTA) 170 affinity chromatography; the column was washed with buffer containing 20 mM 171 imidazole, and the target proteins were eluted with 500 mM imidazole. Eluted fractions 172 were dialyzed against 1x PBS (pH 7.4) and passed through a 0.22 μm filter. 173

DISCUSSION 626 Mechanistic studies of ovarian biology remain challenging due to the lack of 627 highly selective, tunable, and physiologically relevant tools. Existing approaches, 628 including genetic animal models, small molecules, and antibodies, each possess 629 limitations [32, 63, 64]. Using EGFR as a proof-of-concept target in combination with our 630

29 IVFG-based ex vivo ovulation system, our study establishes DARPin as an efficient, 631 versatile, and tunable platform for studying ovarian signaling. We demonstrate that anti-632 EGFR DARPins, particularly the bispecific SX-006 that incorporates a central leucine 633 zipper motif adapted from E69-LZ3-E01 to drive homodimerization, selectively inhibit 634 follicle ovulation without overt cytotoxicity. Moreover, extracellular EGFR blockade 635 produced transcriptional and functional effects distinct from intracellular EGFR kinase 636 small molecule inhibitor, suggesting that extracellular ligand–receptor perturbation and 637 intracellular kinase inhibition represent biologically distinct modes of EGFR modulation 638 during ovulation. Together, these findings support the utility of DARPins not only as 639 experimental tools for dissecting ovarian biology but also as a potential therapeutic 640 modality for ovarian disorders, infertility, and non-hormonal contraception. 641 642 EGFR has been well established as a central mediator of LH/hCG-induced 643 ovulation. Prior studies using genetically modified mouse models and small-molecule 644 EGFR inhibitors demonstrated that EGFR activation is required for cumulus expansion, 645 oocyte maturation, and follicle rupture [19-21]. However, these studies primarily disrupt 646 receptor expression or intracellular kinase activity and do not specifically interrogate 647 extracellular ligand–receptor interactions. Here, we used anti-EGFR DARPins to 648 selectively target the extracellular ligand-binding interface of EGFR. SX-006 potently 649 inhibited follicle rupture and suppressed ovulation-associated gene expression, 650 supporting the concept that extracellular EGFR activation is required for proper 651 ovulatory signaling.
