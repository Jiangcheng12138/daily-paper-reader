## 摘要
绝经不仅影响生育能力，还影响全身健康。然而，这种关联背后的机制仍不十分明确，部分原因是缺乏具有全面分子和表型特征的、稳健且与年龄相关的临床前模型。为此，我们系统地比较了三种候选小鼠绝经模型：(1) 自然衰老；(2) 在多个年龄段使用 4-乙烯基环己烯二环氧化物 (VCD) 进行化学诱导的卵泡耗竭；(3) Foxl2 单倍剂量不足，这是一种基于与人类卵巢早衰相关基因的遗传模型。通过组织学、血清激素分析、单细胞转录组学和机器学习方法，我们识别了卵泡丢失、内分泌紊乱和转录重塑的共有及模型特异性特征。我们的比较框架为选择特定背景下的临床前啮齿动物模型提供了依据，以研究绝经及其对卵巢衰老更广泛生理后果的影响。简述：这项对小鼠绝经模型的系统表征为未来关于绝经全身性影响的临床前研究奠定了基础。

## Abstract
Menopause affects not only fertility but also systemic health. Yet mechanisms underlying this coupling are still poorly understood, partly due to the absence of robust, age-relevant preclinical models with comprehensive molecular and phenotypic characterization. To address this, we systematically compared three candidate mouse models of menopause: (1) intact aging, (2) chemical ovarian follicle depletion using 4-vinylcyclohexene diepoxide (VCD) administered at multiple ages, and (3) Foxl2 haploinsufficiency, a genetic model based on a gene linked to human premature ovarian failure. Through histology, serum hormone profiling, single-cell transcriptomics and machine-learning approaches, we identify both shared and model-specific features of follicle loss, endocrine disruption, and transcriptional remodeling. Our comparative framework enables informed selection of context-appropriate preclinical rodent models to study menopause and the broader physiological consequences of ovarian aging.
