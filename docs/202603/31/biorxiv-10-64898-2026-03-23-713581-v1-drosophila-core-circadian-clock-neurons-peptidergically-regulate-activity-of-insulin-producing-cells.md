## 摘要
中枢节律点神经元结合利用外部刺激和神经肽信号来同步分子振荡，从而产生昼夜节律行为。虽然时钟网络结构以及这些节律点神经元群之间的信号传导已得到充分描述，但这些节律点如何与特定的大脑输出区域进行通信仍不清楚。在本研究中，我们确定了果蝇中的“核心”时钟神经元——腹侧外侧神经元（LNvs）如何向原下丘脑区域，即间部（PI）发送信号。此前人们认为 LNvs 仅间接地与 PI 通信，而我们提供的证据表明，LNvs 以时间依赖的方式功能性地调节 PI 的胰岛素产生细胞（IPCs）。这种功能连接依赖于两种经典时钟神经肽的神经肽能信号传导：色素扩散因子（PDF）和短神经肽 F（sNPF）。连接组学分析未发现从时钟神经元到 IPCs 的任何直接突触输入。分泌 PDF 和 sNPF 的小型腹侧外侧时钟神经元距离 IPCs 约 15-20 微米，这表明在果蝇背侧原脑中，跨越这些距离的体积传输（volume transmission）可能是可行的。通过对 IPCs 进行功能成像并结合神经肽应用，我们深入了解了这两种神经肽如何通过其受体协同作用向 IPCs 发送信号。我们的研究结果表明，LNvs 可以通过体积传输直接向 IPCs 发送信号，并与 IPCs 形成间接的多突触回路，这可能为昼夜节律钟肽如何与其他时钟输出区域通信提供了更广泛的模型。

作者总结：从果蝇到人类，动物大脑中的昼夜节律钟使动物能够预测每日的环境变化并在时间上协调内部过程。大脑中的中枢钟充当主节律点，设定所有其他组织中昼夜节律钟的步调。果蝇一直是发现昼夜节律遗传和细胞基础的关键模型系统，相关研究曾获 2017 年诺贝尔生理学或医学奖。果蝇和哺乳动物的大脑钟利用经典的突触通信和神经调节肽在构成大脑钟的神经元之间传递信号。我们的研究探讨了在时钟神经元之间传递信号的神经肽是否也从时钟神经元向非时钟神经元传递信号。我们发现果蝇标志性的时钟神经肽——色素扩散因子，在大脑钟之外向大脑的胰岛素产生细胞发送信号。时钟神经元与胰岛素产生细胞之间不存在突触连接，相反，信号传导是通过肽信号跨越数十微米的扩散实现的，即体积传输。我们的发现表明，神经肽体积传输可能不仅是钟内信号传导的普遍特征，也是大脑钟向非时钟神经元输出信号的普遍特征。

## Abstract
Central pacemaker neurons use a combination of external stimuli and neuropeptide signaling to synchronize molecular oscillations leading to circadian behaviors. The clock network structure and signaling between these pacemaker neuron groups have been well described, but how these pacemakers communicate with specific brain output regions remains poorly understood. Here, we identified how "core" clock neurons in Drosophila, the ventrolateral neurons (LNvs), signal to the proto-hypothalamic region, the pars intercerebralis (PI). Previously thought to communicate with the PI only indirectly, we provide evidence to show that LNvs functionally modulate insulin-producing cells (IPCs) of the PI in a time-of-day-dependent manner. This functional connectivity relies on neuropeptidergic signaling of two classical clock neuropeptides: pigment dispersing factor (PDF) and short Neuropeptide F (sNPF). Connectomic analysis does not identify any direct synaptic inputs from clock neurons to IPCs. Small ventrolateral clock neurons, which secrete both PDF and sNPF are 15-20 m away from IPCs, suggesting that volume transmission across these distances may be possible in the fly dorsal protocerebrum. Peptide application with functional imaging of IPCs provides insight into how these two neuropeptides may act synergistically via their receptors to signal to IPCs. Our findings indicate that LNvs can signal directly to IPCs by volume transmission and also form indirect multisynaptic circuits with IPCs, which may model more broadly how circadian clock peptides communicate with other clock output regions.

Author SummaryCircadian clocks in the brains of animals from flies to humans allow animals to anticipate daily environmental changes and temporally coordinate internal processes. The central clock in the brain serves as a master pacemaker that sets the pace of circadian clocks in all other tissues. Fruit flies have been an essential model system for discovering the genetic and cellular underpinnings of circadian rhythms, with work in flies being awarded the 2017 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine. Brain clocks in flies and mammals use classical synaptic communication and neuromodulatory peptides to signal between the neurons that make up the brain clock. Our study asks whether the neuropeptides that signal between clock neurons also signal from clock to non-clock neurons. We found that the signature fly clock neuropeptide pigment dispersing factor signals outside the brain clock to insulin producing cells of the brain. There are no synaptic connections from clock neurons to insulin producing cells, instead signaling occurs by diffusion of peptide signals across tens of microns, termed volume transmission. Our findings point to the possibility that neuropeptide volume transmission may be a general feature not only of intra-clock signaling but also of brain clock output signaling to non-clock neurons.
